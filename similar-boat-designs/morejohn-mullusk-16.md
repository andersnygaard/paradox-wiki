# "MULLUSK 16"

**"MULLUSK 16"** is a small-boat **design concept** that Chris Morejohn posted on his [Hogfish Design blog](/references/hogfish-design-website.md) on 15 May 2017 — described in his own words as his version of a [Paradox](/paradox.md), "a bit longer" than [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md)'s original.[1] It is the companion sketch to his earlier ["16′ minimal cruiser"](/similar-boat-designs/morejohn-16-minimal-cruiser.md), and, like it, belongs with Morejohn's small Paradox-kin concepts rather than with his large liveaboard sharpies (see ["Hogfish Maximus"](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md)).

## The design

Morejohn frames "MULLUSK 16" as his idea of a small boat built around **Matt Layden's roller-furling lug sail** — the [lug-type rig](/components/rig/index.md) that is one of Paradox's signature features — carried over onto a hull of his own.[1] The stated features are:[1]

- **The Layden roller-furling lug rig**, credited directly to Layden's design.
- **A lowerable mast**, able to be dropped at sea or at any time.
- **A ballasted keel** for stability.
- **Deck seating** — the boat is meant to let the sailor sit outside on deck in good weather, rather than staying inside a fully enclosed cabin as on Paradox.
- A hull **"a bit longer"** than Paradox; no beam, draft, displacement, or sail-area figures are given.[1]

Where the companion [16′ minimal cruiser](/similar-boat-designs/morejohn-16-minimal-cruiser.md) reaches for offshore capability with a ballasted daggerboard and a mizzen for heaving-to, "MULLUSK 16" is the simpler of the two sketches — a single lug rig, a ballasted keel, and an emphasis on comfort on deck in fair weather.

## Connection to the Paradox circle

The concept comes out of long first-hand contact with Layden's boats. Morejohn recounts meeting Matt Layden about **26 years** before writing — roughly **1991** — in the **Exumas**, where he sailed Layden's Paradox, and where members of the same small-boat circle sailed one another's boats.[1] For Morejohn's biography, his larger designs, and the well-known photograph of him at the helm of a Paradox on Exuma Sound, see [Chris Morejohn](/people/chris-morejohn.md); for the cruising ground itself, see [the Bahamas and the Exumas](/merits/bahamas-and-exumas.md).
