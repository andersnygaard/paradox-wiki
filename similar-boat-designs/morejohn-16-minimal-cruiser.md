# Chris Morejohn's 16′ minimal cruiser

Chris Morejohn's **"16′ minimal cruiser"** is a small-boat **design concept** he posted on his [Hogfish Design blog](/references/hogfish-design-website.md) on 23 January 2016 — his attempt at "the smallest cruiser that I can come up with to work well at sea."[1] It is a from-scratch design inspired by, but distinct from, Matt Layden's [Paradox](/paradox.md): Morejohn had sailed with [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md) aboard his Paradox designs and rates them highly, and set out here to carry that thinking into a slightly different small cruiser of his own.[1] It is treated here alongside its later companion sketch, ["MULLUSK 16"](/similar-boat-designs/morejohn-mullusk-16.md), rather than with Morejohn's much larger liveaboard sharpies (see ["Hogfish Maximus"](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md)).

## The design and how it departs from Paradox

The concept is a **16 ft** cruiser built around a short list of features Morejohn wanted for capable sea work in the smallest possible hull:[1]

- **A ballasted daggerboard** for added stability — where Paradox relies on internal ballast and its [chine runners](/chine-runners/index.md) for lateral resistance, this design puts ballast in a retractable board.
- **An exterior cockpit**, so the sailor can live outside the cabin rather than only within Paradox's fully enclosed shell.
- **A lowerable mast**, able to be dropped at sea if needed.
- **A main-plus-mizzen (ketch/yawl-type) rig** — the added mizzen is there specifically for **heaving-to while lying to a sea anchor** in a blow judged too strong to sail in, a storm tactic the single-sail Paradox does not offer.

No dimensions beyond the 16 ft length — no beam, draft, displacement, ballast weight, or sail area — are given in the post.[1] The design was presented as one of a short series of minimal-cruiser sketches on the blog, with a larger version following it.[1]

## Connection to the Paradox circle

The design grows directly out of Morejohn's first-hand experience of Layden's boats. He points to **"Zoey"** — the Paradox built by professional boatbuilder **Glenn Maxwell** (recorded elsewhere in this archive as ["Zoë"](/boats/zoe.md)) — as an example of a Paradox that "really works," and frames his own minimal cruiser as a variation on that proven idea rather than a replacement for it.[1] For Morejohn's own biography and his place in the wider small-boat circle around Layden and the [Bolducs](/people/bolduc/dave.md), see [Chris Morejohn](/people/chris-morejohn.md).
