# Vent baffle

The baffle inside the Paradox's ventilation trunk keeps spray and green water out of the cabin while still admitting air.

## The transom baffle: sharing one opening between the tiller and the air

A single baffle, fitted to the inside of the transom around the tiller opening, does two jobs at once: it stops water entering the hull through that opening, and it acts as a ventilator, letting air pass the same way.[1] The tiller itself passes through this baffle on its way to the steering line loop inside the cabin.[2] The plans call for the baffle to be cut from the same 6 mm sheet of plywood used for the cabin roof and the hatch cover.[3]

## A rubber or vinyl baffle for genuinely rough water

The built-in baffle is judged adequate for moderate conditions, but not necessarily for the worst of them: if the boat were ever laid ahull in a gale, with the rudder raised clear of the water, "the built-in baffle may not be adequate for keeping water out," and the tiller port would need a waterproof gasket sealing it instead.[4] The recommended precaution, made before going to sea rather than after a problem shows up, is to fit an added rubber or vinyl baffle over the tiller port for rough-water sailing.[5]
