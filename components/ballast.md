# Ballast

The lead and water ballast that gives the Paradox its stability and self-righting range, carried low in the bilge so the boat stays stiff despite its small size and enclosed cabin.

## Cemented lead replacing pumped water ballast, on a second-hand boat

The stock design carries part of its ballast as water, pumped into and out of tanks to bring the boat to her waterline. On at least one secondhand hull, that water ballast had been replaced by a previous owner with loose lead cast permanently into the side ballast bays and set in cement: inspection found the work done "very good," the lead unable to shift "even in the very unlikely event of a total capsize" — with the side benefit that the boat no longer needs water pumped in and out for ballast at all, as the original design requires.[1] Before that inspection was made, the same change had already been described secondhand as "some of the lead ballast has been supplemented with concrete ballast," with the hope — later confirmed — that it had been done well enough to bring the boat down to her designed waterline.[2]

## Loose ballast castings: weights and placement

Where ballast is carried as loose castings rather than fixed pigs, weights on record are uneven rather than uniform: one boat's removable trim ballast comprised two concrete castings of matched size but different weight, one 8.5 kg and the other, probably containing lead, 17.0 kg. The lighter casting went under the floorboards at the forward end of the cabin; the heavier one was kept loose for trimming, likely to end up in the forecastle, further forward than the battery.[3]

## Bilge lockers as ballast stowage, replacing built-in water ballast

Where a boat lacks the stock design's built-in water ballast under the cabin floor, bilge lockers do the same job with removable weight instead: tinned foods, gas cartridges, and bottled drinking water, stowed low in lockers to port and starboard, plus two anchors with their chains and warps stowed under the floor.[4]

## Water ballast carried in bottles, as an alternative to built-in tanks

At least one Paradox dispensed with built-in water-ballast tanks altogether in favor of water ballast carried in bottles under the floor — plausibly filled with fresh water that could be drunk and replaced with sea water if fresh water could not be replenished, doubling as both ballast and a water supply.[5] See [water tanks](/components/water-tanks.md) for the stock tank arrangement this alternative departs from.
