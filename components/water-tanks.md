# Water tanks

The Paradox's built-in tanks serve as both water ballast and a drinking-water supply, and can be lined with several different materials.

## Fitting the tank frames level with the chine logs

The floor supports and the water-tank frames are fitted into the bottom of the boat together, checked with a straight edge for level and for being in line with the chine logs transversely, before bonding — since a good bond between the frames and the boat's bottom is essential to the structure.[1] Each of the boat's two tanks is fitted with its own breather tube, made from small-gauge copper pipe.[2]

## Water ballast carried in bottles, as an alternative to built-in tanks

At least one Paradox dispensed with built-in tanks altogether, carrying her water ballast instead in bottles stowed under the floor — plausibly filled with fresh water that could be drunk and replaced with sea water if fresh water could not be replenished, doubling as both ballast and a water supply. See [ballast — water ballast carried in bottles](/components/ballast.md#water-ballast-carried-in-bottles-as-an-alternative-to-built-in-tanks) for the fuller citation.[3]
