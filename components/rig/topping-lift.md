# Topping lift

The topping lift supports the boom, and is part of the Paradox's rolling-boom furling arrangement.

## What it does

The topping lift supports the boom so it can rotate freely as sail is shortened or increased; without one, the sail tends to foul the side deck or the coachroof as the boom swings.[1] It can also take the weight of the furled sail secured over to one side, keeping the hatchway clear — useful when mooring to port, since the furled sail is normally stowed along the port side deck where it would otherwise be in the way.[1]

## Rope choice for the hauling part

A Polypropylene line is a poor choice for the topping lift's hauling part: it kept slipping out of its jam cleat. Replacing just the hauling part with Terylene rope, while keeping the fixed part (tied off at the apex) in the original Polypropylene, solved the problem.[2]

## Technique: topping up before hoisting

Before hoisting sail, the furled sail should be "topped up" on the topping lift first; while hoisting, care is needed that the yard does not catch under the topping-lift line.[3]
