# GJAC

**GJAC** (also spelled "GJac") is one of [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md)'s **older boats** — a small cruising design in the same family as the [Paradox](/paradox.md), [Little Cruiser](little-cruiser.md), and [Swamp Thing](swamp-thing.md), but a distinct, earlier design rather than a Paradox hull. What is recorded of her comes from two sources: a rudder photograph and a for-sale listing [1][2], with [microcruising.com](/references/microcruising-website.md) placing her in the design lineage [3][4].

## Origin and place in the lineage

GJac was Matt's **next boat after [Swamp Thing](swamp-thing.md)** — a slight **enlargement** of that successful design (**14' 10"**, close to the 14.5 ft in the later resale listing), keeping Swamp Thing's best ideas: the [chine runners](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md) and the **boom-furled lug sail** [3][4]. Matt cruised her for **about a year** (c. 1987) — long enough that he had "learned as much as he could" from her — before moving on in 1988 to build [Little Cruiser](little-cruiser.md), which had more comfortable accommodations [3][4]. After that GJac passed to other owners.

Importantly, **the leeboards seen in photographs of GJac were added by a later owner** — GJac as Matt designed and built her used **chine runners, not leeboards** [3]. This independently confirms the resale-listing detail below (that a later owner fitted the leeboards for light daysailing) and reinforces chine runners as the family's characteristic answer to lateral resistance.

## What is known about the boat

- **Dimensions: 14.5 ft x 4 ft x 9 in** — close to Paradox's own footprint (Paradox is 13' 10" per the [Little Cruiser](little-cruiser.md) comparison), but not identical [2].

- **No cabin top**, unlike Paradox — but she still provides **sleeping accommodation for one person below** [2].

- Has **[chine runners](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md)**, the characteristic Layden-family answer to lateral resistance (see the leeboards note above) [2][3].

- Owner (as of the 2002 resale): based in **Connecticut** [2].

The earliest photographic trace of GJAC is a **half-immersed rudder photograph** — one of four different Layden-boat rudders uploaded, and the only photo of GJAC then available; see [rudder variety across Layden boats](/design/rudder.md#rudder-variety-across-layden-boats) [1].

## Leeboards versus ballast

A later owner fitted GJAC with **leeboards** for sailing her "light," daysailer-style — a departure from Matt's original chine-runner-only design, not a correction of it. The alternative view: **removing the leeboards and adding ballast, "lots of it,"** would let the boat's chine runners alone provide enough lateral resistance to go to windward [2]. Together with the [chine-runner windward-performance](/chine-runners/windward-performance.md) trial on Paradox itself, this reinforces chine runners (not leeboards or a centerboard) as the family's characteristic answer to lateral resistance across more than one Layden design.

## Resale offering

GJAC came up for sale in June 2002 with cruising gear included — **anchor, chain, lines, stove, fenders** — and a **galvanized trailer with new wheels**; contact was `DGMusante@...` (860-448-3615), "all offers will be considered," with a photo at `http://home.triad.rr.com/lcruise/other1.htm` [2]. The market side of this listing is also catalogued under [secondhand market — a second listing: "GJAC" for sale](/operations/secondhand-market.md).
