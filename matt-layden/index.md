# Matt Layden

**Matt Layden** — the designer behind the [Paradox](/paradox.md) — and his
family of small shoal-draft cruising boat designs. This top-level branch holds
his biography and his individual designs. Specific built Paradox hulls live
under [boats](/boats/index.md); the Paradox design itself is the bundle
[trunk](/paradox.md).

# Designs

His boats in build order (Terrapin → Swamp Thing → GJAC → Little Cruiser →
the Balboa 20 interlude → Paradox → Enigma → Sand Flea → Elusion); the
Paradox itself is the bundle trunk.

* [Paradox](/paradox.md) - His most-documented design; the microcruiser at the trunk of this bundle (see also the [original Paradox](/boats/original-paradox.md) hull).
* [Terrapin](terrapin.md) - Matt Layden's first home-built microcruiser, a 16-foot leeboard cat boat (c. 1985) whose severe knockdown off the coast of Maine motivated the chine-runner, self-righting hull form of every later Layden design.
* [Swamp Thing](swamp-thing.md) - Matt Layden's engineless 13-foot micro-cruiser of 1985 — his first design to use chine runners, rated by the Bolducs as the best-sailing of all his boats and restored by them after a Gulf Stream rollover and years of neglect.
* [GJAC](gjac.md) - One of Matt Layden's older small cruising designs — a Paradox-like but cabin-top-less 14.5 ft boat that enlarges Swamp Thing, carries chine runners, and sleeps one below.
* [Little Cruiser](little-cruiser.md) - A Matt Layden cruising boat very similar to the Paradox, sailed two-up by Dave and Mindy Bolduc and often cited as a real-world proof point for Paradox-type capability, with a larger and more complex lug sail than Paradox's and no formal plans on record.
* [Enigma](enigma.md) - Matt Layden's sixth micro-cruiser, an 11'10" cartoppable stitch-and-glue plywood sharpie that pairs Little Cruiser's hull shape with Paradox's furling rig and chine runners; discussed on the paradoxbuilders list during her 2005 construction under the working name "Mini Paradox," and later sold to Dave and Mindy Bolduc.
* [Sand Flea](sand-flea.md) - An 8-foot cruising dinghy Matt Layden built after Enigma and raced near the top of two Watertribe Everglades Challenges and a 2012 Ultra Marathon.
* [Elusion](elusion.md) - Matt Layden's newest micro-cruiser — a 9-foot, roughly 120-lb design closely related to Enigma but light enough to cartop, using a stand-up paddle auxiliary instead of a sculling oar, and a class winner in the 2010 Watertribe Ultimate Florida Challenge.
