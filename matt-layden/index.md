# Matt Layden

**Matt Layden** — the designer behind the [Paradox](/paradox.md) — and his
family of small shoal-draft cruising boat designs. This top-level branch holds
his biography and his individual designs. Specific built Paradox hulls live
under [boats](/boats/index.md); the Paradox design itself is the bundle
[trunk](/paradox.md).

# Biography

* [Matt Layden (biography)](biography.md) - The designer of the Paradox microcruiser and other small shoal-draft cruising boats; sailed full-time ~10 years across five of his own micro-cruisers (Terrapin→Paradox), the full design lineage from a 1985 Maine knockdown to the later Watertribe boats now documented via Dave Bolduc's microcruising.com; reportedly lived aboard his own Paradox two-to-three years, Nova Scotia to the lower Bahamas (secondhand); a November 2002 "Room for 2" post from Dave Bolduc gave a firsthand two/three-up Bahamas cruising account and confirmed Matt's then-girlfriend Karen sailed aboard Paradox herself; a March 2003 eyewitness account from Glen Maxwell, who sailed alongside Matt at Ft. Desoto just before the Everglades Challenge, found Matt's windward sailing ability "far superior," described Matt's solo haul-out roller rig in detail, and relayed the race's early progress; Matt went on to win the ~300-mile race outright, finishing at Key Largo on 11 March 2003 with nobody else having yet reached the second-to-last checkpoint — confirmed contemporaneously by the archive's own "MATT WINS!" results post, which also recorded that Matt stopped mid-race to rescue and tow in an overturned kayaker; the win prompted Larry Whited to call it "a new paradigm" for Watertribe adventure racing and Nels to suggest Matt had "created a class of his own"; a month later, Dave Bolduc visited Matt and got to scull Paradox himself, finding her fitted with a new, straighter, wider-bladed sculling oar (bronze-rod-reinforced tips on both oar and rudder) that had made even Matt's own wrist hurt during the race; a second in-person meeting followed at the 2003 Cedar Key Messabout (3-4 May), where Glen Maxwell asked Matt directly how he furls without a topping lift, learning Matt's technique is simply foot pressure on the furling line and halyard, freeing both hands; days later, on 8 May 2003, Matt posted to the paradoxbuilders list himself for the first time — the only message from his own hand recorded in this archive — revealing he had been quietly lurking for a month or two and sharing a full sailmaking recipe for a new, more weatherly sail he had cut for the original Paradox that February; later that month, David Beard credited Matt's own paraphrased view — that motors are fine as a luxury but shouldn't be depended on to get a sailor out of trouble — for a deliberate motor-free yuloh outing. See also [Glen C. Maxwell's Matt Layden eyewitness record](/people/glen-maxwell/matt-layden-eyewitness.md) for the consolidated, firsthand account of Glen's own in-person encounters with Matt (Ft. Desoto, the Cedar Key Messabout, and welcoming Matt's own first list post), and the [Bolducs' Matt Layden firsthand record](/people/bolduc/matt-layden-authority.md) for the archive's longest and closest firsthand relationship with the designer — Dave and Mindy having known Matt since 1992, owned three of his boats, and cruised the Bahamas in his company.

# Designs

His boats in build order (Terrapin → Swamp Thing → GJAC → Little Cruiser →
the Balboa 20 interlude → Paradox → Enigma → Sand Flea → Elusion); the
Paradox itself is the bundle trunk.

* [Paradox](/paradox.md) - His most-documented design; the microcruiser at the trunk of this bundle (see also the [original Paradox](/boats/original-paradox.md) hull).
* [Terrapin](terrapin.md) - Matt Layden's first home-built microcruiser, a 16-foot leeboard cat boat (c. 1985) whose severe knockdown off the coast of Maine motivated the chine-runner, self-righting hull form of every later Layden design.
* [Swamp Thing](swamp-thing.md) - Matt Layden's engineless 13-foot micro-cruiser of 1985 — his first design to use chine runners, rated by the Bolducs as the best-sailing of all his boats and restored by them after a Gulf Stream rollover and years of neglect.
* [GJAC](gjac.md) - One of Matt Layden's older small cruising designs — a Paradox-like but cabin-top-less 14.5 ft boat that enlarges Swamp Thing, carries chine runners, and sleeps one below.
* [Little Cruiser](little-cruiser.md) - A Matt Layden cruising boat very similar to the Paradox, sailed two-up by Dave and Mindy Bolduc and often cited as a real-world proof point for Paradox-type capability, with a larger and more complex lug sail than Paradox's and no formal plans on record.
* [Enigma](enigma.md) - Matt Layden's sixth micro-cruiser, an 11'10" cartoppable stitch-and-glue plywood sharpie that pairs Little Cruiser's hull shape with Paradox's furling rig and chine runners, later sold to Dave and Mindy Bolduc.
* [Sand Flea](sand-flea.md) - An 8-foot cruising dinghy Matt Layden built after Enigma and raced near the top of two Watertribe Everglades Challenges and a 2012 Ultra Marathon.
* [Elusion](elusion.md) - Matt Layden's newest micro-cruiser — a 9-foot, roughly 120-lb design closely related to Enigma but light enough to cartop, using a stand-up paddle auxiliary instead of a sculling oar, and a class winner in the 2010 Watertribe Ultimate Florida Challenge.
* [Mini Paradox](mini-paradox.md) - An experimental, narrower Paradox variant Matt Layden built and sailed with his wife Karen, keeping the chine runners but trying a shorter, wider, higher-aspect-ratio sail.
