# Elusion

**Elusion** is [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md)'s newest micro-cruiser — the most recent boat in the documented Layden build order, following [Terrapin](/matt-layden/terrapin.md), [Swamp Thing](/matt-layden/swamp-thing.md), [GJAC](/matt-layden/gjac.md), [Little Cruiser](/matt-layden/little-cruiser.md), the [Paradox](/paradox.md), [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md), and [Sand Flea](/matt-layden/sand-flea.md). She is not a Paradox hull but shares much of Enigma's construction and design — itself a boat that blends [Little Cruiser](/matt-layden/little-cruiser.md)'s hull shape with [Paradox](/paradox.md)'s furling rig and [chine runners](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md).

## A lighter, smaller Enigma

Elusion is similar to Enigma in construction and design, but smaller and lighter still — about **120 lbs**, light enough to be realistically transported on the roof of a vehicle, making her even easier to cartop than Enigma.

Sail area is not given. The ~360 lb displacement and the ~120 lb "roof-transportable" weight most likely represent laden/design displacement versus a lighter unladen hull weight, following the same empty-vs-laden pattern documented for [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md).

Interesting details, matching the same set used for Paradox and Enigma: **chine runners**; **stand-up paddle auxiliary**; **self righting**; **positive buoyancy**; **all lines lead into cabin**.

## An unusual auxiliary: a stand-up paddle

Where the rest of the Layden lineage relies on a **sculling oar** or yuloh for auxiliary propulsion (see [yuloh and stowed-rig retention](/components/yuloh.md)), Elusion is the exception: her auxiliary is not the usual sculling oar but instead a **stand-up paddle**.

## Racing history

In **2010**, Matt entered Elusion in the **1,200-mile Watertribe Ultimate Florida Challenge** — the demanding circumnavigation race around Florida in which Enigma had won her class back in 2006 — and **placed first in his class** once again, at one point during the race leading Class 4 (Small Sailboats).[6]

## Paradox, Enigma, and Elusion compared: three sisters, three ballasting strategies

Paradox, Enigma, and Elusion are described as **"sisters"** — Paradox the eldest of the three designs, Elusion the youngest and by far the smallest, at **9 ft by 38 in** against Paradox's 13 ft 10 in.[6] The three share a family resemblance but ballast themselves three different ways: **Paradox** carries fixed lead plus water ballast; **Enigma** carries bagged sand plus cruising gear; **Elusion** carries cruising gear, water, and crew weight, with no fixed lead at all.[6] Like Paradox and Enigma, Elusion is **epoxy-and-GRP-sheathed plywood** construction — a builder comparing all three lug-rigged Layden designs against an unsheathed roof-rack cruiser found this the one construction feature all three shared.[7]

The three designs are also read as one continuous expression of a single design philosophy, carried furthest in the smallest and newest boat: Matt is understood to have "concentrated on being super-efficient at reducing friction" rather than relying on waterline length for speed, so his boats carry **no standing rigging and few external halyards, downhauls, and the like**, and he avoids fitting a topping lift at all.[6]

Set against Paradox specifically, in a comparative appraisal by an owner who had sailed one of Matt's designs for years, Elusion is judged **"a little easier to manage"**: she is transportable on the roof of a car, and her removable wheels slot into the sides of the hull for portaging — though a road trailer would still be preferred where practical. The same appraisal judges Elusion **"not as foolproof when at sea as 'Paradox', on account of being more dependent on the ability of the crew for keeping her upright when the going gets tough."**[10]

## Not to be confused with "Illusion"

A separately built, similarly named hull — "Illusion," owned and later offered for sale by another builder, missing Elusion's cabin top — is "very similar" to Elusion but is not a Matt Layden design; it is a homage built by an admirer, not a sister boat.[8] Elusion's real family resemblance to Paradox has been described directly, in Matt's own boat-family terms, as a "baby sister Paradox."[9]
