# Sand Flea

**Sand Flea** is an **8-foot cruising dinghy** built by [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md), the seventh boat in his documented build order — after [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md) and before [Elusion](/matt-layden/elusion.md), his two other post-hiatus micro-cruiser designs. She is not a Paradox hull.

## Design particulars

An illustrated survey gives the fullest design account on record: Sand Flea is **8 ft by 3 ft 9 in**, a **double-chined pram dinghy** with a deck, entered in the **2007/8 WaterTribe Everglades Challenge**.[3]

| Particular | Value |
|---|---|
| LOA | 8 ft |
| Beam | 3 ft 9 in |
| Empty weight | 85 lb |
| Sail area | 45 sq ft |

Notable details:[3]

- A **single leeboard on her starboard side**, in place of the [chine runners](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md) used on most of Matt's other designs.
- A **sliding hatch** that can completely cover the rectangular cockpit, making the boat lockable and possibly watertight; a later photo shows her fitted with a **dodger** in place of the sliding hatch.
- A **furling, high-peaked lugsail**, 45 sq ft, on a mast, yard, and boom each about **8 ft** long, stowable on the port side deck "without extending beyond the bow and stern."
- Like [Paradox](/paradox.md), a **raisable rudder** with the tiller passing through a sealed port in the transom.
- Secondary propulsion is a **pair of oars**, stowed on the starboard side deck.
- Like [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md), a pair of **attachable wheels for portaging**.

## Race record

Matt built Sand Flea after Enigma and — remarkably for an 8-foot boat — raced her competitively:

- Entered in **two 300-mile Watertribe Everglades Challenges**, in **2007** and **2008**.
- Entered in the **2012 Ultra Marathon**.
- In each of these races she finished **"near the top,"** consistent with the strong race results recorded for Matt's other small boats — see [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md) (1st in class, 2006 Watertribe Ultimate Challenge) and [Elusion](/matt-layden/elusion.md) (1st in class, 2010 Watertribe Ultimate Challenge).

In the 2007 Everglades Challenge specifically, racing under the WaterTribe name "Wizard," Matt sailed Sand Flea solo, paused mid-race to rescue a capsized kayaker, and still finished less than half a day behind a crewed boat.[2]

Set alongside Matt's other designs, Sand Flea reads as "similar, but shorter and wider" than Paradox — "an audacious little cruiser — one that stretches the imagination, not the budget" — and in Matt's hands she sailed **hundreds of miles on the shallow waters of the Everglades**.[4]
