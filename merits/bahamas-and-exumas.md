# The Bolducs' Bahamas cruising, and the 2013 engine-free crossing

David and Mindy Bolduc's cruising log documents decades of Bahamas cruising, capped by a 2013 solo crossing sailed entirely without an outboard.

## The Bolducs: decades of Bahamas cruising, and a 2013 engine-free solo crossing

David and Mindy Bolduc's cruising log — kept mostly aboard "Little Cruiser," a sister design to Paradox — documents a **1993** first Bahamas trip (Bimini–Chub Cay–Cat Cay); a **1994** joint Bahamas cruise with [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md) sailing the [original Paradox](/boats/original-paradox.md) (Bimini–Chub–Nassau–Exumas–Long Island–Conception–Rum Cay–Spanish Wells–Eleuthera), during which the party met a family living aboard the 32-foot sharpie "Hogfish Lips" (see [Chris Morejohn](/people/chris-morejohn.md)); further Bahamas cruises in **1995** (an Abaco circumnavigation), **1996**, **1998**, and **2001** (the 2001 trip departing Key Largo, crossing the Gulf Stream, and taking in the Exumas, Cat Island, Georgetown, and Andros); a **2003** circumnavigation of Andros; and a **2006** three-week cruise of North Carolina's Outer Banks. Each Bahamas cruise averaged roughly 800 miles sailed over about 2.5 months. The last of these, in **2013**, was sailed solo in "Enigma" without an outboard at all — "a voyage for the purist with sail and oar only," and on the site's own account "the smallest sailboat to have ever cruised the islands" — the log-keeper's seventh trip to the islands overall (six of the prior trips had been two-up). The 2013 passage's engine-free technique is covered at [operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md](/operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md#a-fully-engine-free-solo-passage-2013); the same log's record of the community's [Cedar Key](/merits/cedar-key.md#the-bolducs-cruising-log-cedar-key-attendance-across-the-years) attendance is covered on that page.[1][2][3][4]

## The crossing itself, and the Southern California comparison

The Gulf Stream crossing to the Bahamas that opens these cruises is covered in full, as a technique and a benchmark, at [operations/gulf-stream-crossing.md](/operations/gulf-stream-crossing.md) — weather doctrine, ship-traffic risk, and real recorded crossings. That page's own account of the crossing is cited elsewhere as tougher than a Southern California-to-Catalina crossing; see [operations/southern-california-and-catalina-crossing.md](/operations/southern-california-and-catalina-crossing.md) for that comparison and for the wider Southern California Paradox-building record.
