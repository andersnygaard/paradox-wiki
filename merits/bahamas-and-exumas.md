# The Bolducs' Bahamas cruising, and the 2013 engine-free crossing

David and Mindy Bolduc's cruising log documents decades of Bahamas cruising, capped by a 2013 solo crossing sailed entirely without an outboard.

## The Bolducs: decades of Bahamas cruising, and a 2013 engine-free solo crossing

David and Mindy Bolduc's cruising log — kept mostly aboard "Little Cruiser," a sister design to Paradox — documents a joint 1994 Bahamas cruise with Matt Layden sailing the original Paradox, and a further string of Bahamas trips through 2013 averaging roughly 800 miles and 2.5 months each: a 1995 Abaco circumnavigation, a 2003 Andros circumnavigation, and a 2006 three-week Outer Banks cruise. The last of these, in 2013, was sailed solo in "Enigma" without an outboard at all — "a voyage for the purist with sail and oar only," and on the site's own account "the smallest sailboat to have ever cruised the islands." Full log at [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#the-bolducs-cruising-log-and-buddy-boating-with-matt-layden-microcruisingcom); the 2013 passage's engine-free technique is covered at [operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md](/operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md#a-fully-engine-free-solo-passage-2013).[1][2][3]

## The crossing itself, and the Southern California comparison

The Gulf Stream crossing to the Bahamas that opens these cruises is covered in full, as a technique and a benchmark, at [operations/gulf-stream-crossing.md](/operations/gulf-stream-crossing.md) — weather doctrine, ship-traffic risk, and real recorded crossings.
