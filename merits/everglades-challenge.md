# The Everglades Challenge, and the wider Layden racing record

A Paradox hull won the WaterTribe Everglades Challenge outright in 2003, and Matt Layden went on to race three further, lighter WaterTribe-tested designs of his own in the years that followed.

## The 2003 Everglades Challenge: Matt Layden's outright win

Matt Layden entered the original Paradox in the 2003 Everglades Challenge and finished first overall — ahead of the entire paddled field of kayaks and canoes, racing solo on sail and yuloh power alone, sleeping while underway rather than making camp, and pausing mid-race to rescue a capsized kayaker. The result was widely read at the time as proof that a heavy, engine-free cruising monohull could out-race light paddled and multihull competition. Full checkpoint-by-checkpoint record, the official results table, and the discussion the win prompted are at [boats/original-paradox.md](/boats/original-paradox.md#the-finish-checkpoint-4-and-the-win).[1][2][3]

## Beyond Paradox: the wider Layden racing record

Matt Layden went on to design three lighter, cartoppable micro-cruisers after Paradox, and raced each of them in WaterTribe events the same way he had field-tested Paradox. They are not Paradox hulls, and their own design and build history lives under [matt-layden/](/matt-layden/index.md); this is a results summary only, given here as context for the racing tradition the 2003 win began.

- **Enigma** (built 2005) won her class and placed third overall in the 1,200-mile 2006 Ultimate Florida Challenge, breaking Matt Layden's own 2003 solo record along the way. Sold in 2008, her new owners completed three further WaterTribe races in her — a **2009 NC Challenge class win** (sculled roughly 11 of the miles), a **2010 Everglades Challenge class win**, whose hardest test was sculling through Gasparilla Pass at night against a foul tide and 5-foot breaking seas, and a **2010 NC Challenge** in which she was sculled 14 miles/7 hours on the first day alone. Full racing record at [matt-layden/enigma.md](/matt-layden/enigma.md#racing-history-and-change-of-ownership); the post-2008 seasons are also cited as technique evidence at [operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md](/operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md#watertribe-evidence-a-micro-cruiser-can-be-cruised-motor-free-under-race-pressure) — see that page for what the results demonstrate about engine-free propulsion, not repeated here.[4][5][6][7]
- **Sand Flea** (an 8-foot cruising dinghy, built after Enigma) raced two 300-mile Everglades Challenges (2007, 2008) and the 2012 Ultra Marathon, "finishing near the top as usual" each time; in 2007 Matt Layden sailed her solo, paused to rescue a capsized kayaker, and still finished less than half a day behind a crewed boat. Full record at [matt-layden/sand-flea.md](/matt-layden/sand-flea.md#race-record).[8][9]
- **Elusion** (2010) won her class in the 1,200-mile Ultimate Florida Challenge, at one point leading Class 4 (Small Sailboats) outright. Full record at [matt-layden/elusion.md](/matt-layden/elusion.md#racing-history).[10]
- Matt Layden also raced non-Layden-design **kayaks and canoes** in WaterTribe events in other years — a second-place finish in a 10-foot inflatable kayak (2003 Michigan Challenge), and class wins in a self-built decked canoe (2005, 2009). Summarized at [matt-layden/biography.md](/matt-layden/biography.md#the-watertribe-years-enigma-sand-flea-and-elusion).[11][12]
