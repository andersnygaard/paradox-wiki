# Notable voyages

Long passages and extended cruises made under Paradox-family sail, as distinct from the [WaterTribe races](watertribe-and-races.md) and the [gatherings](gatherings-and-rallies.md) elsewhere in this branch. Each entry below is a summary; the full account lives on the linked boat or operations page.

## Little Jim: the Portland passage and the Poole–Cowes Solent crossing (2003)

Alastair Law's "Little Jim" made two landmark UK passages in her first season. In May 2003, a 27-mile-each-way, four-day round trip from Poole Harbour to Portland Harbour and back took her through St Albans Head's tidal race, where green water broke steadily over the boat without a drop reaching the cabin. In September the same year she made "the fastest ever crossing from Poole to Cowes in a Paradox" — and, since no Paradox had made the crossing before, also the only one — 28.4 nm in 7 hours 20 minutes, surviving an 80–90 degree knockdown from a rogue wave off Hurst Narrows and coming up sailing as if nothing had happened. Full accounts at [boats/little-jim.md](/boats/little-jim.md#the-landmark-voyage-a-27-mile-passage-to-portland-and-back-may-2003) and [boats/little-jim.md](/boats/little-jim.md#a-first-crossing-of-the-solent-poole-to-cowes-september-2003).[1][2]

## Faith: a ~500-mile River Crouch-to-Scilly Isles cruise, in company with Little Jim (2008)

Bill Serjeant's "Faith" made a nearly two-month, roughly 800-mile cruise from the River Crouch to the Scilly Isles and back to Plymouth, sailed initially solo and, for the Scillonian leg, in company with "Little Jim" — the pair crossing to Tresco, riding out a severe gale at anchor in New Grimsby, and towing each other's boats between Fowey and Plymouth once the outboard proved the more reliable engine of the two. The cruise's final, abridged part was published in *Messing About In Boats* as "With Great Britain's Dinghy Cruisers: Faith's Cruise," estimating the whole passage at some 500 sea miles. Full account at [boats/faith.md](/boats/faith.md#to-the-scilly-isles-and-back-with-little-jim); the magazine coverage is described at [references/messing-about-in-boats.md](/references/messing-about-in-boats.md#a-british-paradox-in-a-dca-cruise-account-july-2009).[3][4]

## The Bolducs: decades of Bahamas cruising, and a 2013 engine-free solo crossing

David and Mindy Bolduc's cruising log — kept mostly aboard "Little Cruiser," a sister design to Paradox — documents a joint 1994 Bahamas cruise with Matt Layden sailing the original Paradox, and a further string of Bahamas trips through 2013 averaging roughly 800 miles and 2.5 months each: a 1995 Abaco circumnavigation, a 2003 Andros circumnavigation, and a 2006 three-week Outer Banks cruise. The last of these, in 2013, was sailed solo in "Enigma" without an outboard at all — "a voyage for the purist with sail and oar only," and on the site's own account "the smallest sailboat to have ever cruised the islands." Full log at [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#the-bolducs-cruising-log-and-buddy-boating-with-matt-layden-microcruisingcom); the 2013 passage's engine-free technique is covered at [operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md](/operations/sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md#a-fully-engine-free-solo-passage-2013).[5][6][7]

## Small Fry: rounding Cape Leeuwin, and ultra-shoal cruising of Shark Bay and the Abrolhos

Geoff Davis's Western Australian "Small Fry" rounded **Cape Leeuwin** — where the Indian and Southern Oceans meet — and continued several days offshore without putting in, then cruised the ultra-shoal waters of **Shark Bay**, the **Abrolhos Islands**, and **Jurien Bay**, drying out overnight as a matter of routine rather than incident. Full account at [boats/small-fry.md](/boats/small-fry.md).[8][9]

## Planktos: a junk-rigged shakedown cruise to Shark Bay

Roselt Croeser's "Planktos" — the archive's only completed, launched, and cruised junk-rig Paradox conversion — made her shakedown cruise from Perth to **Shark Bay**, roughly 800 km up the Western Australian coast, reported to a boatbuilders' association meeting alongside three further passages "contemplated" but not yet attempted: Perth to Shark Bay again, the Abrolhos and back, and Cape Leeuwin and back. Full account at [boats/planktos.md](/boats/planktos.md#shark-bay-and-what-was-contemplated-after).[10]
