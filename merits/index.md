# Merits

**Merits** collects what a Paradox has actually done, as distinct from [operations](/operations/index.md), which collects how she is sailed, handled, and maintained. Operations is technique: rudder position under a sea anchor, sculling stroke, provisioning for a winter cruise. Merits is the record: races entered and won, passages made, gatherings attended, and the press coverage the design has drawn along the way. A page that uses a race result as evidence for a technique — the yuloh page citing WaterTribe finishes as proof that engine-free propulsion works under real pressure — stays in operations; a page that exists to record that the race happened, and what a boat did in it, belongs here.

Nothing on these pages is told in full for the first time. Every entry below is a one-to-three-sentence summary pointing to the boat, designer, or operations page where the fuller account already lives — read the linked page for the story; read this branch for the standing overview and the way in.

# Press and recognition

Beyond its sailors' own accounts, the Paradox and its designer have drawn continuous, independently edited press coverage since 1991. [Messing About In Boats](/references/messing-about-in-boats.md) is the design's original print venue — Matt Layden's earlier micro-cruisers appear in its pages years before Paradox existed, the design itself debuted there in July 1995, Don Elliott's sixteen-part "Building Paradox" construction series ran 1996–97, and sea-trial reports, sightings, and reader builds continued into the 2010s. *Small Craft Advisor* profiled the design and its designer more than once — a 2003 Cedar Key gathering feature, a 2007 Enigma interview, and a 2009 Matt Layden profile — cited throughout [matt-layden/enigma.md](/matt-layden/enigma.md), [matt-layden/biography.md](/matt-layden/biography.md), and [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md). The Dinghy Cruising Association's own *Bulletin* 193 carried two independent appraisals of the UK-built "Little Jim" side by side — [an owner's fifteen-advantages/eleven-disadvantages list](/references/dca-sailing-the-paradox.md) and, uniquely in print, [a declared sceptic's own sail trial](/references/dca-paradox-gilchrist.md) of the design. [Duckworks Magazine](/references/duckworks-magazine.md) reprinted a builder's tip and a cruise account, and separately carried two original build/launch articles for a second UK Paradox.

None of that coverage is re-described here — see each reference page above for what the source is and what it says; this section only notes that the recognition happened, and where to read about it.
