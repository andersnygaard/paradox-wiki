# Gatherings and rallies

Recurring small-boat meets, club rallies, and organized events Paradoxes have attended, as distinct from the point-to-point [notable voyages](notable-voyages.md) and [WaterTribe races](watertribe-and-races.md) covered elsewhere in this branch. The fullest single source for the American gatherings below is [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md), which stays in `operations/` because its trip reports also ground out crew-size and cold-weather handling knowledge cited elsewhere; this page cross-links its specific headings rather than re-telling them.

## Cedar Key: the annual small-boat messabout (2003–2019)

The **Cedar Key** small-boat gathering, held the first weekend of May off Florida's Gulf coast, is the community's longest-running meeting point. Two Paradoxes, "Zoë" and "Whisper," attended together as early as 2003, drawing a *Small Craft Advisor* magazine feature that praised both boats; Matt Layden himself attended repeatedly through the 2000s and gave a dinner talk on his design lineage in 2019, with author Ida Little in the audience. Full year-by-year attendance record at [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#a-second-gathering-point-the-cedar-key-small-boat-meet) and [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#the-bolducs-cruising-log-and-buddy-boating-with-matt-layden-microcruisingcom); the magazine coverage is at [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#press-coverage-the-2003-small-craft-advisor-cedar-key-article).[1][2][3]

## Apalachicola Bay: a proposed meeting ground, scouted solo (2002)

**Apalachicola Bay**, on Florida's St George Sound, was an early proposed second gathering point; rather than wait for a scheduled group date, one owner made a solo scouting trip there in February 2002 — the first record of a Paradox owner actually acting on a proposed group cruise. Full account at [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#apalachicola-bay-as-a-proposed-meeting-ground).[4]

## The Iowa messabout: a third, unconnected gathering point (proposed 2002)

A general small-boat-builder gathering, not Paradox-specific, was proposed for **Lake Okoboji, northwest Iowa**, for the summer of 2003 — a third, geographically distinct small-boat gathering point alongside Cedar Key and Apalachicola Bay, with no recorded connection to either. Full account at [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#a-third-gathering-point-the-iowa-messabout).[5]

## A DCA week at Cobnor, Chichester Harbour (August 2007)

Two Paradoxes, "Faith" and a second boat, sailed and anchored together for a week at a **Dinghy Cruising Association** meeting at Cobnor, Chichester Harbour, alongside a fleet of DCA dinghies — matching the dinghy fleet's performance on a reach and doing marginally better to windward, and riding out a near-gale at anchor on good holding ground. Full account at [operations/group-cruise-reports.md](/operations/group-cruise-reports.md#a-dca-week-at-cobnor-chichester-harbour-august-2007); "Faith"'s own day-by-day log of the same week, including a man-overboard incident, is at [boats/faith.md](/boats/faith.md#the-cobnor-week-a-dinghy-cruising-association-meeting-and-falling-overboard).[6][7]

## The Texas 200, and a Port Townsend Wooden Boat Show showing

The **Texas 200** is a long-distance small-boat coastal raid along the Texas coast, used by owners as evidence of a boat's real-world cruising ability. Chris Curtis's Idaho-based Paradox, built by Lezlie Hensen, completed the Texas 200 and has been shown several times at the **Port Townsend Wooden Boat Show** — "a great boat in almost every way." Full record at [boats/curtis-paradox.md](/boats/curtis-paradox.md).[8]

Sean Mulligan's Arizona-based Paradox "Scout" — built in Texas by the same Lezlie Henson, under the working name "Tardis" — ran the Texas 200 in 2014, towing a fellow competitor's boat, "Crippled Duck," through 20–25 kt wind along the way. Full record at [boats/scout.md](/boats/scout.md#heavy-wind-sailing-and-the-2014-texas-200).[9]
