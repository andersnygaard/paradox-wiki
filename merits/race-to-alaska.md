# A Paradox in the Race to Alaska (R2AK 2026)

The **Race to Alaska (R2AK)** is a roughly 750-mile, entirely **engine-free** small-craft race from **Port Townsend, Washington** to **Ketchikan, Alaska**, run up the exposed inside waters of the British Columbia and southeast Alaska coast — a hard test of any boat driven only by sail and human power. Its 2026 fleet included, for the first time on record, a **[Paradox](/paradox.md)**: **Team Forget Me Knot**, listed as the **smallest boat in the race at thirteen feet**, among a field otherwise made up of much larger trimarans, monohulls, and catamarans up to thirty-five feet.[1]

The entry is a natural fit for the design's premise — a fully enclosed micro-cruiser propelled by its lug rig and a [yuloh](/components/yuloh.md), needing no motor by the rules or by habit — and a notable outing for it, pitting a boat built for shoal Everglades and coastal cruising against open, cold, tidal northern water. The archive holds only the **entry**, captured from the race's April 2026 fleet statistics; it does not record how Team Forget Me Knot fared, the mirror closing before the outcome was reported. That a Paradox was on the start list at all extends the design's real-world résumé beyond the [Texas 200](/merits/texas-200.md) and [Everglades Challenge](/merits/everglades-challenge.md) to one of the toughest engine-free races in North America.
