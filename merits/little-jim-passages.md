# Little Jim's Portland passage and Solent crossing

Alastair Law's "Little Jim" made two landmark UK passages in her first season, setting an early benchmark for Paradox open-water performance.

## Little Jim: the Portland passage and the Poole–Cowes Solent crossing (2003)

Alastair Law's "Little Jim" made two landmark UK passages in her first season. In May 2003, a 27-mile-each-way, four-day round trip from Poole Harbour to Portland Harbour and back took her through St Albans Head's tidal race, where green water broke steadily over the boat without a drop reaching the cabin. In September the same year she made "the fastest ever crossing from Poole to Cowes in a Paradox" — and, since no Paradox had made the crossing before, also the only one — 28.4 nm in 7 hours 20 minutes, surviving an 80–90 degree knockdown from a rogue wave off Hurst Narrows and coming up sailing as if nothing had happened.
