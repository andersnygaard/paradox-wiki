# Dave Skaife's voyage to Cuba

In 2017 Dave Skaife sailed his fourteen-foot, motorless Paradox to Cuba and cruised its north coast, returning to Florida across the Gulf Stream — the archive's only record of a Paradox making an international passage to Cuba.

## A motorless Paradox to Cuba, and a Gulf-Stream return (2017)

After cruising the southern Bahamas — George Town, the Jumentos, and Ragged Island — [Dave Skaife](/people/dave-skaife.md) made a **17-hour passage to Cuba**, arriving safely at Puerto de Vita on the north coast; the passage was, in his words, "pretty easy," while checking in proved "one big snafu after another." He cruised the north coast west to Cayo Guillermo (Marina Guillermo) and inland to Morón, documenting the Cuban check-in procedure, how the money worked, reprovisioning, and the near-total absence of internet — a rare firsthand cruising account of the country in a boat this small.

Rather than continue the hard, permit-bound coastal slog toward Havana — which he chose to "save for a land based tour" — he turned north for Florida. Taking a light-weather shortcut, he sailed straight up the western edge of the Great Bahama Bank, **anchored some 40 miles from the nearest land in fifteen feet of water** to sleep, and then crossed the Gulf Stream to Caesar Creek, south of Miami, on a passage he expected to take about 25 hours. It nearly did — but the wind died completely just as he entered the strongest part of the Stream, setting him **10 hours adrift at 2.5–3 knots**, still engine-free; he worked every puff perpendicular to the current to claw out of it, finally making Caesar Creek hard on the wind on a dark, moonless night. Full account at [people/dave-skaife.md](/people/dave-skaife.md#four-seasons-under-sail-and-oar); the Gulf Stream crossing as a general technique and benchmark is at [operations/gulf-stream-crossing.md](/operations/gulf-stream-crossing.md), and the source blog at [references/dave-skaife-blog.md](/references/dave-skaife-blog.md).[1][2]
