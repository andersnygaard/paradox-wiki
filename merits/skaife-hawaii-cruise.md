# Dave Skaife's Hawaiian island cruise

Dave Skaife's first season with his newly built, motorless Paradox was a roughly 415-nautical-mile inter-island cruise of the main Hawaiian islands in 2014.

## A ~415-nm engine-free inter-island cruise, Hilo to Kauai (2014)

Having built the boat in Hawaii, [Dave Skaife](/people/dave-skaife.md) spent the summer of 2014 sailing it up the main Hawaiian chain — from Hilo on the Big Island by way of Lanai, Molokai, and Oahu to Kauai — visiting, on his own account, every spot he had set out to reach "and then some." By the chart he plotted afterward the whole cruise came to only about **415 nautical miles**, less than it had felt, sailed and sculled **without an engine**; his longest unbroken stretches aboard the fourteen-foot boat were two separate four-day passages, on which his biggest problem was boredom with the food. He called the trip "full circle" not because he sailed a geographic loop but because, about a week after the boat was sent off from Kauai, it was "safely back in my garage" — the season closing where it began. It was the boat's shakedown as a cruiser and the season that gave Dave the confidence, as he put it, to start reviewing "what worked and what didn't." Full account and the season's gear reviews at [people/dave-skaife.md](/people/dave-skaife.md#four-seasons-under-sail-and-oar); the source blog is described at [references/dave-skaife-blog.md](/references/dave-skaife-blog.md).[1][2]
