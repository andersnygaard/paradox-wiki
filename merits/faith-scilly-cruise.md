## Faith: a ~500-mile River Crouch-to-Scilly Isles cruise, in company with Little Jim (2008)

Bill Serjeant's "Faith" made a nearly two-month, roughly 800-mile cruise from the River Crouch to the Scilly Isles and back to Plymouth, sailed initially solo and, for the Scillonian leg, in company with "Little Jim" — the pair crossing to Tresco, riding out a severe gale at anchor in New Grimsby, and towing each other's boats between Fowey and Plymouth once the outboard proved the more reliable engine of the two. The cruise's final, abridged part was published in *Messing About In Boats* as "With Great Britain's Dinghy Cruisers: Faith's Cruise," estimating the whole passage at some 500 sea miles. Full account at [boats/faith.md](/boats/faith.md#to-the-scilly-isles-and-back-with-little-jim); the magazine coverage is described at [references/messing-about-in-boats.md](/references/messing-about-in-boats.md#a-british-paradox-in-a-dca-cruise-account-july-2009).[1][2]
