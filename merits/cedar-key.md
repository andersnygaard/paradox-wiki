# Cedar Key, the annual small-boat messabout

Cedar Key, held the first weekend of May off Florida's Gulf coast, is the Paradox community's longest-running meeting point.

## Origins: the annual gathering

The annual **Cedar Key** small-boat meet in Florida is the group's most established gathering point. Cedar Key hosts "an ecclectic event" every year on the first Saturday and Sunday of May, drawing many home-made and other small boats and "many free spirits." A longtime attendee — sailing a Seapearl 21 trimaran and a Seapearl 28, and builder of a Bolger Micro, a Nymph, and a CLC Cape Charles 18 kayak — introduced the event to the Paradox group, calling it "a lovely place and a lot of fun"; one Paradox owner started his own sailing career in a Little Cruiser at Cedar Key.[1][2]

Thanks for the invitation prompted a reply that one owner had meant to attend that same year with "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)" but had forgotten until seeing the event covered in *Messing About In Boats*, and would try to attend the following year; that reply also set in motion a plan to compare "[Whisper](/boats/whisper.md)" and "Zoë" side by side under sail — the first record of a proposed direct comparison between two named, sailing Paradox hulls, distinct from the individual hull-speed/GPS data points recorded on the [hull speed](/operations/hull-speed.md) page. Cedar Key lies a two-hour drive from Zoë's home waters; Zoë had already sailed from Cedar Key to Clearwater for her sea trials (see [Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)).[3]

Cedar Key (first weekend of May) and the ad hoc [Apalachicola Bay meetup](/merits/gatherings.md#apalachicola-bay-a-proposed-meeting-ground-scouted-solo-2002) are the community's only two recorded data points, prior to 2003, on Paradox owners meeting up with each other or the wider small-boat community in person, as distinct from the [Texas 200 and Port Townsend Wooden Boat Show](/merits/texas-200.md#the-texas-200-and-a-port-townsend-wooden-boat-show-showing), which are individual-boat cruising/show achievements rather than group meetups.

## The 2003 Cedar Key messabout: dates and logistics

The **2003 Cedar Key messabout** was held the first weekend of May — the **3rd and 4th** — with "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)" made available at the event for anyone wanting to "try one on for size and test sail," and most of the activity centered on **Atsena Otie Key**. An archival website (`members.ij.net/wctss/wctss/index.htm`) carries archival Cedar Key photos; the gathering is "a wonderful place to meet like minded people," with priceless natural habitat.[4][5][6][7][8]

Trip-planning detail for the event: the regional forecast for that time of year is "the best time of the year," with little rain, highs in the 80s, and lows in the upper 60s. Cedar-Key-specific conditions run "usually great sailing," with morning wind up to 20 kt dying to nearly calm by 2 pm and sometimes returning in the evening, depending on the prevailing weather. That year had no fishing tournament competing for space, lodging options included the **Gulfside Motel** and the Island Place next door (with others scattered at other hotels or the RV camp outside town), and most boats are left on the **Island Place beach** at low tide, with some sailors anchored out by Atsena Otie.[9][10][11]

This is the first confirmed, dated commitment to a specific Cedar Key messabout, as opposed to the general "hope to attend next year" sentiment recorded the previous July — and the record, together with [the Bolducs' cruising log](#the-bolducs-cruising-log-cedar-key-attendance-across-the-years) below, confirms the gathering did go ahead substantially as planned, with Zoë and Whisper both among the boats present.

## Glen Maxwell attends, and meets Matt Layden in person

The messabout went ahead as planned on **3-4 May 2003**, with a firsthand, contemporaneous confirmation that "we all had a great time."

**Matt Layden** was present in person at the gathering — a second recorded face-to-face meeting between him and a Paradox owner, after sailing together at Ft. Desoto just before the 2003 Everglades Challenge — yielding a technical detail on his furling method: to furl without a topping lift, Matt presses his **foot** on the furling line while hoisting and on the halyard while furling, freeing both hands. See [Matt Layden — meeting at the 2003 Cedar Key Messabout](/matt-layden/biography.md) and [furling system](/components/rig/furling.md) for the fuller record of this exchange.[12]

## Independent photographic confirmation: West Coast Trailer Sailers

A further, independent piece of photographic evidence for the 2003 Cedar Key gathering came from the **West Coast Trailer Sailers**, a small-boat club distinct from the Cedar Key event's own organizers: the club posted photos on their website (`members.ij.net/wctss/wctss/photo.htm`, the same archival site already pointed to for Cedar Key photos above), showing **two Paradoxes** present at the recent Cedar Key get-together, "indeed great" pictures with "several of Zoe looking great," including side-by-side shots of "[Whisper](/boats/whisper.md)" and "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)" together and photos of Zoë under sail, "especially enticing."[13][14][15]

This is the earliest photographic confirmation, from a source outside the mailing list itself, that both "Zoë" and (per [the Bolducs' own cruising log](#the-bolducs-cruising-log-cedar-key-attendance-across-the-years)) "Whisper" attended the 2003 Cedar Key Messabout side by side — corroborating, from three independent angles now (the group's own contemporaneous planning thread, the Bolducs' cruising log, and this outside club's photo page), that the gathering came off much as planned. The same West Coast Trailer Sailers photos also prompted the Bolducs — freshly back from a Bahamas cruise — to visit Glen Maxwell in person, the archive's only recorded face-to-face meeting between two of its most prominent correspondents, at which Little Cruiser and "Zoë" were seen firsthand by each other's owners for the first time (see [Dave Bolduc — stories and community notes](/people/bolduc/dave.md#stories-and-community-notes)).[13]

## Press coverage: the 2003 Small Craft Advisor Cedar Key article

The 2003 Cedar Key gathering also reached print. *Small Craft Advisor*'s Sept/Oct 2003 issue carried a "Cedar Key Small Boat Meet" feature, which devoted substantial space to the two Paradoxes present — "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)" and "[Whisper](/boats/whisper.md)" — praising both and noting at one point that "Zoë appeared to be sailing to windward higher and faster than my Sea Pearl with its leeboard down." The article also recorded seeing designer [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md), described as "the reclusive designer," at the gathering, and ran four photographs of "Zoë" and "Whisper" alongside other boats present. (The same event's earlier photographic record came via a similarly named website, `members.ij.net/wctss` — see [independent photographic confirmation](#independent-photographic-confirmation-west-coast-trailer-sailers) above; the archive does not confirm whether this is the same source as the article's author.) The same issue carried an unrelated installment on the Krugers' "Two Continent Canoe Expedition."[16]

## The 2006 Cedar Key gathering, and notable past attendees

Asked in January 2006 whether a Cedar Key get-together would happen again that year, the answer confirmed the pattern holds: the gathering always falls on the first full weekend of May — in 2006, **6-7 May**. By then the event had grown into "a big affair with a lot of stars of the small boat building community": the previous year's gathering had drawn Matt Layden, sailing his new, lighter Paradox, alongside other well-known small-boat figures including the makers of WEST System epoxy, among many others. One owner was by then a regular attendee with "Zoë." Motels around Cedar Key fill to capacity that weekend, making early booking necessary.[17][18]

Another owner planned to attend in 2006 after several years away, having spent the intervening seasons sailing the Mississippi and Alabama Gulf Coast instead; group gatherings there had been abandoned after Hurricane Katrina, the region judged "just too depressing."[19]

The pattern held again for 2007: the gathering was confirmed for the first weekend of May, with both "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)" and "[Whisper](/boats/whisper.md)" expected to attend — a prospective South Alabama builder hoping to see a finished Paradox in person was told he was welcome to visit near Live Oak, Florida (just southeast of Live Oak, on I-10 about 70 miles east of Tallahassee) ahead of the gathering, or to see both boats together there.[20] Days off falling only on Fridays and Saturdays, plus the demands of a preaching schedule that make a Sunday trip difficult, meant a visit could not be planned before May at the earliest.[21]

The 2007 gathering duly took place, drawing both "Zoë" and "Whisper" as expected; photographs went up on another forum and the Bolducs' own website first, with the group's own Photos section, under "Cedar Key 2007," to follow once a camera was downloaded.[22] A separate request for a link to the gathering's photographs was answered with a pointer to the same West Coast Trailer Sailers photo page already used for earlier years' Cedar Key coverage (see [independent photographic confirmation](#independent-photographic-confirmation-west-coast-trailer-sailers) above).[23][24]

Ahead of the 2008 gathering, an invitation went out to the two other regular Cedar Key attendees to bring their own boats again, with the hope of cruising together for a while once there.[25] One had to decline: a spouse's health had by then worsened to needing near-constant care, compounded by a daughter with two young children of her own to look after, leaving room at most for a single day's visit (see [Glen C. Maxwell — build and "Zoë" — continued vane refinement, and a family health setback](/people/glen-maxwell/build-and-zoe.md#continued-vane-refinement-and-a-family-health-setback) for the fuller record). The other confirmed he would still bring "[Whisper](/boats/whisper.md)" to the gathering as planned, so at least one Paradox would be there.[29][30]

## The Bolducs' cruising log: Cedar Key attendance across the years

A decades-long cruising log kept on microcruising.com corroborates the gathering's recurrence across many more years than the 2003 and 2006-2008 correspondence above covers on its own: attendance is recorded in **2003**, **2006**, **2007** ("nine sharpies" were present that year), **2008** (sailing Enigma), and **2019**, when Matt Layden gave a dinner talk on his design lineage with author [Ida Little](/references/beachcruising-book.md) present in the audience. Cross-link [gulf stream crossing](/operations/gulf-stream-crossing.md) and [micro cruising group](/references/micro-cruising-group.md) for the wider cruising log this attendance record is drawn from — see [the Bolducs' Bahamas cruising](/merits/bahamas-and-exumas.md) for the same log's voyage record.[26][27][28]
