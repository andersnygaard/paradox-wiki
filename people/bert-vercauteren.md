# Bert Vercauteren

**Bert Vercauteren** is a boat designer in Belgium and an early member (from July 2001) of the `paradoxbuilders` list. He bought the [Paradox plans](/references/building-plans.md), not to build a Paradox but as design inspiration for a bigger, two-person micro cruiser of his own — expecting it to be quite different from the Paradox but "the same in spirit," and joking it might be called "PARODAX."

## Contributions

- **Rig mechanics.** Early questions about the [Paradox rig](/components/rig/index.md) — what holds the yard to the mast when there is no parrel and only the halyard appears to, and how the sail is fastened to the rolling boom — helped surface how the balanced-lug rig actually works.
- **A furling fix proposed and disproved.** In the "Furling system" thread (see [furling system](/components/rig/furling.md)), Vercauteren proposed running the downhaul ("tack strop") to a cleat like the reef line, so reefing could be done by alternately easing the tack strop and rolling in the reef line while easing the halyard, letting the sail's own luff and leech act as its topping lift. Field testing disproved the idea: releasing the tack strop leaves the furling-drum line with nothing to work against. How Matt Layden furls his sail without a topping lift remained an open question afterward.
- **Independent stability-calculation confirmation, and a safety worry.** Modeling a Paradox in his own "Nautilus" boat-design software, Vercauteren confirmed Matt Layden's stability calculation checks out at 100% given a Vertical Center of Gravity as low as believed (~120 mm above DWL) — see [self-righting and capsize recovery — angle of vanishing stability](/design/self-righting-and-capsize-recovery.md#angle-of-vanishing-stability-160-and-165-degrees-from-independent-sources). Drawing on a traditional European boatbuilding education, an unresolved safety worry was also raised that the Paradox's flat bottom and side panels might invite the full force of a breaking wave in a way a round hull like the one in [BRIS](/boats/bris-paradox.md) does not — see [blue-water suitability — a designer's worry: do flat panels invite the "brute hammer" of a breaking wave?](/design/blue-water-suitability.md#flat-panels-and-the-brute-hammer-of-a-breaking-wave).
- **On design choices for a round-hulled voyaging design.** A revised translation of book chapter 9 on the subject was judged one of the strongest, most convincing texts written about Paradox, and the question was raised of why the chine-runner/heavy-displacement Paradox concept was not used on that project's own latest iteration — see [Sven Yrvind's book — a revised translation, and an open question about Sven Yrvind's own design choices](/references/yrvind-book.md#a-revised-translation-and-an-open-question-about-sven-yrvinds-own-design-choices).
- **Photo-access bug.** Corroborated a May 2004 Yahoo Groups error blocking access to the photo section, part of the storage-limit problem later resolved by the list's founder — see [the mailing list — the photo-storage limit, and the founding of paradoxbuilders2](/references/mailing-list.md#the-photo-storage-limit-and-the-founding-of-paradoxbuilders2).
