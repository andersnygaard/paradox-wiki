# Bert Vercauteren

**Bert Vercauteren** is a boat designer in Belgium and an early member
(from July 2001) of the `paradoxbuilders` list. He bought the
[Paradox plans](/references/building-plans.md) from Dave Bolduc not to build a
Paradox but as design inspiration for a bigger, two-person micro cruiser of his
own — expecting it to be quite different from the Paradox but "the same in
spirit," and joking he might call it "PARODAX."

## Contributions

- **Rig mechanics.** Early questions about the [Paradox rig](/design/rig.md) —
  what holds the yard to the mast when there is no parrel and only the halyard
  appears to, and how the sail is fastened to the rolling boom — helped
  surface how the balanced-lug rig actually works.
- **A furling fix proposed and disproved.** In the "Furling system" thread
  (see [furling system](/design/furling-system.md)), Vercauteren proposed
  running the downhaul ("tack strop") to a cleat like the reef line, so
  reefing could be done by alternately easing the tack strop and rolling in
  the reef line while easing the halyard, letting the sail's own luff and
  leech act as its topping lift. Field testing disproved the idea: releasing
  the tack strop leaves the furling-drum line with nothing to work against.
  How Matt Layden furls his sail without a topping lift remained an open
  question afterward.
- **Virus warning.** He flagged a suspicious offlist e-mail purportedly
  replying to the furling thread, from a sender identified only as "Ken La
  Rose" — content-free, carrying two empty attachments that triggered virus
  warnings, with no working reply address.
- **Independent stability-calculation confirmation, and a safety worry.**
  Modeling a Paradox in his own "Nautilus" boat-design software, Vercauteren
  confirmed Matt Layden's stability calculation checks out at 100% given a
  Vertical Center of Gravity as low as believed (~120 mm above DWL) — see
  [weight, ballast, and displacement — independent software confirmation of
  Matt Layden's stability
  calculation](/design/weight-and-ballast.md#independent-software-confirmation-of-matt-laydens-stability-calculation).
  Drawing on his traditional European boatbuilding education, he also raised
  an unresolved safety worry that the Paradox's flat bottom and side panels
  might invite the full force of a breaking wave in a way a round hull like
  [Sven Yrvind](/boats/bris-paradox.md)'s does not — see
  [blue-water suitability — a designer's worry: do flat panels invite the
  "brute hammer" of a breaking
  wave?](/design/blue-water-suitability.md#flat-panels-and-the-brute-hammer-of-a-breaking-wave).
- **On Sven Yrvind's design choices.** He judged Kjell Karlsson's revised
  translation of Yrvind's book chapter 9 one of the strongest, most
  convincing texts written about Paradox, and raised the question of why
  Yrvind did not use the chine-runner/heavy-displacement Paradox concept on
  his own latest project — see [the "BRIS Paradox" — a revised translation,
  and an open question about Sven Yrvind's own design
  choices](/boats/bris-paradox.md#a-revised-translation-and-an-open-question-about-sven-yrvinds-own-design-choices).
- **Photo-access bug.** Corroborated a May 2004 Yahoo Groups error blocking
  access to the photo section, part of the storage-limit problem later
  resolved by the list's founder — see [the mailing list — the photo-storage
  limit, and the founding of
  paradoxbuilders2](/references/mailing-list.md#the-photo-storage-limit-and-the-founding-of-paradoxbuilders2).
