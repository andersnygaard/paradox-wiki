# People

The designer and notable builders/contributors in the [Paradox](/paradox.md)
community. Concepts are added here as they are sourced from the archive.

> The **designer, Matt Layden**, has his own top-level branch at
> [/matt-layden/index.md](/matt-layden/index.md) (his biography and his other
> designs). Individual built Paradox hulls live under
> [/boats/index.md](/boats/index.md).

# Concepts

* [Glen C. Maxwell](glen-maxwell.md) - Professional boat builder who built the Paradox "Zoë" and is a valuable firsthand eyewitness of Matt Layden under sail — the one builder to sail his own Paradox alongside Matt's.
* [The Bolducs — David & Mindy Bolduc](bolduc.md) - North Carolina micro-cruisers and the archive's deepest firsthand authorities on Matt Layden, whom Dave met in 1992 — see [bolduc.md](bolduc.md) for their boats, list role, and Matt Layden firsthand record.
* [Sven Yrvind](yrvind.md) - Swedish designer and ocean voyager celebrated for extremely small blue-water boats (the "Bris" series), who never posted to the list but is one of its most-mentioned outside figures — the benchmark against which Paradox's offshore capability is weighed, and the chine runner's most authoritative external champion, a personal friend of Matt Layden who sailed Paradox himself. This hub page indexes two sub-pages under `yrvind/`: [voyages and boats](yrvind/voyages-and-boats.md) (his ocean-voyaging career, his own boats, and his flex-composite/maximum-safety design philosophy) and [Paradox and the chine runner](yrvind/paradox-and-the-chine-runner.md) (his 1997 month sailing Paradox, his "deflector, not hydrofoil" theory, and his "greatest innovation of the century" verdict).
* [Don Elliott](don-elliott.md) - Early Paradox builder and author of the paid "Building the Paradox" construction articles, based in Tomah, Wisconsin, who advocated strict fidelity to Matt Layden's plans and resigned from the list over the foam-flotation dispute.
* [Alastair Law (Yeovil, England)](alastair-law.md) - UK builder of the Paradox "Little Jim," who built the boat single-handed in a garage barely longer than the hull and became one of the archive's most detailed cruising and construction correspondents.
* [Chuck Leinweber](chuck-leinweber.md) - Publisher of Duckworks Magazine and a boat builder active on the early paradoxbuilders list.
* [Chris Becker](chris-becker.md) - Australian (Brisbane, Queensland) Paradox builder who built his boat "Walden" from scratch, and an early canvasser of the community's who-has-a-Paradox roster.
* [Paul (New Zealand)](paul-nz.md) - A prospective builder in the south of New Zealand drawn to the Paradox for enclosed, simple-rig sailing in rough weather.
* [Bert Vercauteren](bert-vercauteren.md) - A Belgian boat designer who bought the Paradox plans as inspiration for a larger two-person micro cruiser, proposed a furling-system fix that field-testing later disproved, and independently confirmed Matt Layden's stability calculation using his own boat-design software.
* [Chris Morejohn](chris-morejohn.md) - A Florida Keys sharpie builder and liveaboard cruiser in the Layden/Bolduc circle, recorded at the helm of a Paradox making 6 kts in 25 kts on Exuma Sound.
* [Jeff UK ("roverticket")](jeff-doryman.md) - UK boatbuilder and prolific list contributor — posting first as doryman_uk, then as apcf14, both under the same Yahoo profile handle roverticket — who abandoned an early from-scratch 16 ft redesign, "Paramour," then years later built and painted his own enlarged Paradox variant, "NOLI" (originally "PARANIGMA"), toward a 2008 launch.
* [David Beard](david-beard.md) - A first-time Paradox builder from Tupelo, MS, who bought and refitted a secondhand Paradox — naming her "Whisper" — while also building his own Paradox from scratch, and became one of the list's most prolific and enthusiastic contributors.
* [Charles Wilson](charles-wilson.md) - UK-based Paradox plans customer and early paradoxbuilders member who went on to build a different boat.
* [Paul V. (cruisenewsnet / sohaven2002)](paul-v.md) - A US small-boat designer and Paradox builder from the northern woods who documented his cold-climate build in a public "Build Book," volunteered as a list co-moderator, and posted the archive's fullest weight-vs-displacement discussion and real-time Everglades Challenge checkpoint reports.
* [Nels Arvidson](nels-arvidson.md) - A Canadian prospective Paradox builder who chronicled Matt Layden's 2003 Everglades Challenge win in real time, proposed a lengthened "motorsailer" variant, and questioned plywood deck-beam lamination.
* [Pete Hodges](pete-hodges.md) - A tall Florida paradoxbuilders member who planned and began a 10%-upsized "Paradox 1.1" and was a prolific source of construction, rig-scaling, and materials questions and ideas.
* [Jeff Forrest](jeff-forrest.md) - Southern Oregon builder constructing a Paradox from scratch, an early stretched-Paradox inquirer, and the list's main advocate of stitch-and-glue hull construction.
* ["guylita"](guylita.md) - A paradoxbuilders list member, known only by the screen name "guylita," whose questions on self-steering, lug-rig balance, and outboard ballast placement read as an experienced sailor's due diligence on the design.
* [Mat ("mat_man22")](mat-man22.md) - A paradoxbuilders list member and materials-minded contributor known for his metal-clad chine-runner shoe research and his scaling-formula guidance for enlarged Paradox variants.
* [Harald Lillevang](harald-lillevang.md) - A Danish list member and prospective Paradox builder, the first Scandinavia-based builder on record, drawn to the design after finding its sketches online in mid-2001.
* [David LeBlanc](david-leblanc.md) - A Seattle-area microcruising enthusiast who never built a Paradox but was a prolific contributor of materials, refit, antifouling, and small-boat knowledge to the paradoxbuilders community, 2002-2003.
* [William H. Longyard](william-longyard.md) - A Winston-Salem, NC technical contributor with no Paradox of his own, notable for firsthand knowledge of Sven Yrvind's designs and building philosophy and as author of the small-boat-voyaging book *A Speck on the Sea*.
* [Ron Eike](ron-eike.md) - Non-building paradoxbuilders list member who served as the thread's small-craft sailmaker referral and its persistent WaterTribe Everglades Challenge commentator.
* [David C. Smith](david-c-smith.md) - A Pacific Northwest professional boatbuilder who built his own Paradox "Swash" and became a leading voice on design fidelity, sail trim, cabin heating, ground tackle, and navigation lights.
* [Andrew Graham](andrew-graham.md) - A Brisbane, Australia builder who launched his Paradox "Aussie Paradox" — named "Zero" — on 29 November 2003, tentatively claimed as the first Australian-built Paradox to sail (Andrew later learned of an earlier, 2000-built secondhand example), after posting the archive's most detailed first-post construction account and a set of carefully flagged, plans-deviating build choices adapting the design to Australian plywood.
* [Kjell Karlsson](kjell-karlsson.md) - A Swedish builder constructing the only recorded Paradox in Sven Yrvind's "BRIS" foam-sandwich composite rather than plywood.
* [Jack Gardiner](jack-gardiner.md) - A Rhode Island carpenter and tradesman who committed to building a Paradox in December 2003 and opened the archive's fullest debate on building the hull in steel instead of plywood.
* [Steve (Kansas City)](steve-kc.md) - A Kansas City, MO prospective Paradox builder following a "start with the appendages" build plan on a multi-year, retirement-driven timeline.
* [Derek Clark](derek-clark.md) - UK Paradox builder who built "Enuf," contributed a steel-hull mast query and mainsheet/outboard questions, wrote a free community beginner's guide to building a Paradox, and sold the boat and left the group in September 2006 after a diabetes diagnosis made sailing her too risky.
* [Bill Serjeant](bill-serjeant.md) - UK small-boat sailor who built the community's principal UK web presence and boat registry, spending nearly two years as the group's de facto UK registrar and facilitator; his own Paradox, "Faith," is documented at boats/faith.md.
* [Mark Waters](mark-waters.md) - A builder (Los Angeles, California per his own 2006 account; Portland, Oregon per a 2014 census — unresolved) who completed his Paradox, "Raffles," strictly to plans, reaching 90% completion by February 2006, and whose finish was repeatedly praised as among the best built.
* [Matt (Ithaca, NY)](matt-noonan-ithaca.md) - An Ithaca, New York builder who started a from-scratch Paradox in June 2006 after building two Yost-designed folding kayaks and a PDRacer, keeping a public online build log.
* [Stephen Yahn](stephen-yahn.md) - A Puget Sound (Manchester, Washington) builder who started his Paradox, named "Ironsides," in July 2006 after a long run of detailed pre-build questions on rigging, ballast, and wiring, previously sailing a non-trailerable Picaroon.
* [Bengt Genell](bengt-genell.md) - A central Sweden builder who spent two years building a budget, plywood Paradox, launched on Lake Mälaren in July 2006 as "Ladikan," the design's second independent documented Swedish build.
* [Sean Mulligan](sean-mulligan.md) - Owner of the Paradox "Scout" (ex-"Tardis," built by Lezlie Henson), based at Lake Havasu, Arizona, founder of the Havasu Pocket Cruiser's Convention, and a prolific source of Paradox sailing video. Not to be confused with [Sean Herron](/boats/defiant.md).
* [Pete Martin](pete-martin.md) - A Cheltenham, England builder who ordered Paradox plans in October 2006 and started construction on 1 December 2006, building a boat he intends to name "JoHanna."
* [Ian Lang](ian-lang.md) - A North London, England builder who gathered plywood and timber quotes through spring 2007 and started construction on his Paradox, named "Irene May" after his late mother, in late June 2007.
* [Robert Biegler](robert-biegler.md) - A Norway-based owner who bought Derek Clark's former Paradox "Enuf," shipped her to the Trondheimsfjord area, re-fixed her ballast, and built two yulohs after the first was stolen.
* [James (Bradford, Yorkshire, UK)](james-bradford.md) - A Bradford, Yorkshire builder who began cutting plywood in January 2008 for a lightened, car-topping Enigma-style Paradox variant of his own design, after over a year floating and refining the same idea on the list.
* [John Gilbert ("JG")](john-gilbert.md) - A recurring non-builder technical commentator ("JG", gilberj55) active December 2006 - April 2008, contributing ballast physics, scaling, rig, yuloh, and construction technique across the archive without a Paradox of his own.
* [Jim Baltaxe](jim-baltaxe.md) - An Island Bay, Wellington, New Zealand correspondent with a professional composite-construction background who weighed building a Paradox in foam/glass before ordering and receiving stock plans in early 2008.
* [Jimmy Lu](jimmy-lu.md) - A Taiwan-based first-time boatbuilder (jimmylu88) whose rapid, professionally finished Paradox build drew unprompted praise from established list members and launched 18 February 2008.
* [Roselt Croeser](roselt-croeser.md) - A Western Australian builder, never a list correspondent, who built the junk-rigged Paradox "Planktos" from early 2013 to the end of 2016 across four locations while owner-building a house, and presented the build and its Shark Bay shakedown cruise to the Amateur Boat Builders' Association in April 2021.
* [Dave Skaife](dave-skaife.md) - An American microcruiser who built a Paradox in Hawaii and sailed it engine-free for four seasons (2014–2018) — Hawaii, the Gulf ICW, the Bahamas twice, and Cuba — leaving the archive's longest continuous first-person Paradox cruising log after Bill's Log at daveskaife.wordpress.com.
* [Alberto Callejon](alberto-callejon.md) - A Rawson, Chubut builder who started "Cachivache," billed as the most southerly Paradox on record, in May 2008 after weighing the design's suitability for the open South Atlantic.
* [Rodney Cannon](rodney-cannon.md) - A Venice, California newcomer to both sailing and boatbuilding who ordered Paradox plans in June 2008 and began construction that August on a boat later identified, in a 2014 census, as "Wizard Spawn."
* [Eerik Mee](eerik-mee.md) - The Estonian builder, based in Tartu, of "THU," a Paradox scaled up 20% on every plans dimension and given a purpose-designed cambered Chinese junk rig, launched in 2017 — the archive's only builder of a scaled-up Paradox actually built and sailed, and of a non-conversion junk rig on the design; documented on his blog paradoxbuild.blogspot.com under the byline "Eerik."
* [Lezlie Henson](lezlie-henson.md) - The Houston-area first-time builder of "Tardis," a stock Paradox she built to a high standard over roughly two years (2011–2012) and sold almost finished without ever sailing it; the boat became well-known under Sean Mulligan as "Scout." Documented on her blog, lezliesworld.blogspot.com.
* [Tim McKinney](tim-mckinney.md) - A Texas sailor who owns a Paradox built by Michael Beebe of Rockport, Texas, started the "Paradox Sailboat" Facebook group in February 2019, and gave the archive its first candid modern owner's account of the boat, including a 2019 Texas 200 attempt cut short by a snake bite.
* [André-François Bourbeau](andre-francois-bourbeau.md) - A Québec builder and designer of the Gorfnik who owns two Paradoxes and, in 2021, scratch-built a twin-masted Enigma ketch, "Question," with demountable beaching wheels and a rise-when-grounded rudder.
* [Simas Butavičius](simas-butavicius.md) - A Lithuanian builder whose exterior-grade Baltic birch Paradox, thinned to 12 mm bottom / 9 mm sides / 6 mm deck to offset the wood's extra weight, has become the archive's reference scantling scheme for Baltic and Nordic builders.
* [Andreas Westerberg](andreas-westerberg.md) - A Swedish builder who 3D-modelled his entire Paradox from the plans' drawings and CNC-routed the bulkheads and parts — the archive's only recorded digital-first, CNC-driven build of the design.
* [Christian Blohm](christian-blohm.md) - A north-east Ohio builder who spent months gathering materials before his first cuts in February 2023, then documented a careful first-time build (named "Rebel") that stalled half-finished in his garage.
* [Dave Fahy](dave-fahy.md) - A Gold Coast, Queensland builder who built and launched the Enigma 3.6 "Goliath" in March 2021, then substantially increased her ballast to tame a tender hull — the archive's only documented Australian Enigma builder-sailor.
* [Mark Brothers](mark-brothers.md) - A former racing sailor in New South Wales who returned to sailing in 2022 by buying and refurbishing a secondhand Paradox — one of about four then in Australia, "Pamela B" — and became the reference owner for the modern Michael Storer / Really Simple Sails sail.
* [Johan Bloom](johan-bloom.md) - A Swedish owner who sails "Tetris," a Divinycell foam-cored Paradox nicknamed "Paraplastic," out of the Bohuslän fjords year-round — the boat Kjell Karlsson built with Sven Yrvind's help (documented as the "BRIS Paradox").
