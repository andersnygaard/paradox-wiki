# People

The designer and notable builders/contributors in the [Paradox](/paradox.md)
community. Concepts are added here as they are sourced from the archive.

> The **designer, Matt Layden**, has his own top-level branch at
> [/matt-layden/index.md](/matt-layden/index.md) (his biography and his other
> designs). Individual built Paradox hulls live under
> [/boats/index.md](/boats/index.md).

# Concepts

* [Glen C. Maxwell](glen-maxwell.md) - Professional boat builder who founded the original paradoxbuilders Yahoo group (7 July 2001) and moderated it for over a year; built the Paradox "Zoë"; a valuable firsthand eyewitness of Matt Layden under sail — notably the one builder to sail his own Paradox alongside Matt's. This hub page indexes three detail sub-pages under `glen-maxwell/`: [build and "Zoë"](glen-maxwell/build-and-zoe.md) (his own build/launch/sailing history and the extensive construction Q&A he gave as a fellow builder), [list founder and moderator](glen-maxwell/list-founder-and-moderator.md) (founding the group, the recurring plans-purchase FAQ, the tripod-link spam saga, recruiting volunteer co-moderators), and [Matt Layden eyewitness record](glen-maxwell/matt-layden-eyewitness.md) (sailing alongside Matt at Ft. Desoto before the 2003 Everglades Challenge, the Cedar Key Messabout meeting, and welcoming Matt's own first-ever list post — primary-goal content on the designer himself).
* [The Bolducs — David & Mindy Bolduc](bolduc.md) - North Carolina micro-cruisers — Dave a carpenter and distributor of the Paradox plans, Mindy a pharmacist and co-skipper of Little Cruiser — and the archive's deepest firsthand authorities on Matt Layden, whom Dave met in 1992, whose boats they own (Little Cruiser, Swamp Thing, Enigma), and alongside whom they cruised the Bahamas. This hub page indexes three sub-pages under `bolduc/`: [Dave Bolduc](bolduc/dave.md) (his boats, list role, and the wide body of design, construction, and cruising knowledge he relayed from Matt), [Mindy Bolduc](bolduc/mindy.md) (the two-up Bahamas crossings, helming a severe overnight gale, and her own trip reports), and [Matt Layden firsthand record](bolduc/matt-layden-authority.md) (meeting Matt in 1992, owning and cruising his boats, the joint Bahamas cruises, and the techniques and words of his they preserved — primary-goal content on the designer himself).
* [Sven Yrvind](yrvind.md) - Swedish designer and ocean voyager celebrated for extremely small blue-water boats (the "Bris" series), who never posted to the list but is one of its most-mentioned outside figures — the benchmark against which Paradox's offshore capability is weighed, and the chine runner's most authoritative external champion, a personal friend of Matt Layden who sailed Paradox himself. This hub page indexes two sub-pages under `yrvind/`: [voyages and boats](yrvind/voyages-and-boats.md) (his ocean-voyaging career, his own boats, and his flex-composite/maximum-safety design philosophy) and [Paradox and the chine runner](yrvind/paradox-and-the-chine-runner.md) (his 1997 month sailing Paradox, his "deflector, not hydrofoil" theory, and his "greatest innovation of the century" verdict).
* [Don Elliott](don-elliott.md) - Early Paradox builder and author of the paid "Building the Paradox" construction articles, based in Tomah, Wisconsin, who advocated strict fidelity to Matt Layden's plans and resigned from the list over the foam-flotation dispute.
* [Chris Curtis](chris-curtis.md) - Stub; redirects to [boats/curtis-paradox.md](/boats/curtis-paradox.md), the canonical page for both Chris Curtis and his Paradox (built by Lezlie Hensen, Texas 200, Port Townsend Wooden Boat Show).
* [Alastair (Yeovil, England)](alastair.md) - UK builder of the Paradox "Little Jim," who built the boat single-handed in a garage barely longer than the hull and became one of the archive's most detailed cruising and construction correspondents.
* [Chuck Leinweber](chuck-leinweber.md) - Publisher of Duckworks Magazine and a boat builder active on the early paradoxbuilders list.
* [Chris Becker](chris-becker.md) - Australian (Brisbane, Queensland) Paradox builder constructing his boat "Walden" from scratch, and an early canvasser of the community's who-has-a-Paradox roster.
* [Paul (New Zealand)](paul-nz.md) - A prospective builder in the south of New Zealand drawn to the Paradox for enclosed, simple-rig sailing in rough weather.
* [Bert Vercauteren](bert-vercauteren.md) - A Belgian boat designer who bought the Paradox plans as inspiration for a larger two-person micro cruiser and proposed a furling-system fix that field-testing later disproved.
* [Chris Morejohn](chris-morejohn.md) - A Florida Keys sharpie builder and liveaboard cruiser in the Layden/Bolduc circle, recorded at the helm of a Paradox making 6 kts in 25 kts on Exuma Sound.
* [Jeff (UK, "doryman_uk")](jeff-doryman.md) - UK boatbuilder and list member who ordered materials for his own Paradox derivative, contributed chine-runner and stitch-and-glue construction methods, argued against bilge keels and metal-edged runners, and designed a from-scratch 16 ft redesign, "Paramour."
* [David Beard](david-beard.md) - A first-time Paradox builder from Tupelo, MS, who bought and refitted Bob Archibald's secondhand Paradox — naming her "Whisper" — while also building his own Paradox from scratch, and became one of the list's most prolific and enthusiastic contributors.
* [Charles Wilson](charles-wilson.md) - UK-based Paradox plans customer and early paradoxbuilders member who went on to build a different boat.
* [Paul V. (cruisenewsnet / sohaven2002)](paul-v.md) - A US small-boat designer and Paradox builder from the northern woods who documented his cold-climate build in a public "Build Book," volunteered as a list co-moderator, and posted the archive's fullest weight-vs-displacement discussion and real-time Everglades Challenge checkpoint reports.
* [Nels ("Edwin", Canada)](nels.md) - A Canadian prospective Paradox builder who chronicled Matt Layden's 2003 Everglades Challenge win in real time, proposed a lengthened "motorsailer" variant, and questioned plywood deck-beam lamination.
* [Pete Hodges](pete-hodges.md) - A tall Florida paradoxbuilders member who planned and began a 10%-upsized "Paradox 1.1" and was a prolific source of construction, rig-scaling, and materials questions and ideas.
* [Jeff Forrest](jeff-forrest.md) - A Southern Oregon builder constructing a Paradox from scratch, interested in a 10–15% stretch and an advocate of stitch-and-glue construction.
* ["guylita"](guylita.md) - A paradoxbuilders list member, known only by the screen name "guylita," who probed the Paradox's self-steering, lug-rig balance, and outboard ballast placement as an experienced sailor evaluating the design from the outside.
* [Mat ("mat_man22")](mat-man22.md) - A paradoxbuilders list member and materials-minded contributor known for his metal-clad chine-runner shoe research and his scaling-formula guidance for enlarged Paradox variants.
* [Harald Lillevang](harald-lillevang.md) - A Danish list member and prospective Paradox builder, the first Scandinavia-based builder on record, drawn to the design after finding its sketches online in mid-2001.
* [David LeBlanc](david-leblanc.md) - A Seattle-area microcruising enthusiast and prolific non-building commentator on the paradoxbuilders list, active 2002-2003, who ordered plans for a different boat (a Golant Gaffer), later judged Paradox too small for himself, and contributed materials, refit, and antifouling knowledge across many threads.
* [William H. Longyard](william-longyard.md) - A Winston-Salem, NC list member with no Paradox of his own who contributes recurring technical knowledge, notably a firsthand acquaintance with Sven Yrvind's designs and building philosophy.
* [Ron Eike](ron-eike.md) - A recurring paradoxbuilders list member ("sailman58") who never built a Paradox but served as the thread's small-craft sailmaker referral and offered persistent commentary on the WaterTribe Everglades Challenge.
* [DC](dc.md) - A Pacific Northwest professional boatbuilder building his own Paradox "sistership," named "Swash," and a substantial early-2003 contributor on design fidelity, sail trim, cabin heating, ground tackle, and navigation lights.
* [Andrew Graham](andrew-graham.md) - A Brisbane, Australia builder who launched his Paradox "Aussie Paradox" — named "Zero" — on 29 November 2003, tentatively claimed as the first Australian-built Paradox to sail (an earlier, 2000-built secondhand example later came to light), after posting the archive's most detailed first-post construction account and a set of carefully flagged, plans-deviating build choices adapting the design to Australian plywood.
* [Kjell Karlsson](kjell-karlsson.md) - A Swedish builder constructing the only recorded Paradox in Sven Yrvind's "BRIS" foam-sandwich composite rather than plywood.
* [Jack Gardiner](jack-gardiner.md) - A Rhode Island carpenter and tradesman who committed to building a Paradox in December 2003 and opened the archive's fullest debate on building the hull in steel instead of plywood.
* [Steve (Kansas City)](steve-kc.md) - A Kansas City, MO prospective Paradox builder who introduced himself with a "start with the appendages" build plan and a multi-year, retirement-driven timeline.
