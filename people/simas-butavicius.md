# Simas Butavičius

**Simas Butavičius** is an amateur [Paradox](/paradox.md) builder in **Lithuania**, in the Baltics, who started his boat in the winter of 2019–2020 and — after a long pause — glued up the bulkheads and sides in November 2024.[1][7] He is the archive's clearest example of building a Paradox in **exterior-grade Baltic birch plywood**, the cheapest good option available to him locally, and his solution to that wood's extra weight — thinning the panel schedule to 12mm bottom, 9mm sides, and 6mm deck — has become a reference scheme other Baltic and Nordic builders have since drawn on.[1]

## Material choice: Baltic birch, thinned to compensate

Butavičius chose exterior-grade Baltic birch plywood "because it's cheapest here," at roughly **680 kg/m³** — about **1.3 times the density of okoume**, the plywood the plans assume.[1] Rather than build to the plans' stock thicknesses in the heavier wood, he thinned the schedule to offset the weight gain, using **12mm for the bottom, 9mm for the sides, and 6mm for the deck**.[1] For the floorboards he used **English/European oak** (*Quercus robur*), chosen for being heavy — good below the waterline — and very rot-resistant; one board came from table-leg stock his neighbour gave him, ripped in half and glued to make wider boards.[1] For deck beams and the above-waterline transom bottom cleat he used **Norway spruce** (*Picea abies*), about half the weight of the oak.[1] For beams and some bulkhead-to-beam joints he used **polyurethane glue**, waterproof to the European **D4** standard and, by his account, the European equivalent of Gorilla glue — convenient to apply and easy to clean up, since the squeeze-out cures to a foam.[1]

## Build details

He built the **vent box separately on the table** rather than in place in the hull, since it involves finicky drain-pipe angles; after ruining two wood blocks trying to drill a long 35mm hole for the drain pipe, he instead cut a groove for it with a table saw and bedded the pipe in thickened epoxy, roughening the PVC pipe first with 40-grit sandpaper to help the epoxy grip it.[2] He built the **stem** from the plans' line drawings rather than measuring off the pre-assembled hull, having no confident way to take measurements from the assembly directly; it came out accurate to about ±1mm by laser level once the sides were pulled to meet it.[3] His mast is a tapered **birdmouth** spar.[4][5] Half-joking about the result of his choices, he called his build the **"Sport Edition."**[6]

After starting in the 2019–2020 winter, the build sat for roughly two years; in November 2024 he glued up the bulkheads and sides using a two-stage dry-assembly method — screwing the bulkheads in place to cut sheer-clamp notches, then re-doing the dry assembly with ground-down screws to leave gaps for thickened epoxy — that let him glue the hull together solo, in alignment, without the usual scramble.[7]

He has also engaged with the wider community on the [Facebook group](/references/paradox-facebook-group.md): asking after tips on rudder pintle rod, spar lumber, and sail cut; passing on build advice to other builders; and floating, as early as 2020, the idea of a shared online "paradox-wiki" of building tips — a suggestion this archive's bundle later realised in a different form.[8]
