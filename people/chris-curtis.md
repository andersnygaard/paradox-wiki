# Chris Curtis

**Chris Curtis**, of **Sandpoint, Idaho**, was the third owner of the [Paradox](/paradox.md) **"[Scout](/boats/scout.md)"** — the hull [Lezlie Henson](/people/lezlie-henson.md) built in Texas as "Tardis" and [Sean Mulligan](/people/sean-mulligan.md) finished and launched. He bought her from Mulligan, sailed her for many years in north Idaho and the Puget Sound, and sold her on in August 2024. He is a lifelong shoal-draft sailor, and "Scout" was one of a small succession of **[Matt Layden](/matt-layden/index.md) and Layden-influenced boats** he has owned. The boat's own build-and-sailing record, across all four owners, is kept at [Scout](/boats/scout.md); this page covers Curtis himself.

## Owner of "Scout"

Curtis bought the boat from **[Sean Mulligan](/people/sean-mulligan.md)**, making him her third owner in a chain of **Henson → Mulligan → Curtis → Lee**, and he rated her built to a very high standard.[1][2] Under his ownership she was **shown several times at the Port Townsend Wooden Boat Show**, pictured there around 2017, and in 2019 he called her a great boat in almost every way.[1] He day-sailed her hard on **Priest Lake** in north Idaho — once knocked down by a gust, though not past about 90° — cruised the **Puget Sound**, and camped aboard many nights.[2] Her 2014 **[Texas 200](/merits/texas-200.md#the-texas-200-and-a-port-townsend-wooden-boat-show-showing)** run was made under Mulligan, before Curtis owned her.

A refit he began — deck hardware to re-bed, windows to reinstall, a repaint, and a slow leak at the vent-hole end-grain — stalled when he moved on to a much larger boat, and he offered her for sale from 2022.[2] In **August 2024** he sold her to **Lee, of Maine**, who trailered her some 3,500 miles home.[8]

## A fleet of Layden boats

Curtis also owns an **[Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md) 460** named **"Fantasma"** — a Layden design slightly larger than a Paradox but lighter in displacement, built in the same style as the smaller Enigma, with an interior he reckons roughly 42 inches by 7 feet — which he has offered for sale over several years and exhibited at the Port Townsend Wooden Boat Festival.[4] An Enigma of his was inspected in depth by **Matt Layden** at Sail Oklahoma in 2014; by Curtis's account Layden "quietly gave me the OK, saying it was well built."[5]

By the mid-2020s he had, in his own phrase, graduated to a slightly bigger boat: a roughly 30,000 lb **[Hogfish Maximus](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md)** — **[Chris Morejohn](/people/chris-morejohn.md)'s** big shoal-draft sharpie, which Curtis calls basically a gigantic Paradox and beaches the same way. He lives aboard her, cruising from the Pacific Northwest down to Southern California and Mexico.[6][7] It was that move to a larger boat that finally freed "Scout" for sale.[8]

## On the design

Curtis set out his design loyalties plainly in the community: that the Paradox's windward performance is doubted only by people who do not own one, that her roller-furling lug is remarkable, and that ordinary keelboat sailors will never understand how much simpler and less stressful life is without a keel, grounding out being no worry at all.[3]
