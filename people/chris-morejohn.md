# Chris Morejohn

**Chris Morejohn** is a Florida Keys boatbuilder and liveaboard cruiser associated with the wider circle of small-boat builders around [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md) and the [Bolducs](/people/bolduc/dave.md). He is best known in the Paradox archive as the helmsman in the well-known photo of a [Paradox](/paradox.md) sailing on Exuma Sound, and beyond it as the designer-builder of the flat-bottomed ["Hogfish" sharpies](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md).

## What he built and did

Morejohn built his own boats in the Florida Keys — including the 32-foot sharpie "Hogfish Lips" and the larger "Hogfish Maximus" — and is remembered as "the creator of several large liveaboard sized sharpies as well as some revolutionary flats boats." His large flat-bottomed hulls are treated as a design in their own right at ["Hogfish Maximus" (Chris Morejohn's Paradox-family Modified Sharpie)](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md), and stand as a real-world proof point for the seaworthiness of sharpie hulls generally; see [blue-water suitability](/design/blue-water-suitability.md).

## Contributions and stories

- **The Exuma Sound performance photo.** Morejohn was at the helm of a heavily reefed Paradox making about 6 kts in 25 kts of wind on Exuma Sound (with Matt Layden and Mindy Bolduc below), the outing standing as evidence of the boat's real-world performance under its [lug-type rig](/components/rig/index.md).
- **An Atlantic crossing aboard "Hogfish Maximus."** In May 2004, Morejohn set out from Florida aboard his own **"Hogfish Maximus"** — "basically a 38 foot version of Paradox" — bound across the Atlantic for Portugal by way of Bermuda and the Azores, the passage that proved his chine-runner hull at ocean-crossing scale. The boat and that crossing are covered as a design in their own right at ["Hogfish Maximus"](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md); the full firsthand passage account is at [Glen C. Maxwell — Matt Layden eyewitness record](/people/glen-maxwell/matt-layden-eyewitness.md#an-atlantic-crossing-aboard-hogfish-maximus).
- **Epoxy choice.** He is remembered as using FASCO epoxy building his own boats in the Keys — an indirect data point in the wider discussion of [epoxy brands, chemistry, and cure behavior](/construction/epoxy-brands-and-chemistry.md) rather than a Paradox-specific technique.
- **The Gulf Stream collision.** He once sank a boat after striking a submerged obstruction (an oil drum, log, or container) at night, then drifted north in the Gulf Stream for 24 hours with sharks about before fishermen rescued him — a story cited to argue that the real dangers of an open-water passage are collisions with ships or unseen obstructions, not weather.
- **A 40 ft "Hogfish" listed for sale, later identified as "Jubilee."** A 40-foot version of his "Hogfish" sharpie design was advertised for sale in 2005 (via mermaidresearch.com), visibly built with the same "Paradox"-style chine runners as his other boats, and later photographed on a travelift under the name **"Jubilee."** Like "Hogfish Maximus," she combines the chine runners with a **centerboard**; see ["Hogfish Maximus" — chine runners plus a centerboard](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md#chine-runners-plus-a-centerboard) and the runner-sizing discussion at [how chine runners work](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md#narrow-wing-runners-on-a-chris-morejohn-sharpie-and-a-centerboard-plus-runner-combination).
- **A design blog, and two small Paradox-inspired cruisers.** On his own [Hogfish Design blog](/references/hogfish-design-website.md) Morejohn published two small-boat concepts drawn directly from his experience of Paradox: a 2016 ["16′ minimal cruiser"](/similar-boat-designs/morejohn-16-minimal-cruiser.md) — his idea of the smallest cruiser that works well at sea, with a ballasted daggerboard, an exterior cockpit, a lowerable mast, and a mizzen for heaving-to on a sea anchor — and a 2017 ["MULLUSK 16"](/similar-boat-designs/morejohn-mullusk-16.md), his own version of Paradox "a bit longer," keeping Matt Layden's roller-furling lug rig with a ballasted keel and deck seating. Writing in 2017 he recounts meeting Layden about 26 years earlier (≈1991) and sailing Paradox in the Exumas — the earliest date attached to his time in that circle.[9]

The Morejohns, cruising the Exumas, were met by others living aboard "Hogfish Lips" during a 1994 joint trip to the Bahamas in tandem with Matt Layden; the connection is recorded on [microcruising.com](/references/microcruising-website.md).
