# Roselt Croeser

**Roselt Croeser** is a Paradox builder in **Western Australia** and a member of the **Amateur Boat Builders' Association**, the Perth home-boatbuilding club.[1] He built the Paradox "**[Planktos](/boats/planktos.md)**" from **early 2013 to the end of 2016**, and presented the build and its shakedown cruise to Shark Bay at the association's **April 2021** meeting, the write-up of which is the archive's sole source on him.[1][2]

He stands apart from most builders on record in that he was **never a mailing-list correspondent**. His build ran years after the list era the archive is otherwise drawn from, worked up from the plans and — on his own assessment — from the builders' group behind them, which he credited alongside the quality of the plans in judging Paradox "the easiest type of boat to build."[3]

## Approach

The build was run **alongside owner-building a house**, moved through **four different locations**, and was finished deliberately to a **working boat** standard: "very strong, very simple," using whatever was already to hand.[2] That frugality is what makes the build notable — the mast was **repurposed from a sunken boat** and the sail came from an **SS18**, holding the whole build to about **$2500** in materials.[2][3]

He also **converted the boat from the stock lug rig to a junk rig**, making Planktos the only junk-rigged Paradox in the archive built by *converting* the stock lug rather than designing the rig from scratch — the other built-and-sailed junk-rig Paradox on record, [Eerik Mee](/people/eerik-mee.md)'s [THU](/boats/thu.md), was purpose-designed.[1] His verdict on the conversion, and his full account of the build, costs, and how she sails, are recorded at [Planktos](/boats/planktos.md).

## Earlier projects

Planktos was not his first boat. The association's newsletter refers readers to its **June–July 2014** and **April–May 2015** issues for his past projects.[1] Neither issue is held in the archive, so nothing further about those boats is on record.
