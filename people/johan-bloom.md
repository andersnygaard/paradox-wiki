# Johan Bloom

**Johan Bloom** owns and sails **"Tetris,"** a [Paradox](/paradox.md) built in Divinycell foam-core composite rather than the plans' plywood, out of the fjords on Sweden's west coast — sailing her year-round, including, by his own account, on New Year's Day.[1][4] He introduced himself to the [Paradox Sailboat Facebook group](/references/paradox-facebook-group.md) in **August 2020** as "a happy new owner of the only Paradox in Sweden or even Scandinavia," and other members recognized the boat on sight by an existing nickname, **"Paraplastic."**[1]

## A Divinycell hull, not of his own building

Bloom's Paradox departs from the stock plywood build the same way as one other boat on record in this archive: a **Divinycell foam core** — 30 mm in the deck and hull, 50 mm in the bottom — laminated in **epoxy and dacron outside** and **carbon-fibre and epoxy inside**, with a **bronze-sheathed bottom** and a **watertight hatch**, and built slightly wider and longer than Matt Layden's plans.[1][3] Bloom has been explicit that he did not build the boat himself: replying to a builder asking about foam-sandwich construction, he wrote that it was "not built by me but built to Yrvind's ideas and standards of a light indestructable boat," adding that it is "fully insulated for sailing all year" and "perfect for the area where I am, west coast of Sweden."[3] Other members' recognition of the "Paraplastic" name on sight of Bloom's boat — Pete Martin's "Ah, Paraplastic" on his 2020 introduction post, and again "Paraplastic was built from divinycell" on a later thread — carries over the nickname long used for [Kjell Karlsson](/people/kjell-karlsson.md)'s Divinycell-and-carbon "BRIS" Paradox, documented in this archive from 2003 in the mailing-list era.[1][8] A 2024 post named the builder directly: **Anders Nygaard**, replying to a prospective builder's question about foam-cored Paradoxes, wrote that "Kjell Karlsson built a Paradox called Paraplastic with help from Sven Yrvind," linking straight to Bloom's original 2020 introduction post — so Bloom's "Tetris" was built by [Kjell Karlsson](/people/kjell-karlsson.md) with **[Sven Yrvind](/people/yrvind.md)**'s help, and is now owned and sailed by Bloom.[9] The fuller record of that Kjell-Karlsson-built "Paraplastic" hull — its 2003–2006 construction in Yrvind's BRIS foam-sandwich method — is kept at [the "BRIS Paradox"](/boats/bris-paradox.md), which the project owner has **confirmed is this same boat** under Bloom's later ownership; the ownership chain is Kjell Karlsson (builder) → Johan Bloom. Two details in Bloom's second-hand account differ from Kjell's build record (he describes the bottom as bronze- rather than stainless-sheathed, and the inner skin as carbon fibre rather than glass) — the natural imprecision of an owner describing a boat he did not build.

## Sailing the west-coast fjords year-round

Bloom sails out of **Bohuslän**, the fjord-cut archipelago on Sweden's west coast, and early on, describing himself as a "new Paradoxian" but experienced sailor, asked the group for windward-trimming tips for when the breeze pipes up.[2] He rang in **2022** sailing the Swedish fjords on New Year's Day, and by midsummer that year was "scouting along" in the boat — by then named **"Tetris"** — exploring shallow areas and small islands he judged accessible only "to men in green boxes and kayaks," drawing a reply from a fellow member sailing at Kiel Week praising Bohuslän's near-endless midsummer light.[4][5] The following winter, waiting out the cold, he looked back on a summer 2022 outing on **Lake Siljan** in central Sweden while "longing for new adventures."[7] A photo of "Tetris" was later chosen to represent the group itself, which Bloom said he was honoured by.[6]
