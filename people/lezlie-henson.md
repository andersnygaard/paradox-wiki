# Lezlie Henson

**Lezlie Henson** is the builder of **"Tardis,"** a [Paradox](/paradox.md) she built to a high standard in the **Houston, Texas** area (Pasadena, then Kemah) between 2011 and 2012, as her first from-scratch boatbuilding project. She documented the whole build on a personal blog, `lezliesworld.blogspot.com`, preserved as a first-class source at [references/lezlie-henson-blog.md](/references/lezlie-henson-blog.md); this page draws the builder and her build out of it. She sold the boat **almost finished and never sailed her**; it went on to be well-known under its new owner **[Sean Mulligan](/people/sean-mulligan.md)** as **"[Scout](/boats/scout.md)"** — where the boat's launch, sailing, and Texas 200 record are documented in full.

She signs her blog only "Lezlie." The surname **"Henson"** used here follows the community record — Bill Serjeant's Paradox blog and owner list — though it is spelled inconsistently across sources ("Hensen," and "Hansen" in *Messing About In Boats*), and the blog itself does not give it.[1]

## The build

Henson came to the Paradox as a relative newcomer to both sailing and boatbuilding: she had learned to sail about three and a half years earlier on a **41′ Irwin ketch**, and — after her husband **Dale** found Matt Layden's design and she asked for plywood for Christmas — built two **PDRacers** (Puddle Duck Racers) first, to learn epoxy, fibreglass, rudders, leeboards, and tools, before taking on the Paradox.[2][3] She built to the plans plus **Don Elliott's** construction manual, working questions out on the **[Paradox Yahoo group](/references/mailing-list.md)**.[4]

The build ran roughly two years in a garage and driveway: first dry-fit of the bulkheads in **January 2011**, the bottom on by **March**, fit-out through 2011–12, and the boat all but complete by early 2013.[4][5] It is, so far as the archive records, the **only Paradox she is known to have built**.

## What she built, and how

The construction is documented in detail on the boat's page; in brief, hers was a **stock Paradox built carefully and a little beyond the minimum**. She **insulated** the hull (not all Paradoxes are) and covered the insulation with thin ply on epoxied dowel pegs; built the plans' **400 lb of lead "pig" ballast** into bolt-down "pens" alongside water-tank ballast that Dale — an electrical engineer — plumbed with 3-way valves for ballast use only; made her own **gimbaled stove** rather than buy one, modelling it on **[Glen Maxwell's](/people/glen-maxwell.md)** stove for "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)"; and finished her with off-white deck paint, a navy stripe, bright-varnished spars and trim, and non-skid decks.[5][6][7] The full construction record is at [Scout](/boats/scout.md#the-build-a-stock-paradox-named-tardis).

## Sold unlaunched, and a candid record of why

What sets her account apart is its candour about **why the boat was sold before it ever sailed**. Henson had begun the build as a dream shared with her husband; after they separated, the boat became "a reminder of a dead dream," she lost her motivation to finish and launch, and in **February 2013** she sold "Tardis" almost complete to a buyer in Arizona — writing, "I do wish I had been able to sail her... at least once."[8] Her January 2013 note of sudden interest from the United Kingdom is the seller's-side record of the same episode as the UK import-cost estimate documented on [Scout](/boats/scout.md).[9] When the boat was launched that September as "Scout" by Sean Mulligan, she followed it from his blog, closing her build series on the boat in someone else's hands.[10]
