# Walden

**"Walden"** is a [Paradox](/paradox.md) built by [Chris Becker](/people/chris-becker.md) in **Brisbane, Australia**, starting in 2001 — the earliest Australian Paradox build reported to the list, though an earlier, 2000-built example later came to light (see [boats/aussie-paradox.md](/boats/aussie-paradox.md)).

# The build

Construction began **20 August 2001** from **9 mm (3/8")** plywood, with bulkheads the first parts cut. Before committing to full-size ply, Chris built a **1:10 scale model of all the components** (cleats, chine logs, etc.) to check how they fit together — a modelling trick several builders used before cutting full-size panels (see [panel layout](/construction/panel-layout.md)).

The build then **stalled through autumn 2001**: a couple of sheets of 9 mm ply had been cut and paper patterns made, but work and health issues interrupted progress for several months. Work resumed **7 December 2001** with an order for the rest of the plywood and epoxy. Fit-out ideas under consideration at this point — a small **icebox** in the second forward compartment (starboard side, opposite the battery) and a small **detachable sink** alongside the stove in the transom — remained unresolved and were not yet built.

All ten sheets of plywood arrived **11 December 2001**: **Pacific Maple marine ply**, guaranteed void-free, costing **$AUS790** — see [build effort and workspace](/construction/build-effort.md). On **15 December** panels and bulkheads were marked out and material lists drafted. The same bulkhead-#2 layout fit issue documented by earlier builders turned up again, resolved by moving the bulkhead 40 mm and relocating the transom to a spare sheet (see [panel layout and nesting](/construction/panel-layout.md)); plan-layout omissions (vent trunk pieces, breasthook, butt blocks) were flagged, and the timing for resin-soaking the plywood before cutting was worked out — see [epoxy pre-coating technique](/construction/epoxy-coating-technique.md). Cutting continued through **Christmas Day**, and by **New Year's Day 2002** all panels were cut out, ready for timber cleats on the bulkheads and scarfing of the side panels. A **three-week military exercise** then limited progress to workshop maintenance, with a resume expected in late January or early February 2002.

The build resumed for a second time in **August 2002**, after a roughly seven-month gap in updates caused by three compounding factors: **work**, **no money**, and — the main cause — **moving house**. The move was "pretty far," requiring time to get a computer running again and to **rebuild the workshop**; previously located some distance from home, the new house had space to build **underneath it** instead, an improvement in the build situation. With the remaining timber, epoxy, and glass now bought, "Walden" stood at **bulkheads and scarfed panels** — consistent with, but not advanced beyond, its New Year's Day 2002 status — the intervening seven months having been consumed entirely by the house move rather than by build progress.

Fit-out plans disclosed at this point, not yet built: **two clear deck plates** on each side of the vent trunk (matching the existing water-tank/transom deck plates — see [transom deck plate](/construction/transom-deck-plate.md)), and **flyscreens** on the transom vent, deck vent, and main hatch, motivated by a dislike of mosquitoes and other biting insects — an early instance of insect-screening as a Paradox fit-out detail.

# Status timeline

* **20 Aug 2001** — under construction: bulkheads being cut from 9 mm ply; a 1:10 scale model made first.
* **Sep–Nov 2001** — building **stalled**: a couple of sheets of 9 mm ply cut and paper patterns made, but work and health issues interrupted the build for a long absence.
* **7 Dec 2001** — **back online and resuming** the build: ordering the rest of the plywood and epoxy, with good progress hoped for before an absence in January.
* **11 Dec 2001** — **all plywood delivered**: ten sheets of Pacific Maple marine ply, void-free, $AUS790.
* **15 Dec 2001** — a full day spent **marking out panels and bulkheads** and drafting material lists.
* **25 Dec 2001–1 Jan 2002** — Christmas Day cutting, and by New Year's Day **all panels cut out**, ready for cleats and scarfing; then three weeks on military exercise.
* **Aug 2002** — **identity confirmed, build resumes a second time**, after a seven-month gap consumed by work, finances, and a house move; boat still at bulkheads and scarfed panels.

## The name

The name **"Walden"** references Henry David Thoreau's book *Walden*. The choice fits a wider community reading of [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md)'s ethos as that of "a modern day Thoreau" — someone who quit his job, built a small boat, and sailed off to find Paradise — a characterization of the designer prompted by the boat's name, not a claim about Layden's actual biography.
