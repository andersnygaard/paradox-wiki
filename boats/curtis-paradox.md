# Chris Curtis's Paradox

The [Paradox](/paradox.md) owned by **Chris Curtis** (Sandpoint, Idaho). His boat is **"[Scout](/boats/scout.md)"** (built as "Tardis") — the hull [Lezlie Henson](/people/lezlie-henson.md) built in Texas and Sean Mulligan launched and sailed, bought on by Curtis as her third owner. Her full build-and-sailing record, across all three owners, lives at [Scout](/boats/scout.md); this page is the canonical home for **Chris Curtis himself**.

Distinct from two other below-threshold, citation-only correspondents who also used "Chris Curtis"/"Curtis" on the mailing list: a 2006 newcomer with a Sandpoint Computers signature and no boat/build on record (Sandpoint, Idaho, `joe_mapango`, u=181858645), and one from Phoenix, Arizona (see [references/paradox-uk-website.md](/references/paradox-uk-website.md#a-uk-and-worldwide-build-count)). That mailing-list `joe_mapango` maxi-Paradox builder resurfaced in January 2007, continuing the scale-up discussion with the same Dave Bolduc plans (see [design/ideas/stretched-paradox.md](/design/ideas/stretched-paradox.md#why-not-scale-moment-of-inertia-and-getting-matts-plans-directly)); his `X-Yahoo-Group-Post` member id had by then changed to 292110282, but the globally unique profile handle `joe_mapango` and the `ccurtis` account are unchanged, so this is the same below-threshold correspondent (that member id is a per-subscription token, not a stable profile identifier) — still distinct from the finished-boat Texas-200 Chris Curtis who is the subject of this page.

# Provenance and record

- **Chris Curtis, of Sandpoint, Idaho**, is the boat's **third owner**, having bought her from **Sean Mulligan** — the [Paradox](/paradox.md) **"[Scout](/boats/scout.md)"** that [Lezlie Henson](/people/lezlie-henson.md) built in Texas as "Tardis."[1]
- Under Curtis she has been **shown several times at the Port Townsend Wooden Boat Show** (pictured there ~2017), and he calls her "a great boat in almost every way."[1]
- The boat completed the **[Texas 200](/merits/texas-200.md#the-texas-200-and-a-port-townsend-wooden-boat-show-showing)** in 2014, during Mulligan's ownership.

"Chris Curtis's Paradox" and "[Scout](/boats/scout.md)" are **one hull under successive owners** — **Henson → Mulligan → Curtis** — not two Henson-built boats: Henson's blog records her building only one Paradox, and Curtis's own account has this boat built by Henson and bought from Mulligan. The boat's full record is kept at [Scout](/boats/scout.md); this page covers Chris Curtis as owner. (The source spells the builder "Hensen.")[1]
