* [Ground tackle and anchoring](ground-tackle.md) - Anchor choices, stowage, and cockpit-worked deployment/recovery techniques for the Paradox, whose small size and enclosed layout push ground tackle below deck rather than onto the bow, including competing flush-well designs for the bow and afterdeck.

* [Sailing characteristics and rig balance](sailing-characteristics.md) - How the Paradox's lug rig balances under sail — weather and lee helm on different points of sail, real-world tacking and jibing behavior, the weight-distribution and center-of-effort/center-of-lateral-resistance theory behind it, and how her directional stability compares with Little Cruiser and Swamp Thing.
* [Cabin heating for cold-weather sailing](cabin-heating.md) - Cabin heating options for the Paradox in cold weather — body heat and solar gain, the cabin stove run on low with cracked ventilation, an inverted-flower-pot heat retainer, a can-based charcoal or kerosene cabin heater, window-condensation mitigation, and why unvented combustion heaters are a carbon-monoxide hazard.
* [Crew size and cabin fit ("wearing the boat")](crew-size-and-fit.md) - How a sailor's height and weight fit the Paradox's enclosed cabin, and how the boat handles being sailed and cruised with two or three aboard.

* [Auxiliary outboard power](auxiliary-outboard-power.md) - Auxiliary outboard-motor options for the Paradox, from converting a small outboard to run on an external fuel tank to multi-year field experience and the eventual move to engine-free sail-and-oar cruising.
* [Sculling and yuloh technique](sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md) - How to scull a Matt Layden micro-cruiser with a yuloh as engine-free auxiliary propulsion, and the evidence — from a firsthand technique write-up and Watertribe results to Matt Layden's own race-tested yuloh speed — that it works as primary propulsion, not just a calm-weather trick.
* [Head and sanitation arrangements](head-and-sanitation.md) - Sanitation aboard a Paradox is improvised rather than designed-in — bucket-and-chuck-it and disposable-bagging practice, placement options for a portable head, and the legality and uneven enforcement of overboard disposal.
* [Bailing and bilge pumps](bailing-and-bilge-pumps.md) - How a Paradox is bailed without opening the hatch — the Whale Guzzler/Gusher manual pump installation and its stern discharge, the separate vent-trunk drain, and how much real use the pump gets against a closed-bilge, sponge-first philosophy.
* [Provisioning — food and water for multi-month cruises](provisioning-and-water.md) - How food and water are provisioned for multi-month winter Bahamas cruises aboard a Matt Layden micro-cruiser with no refrigeration, how the ship's stores double as working ballast, and two regional trolling techniques for supplementing stores with caught fish.
* [Southern California sailing and the Catalina Island crossing question](southern-california-and-catalina-crossing.md) - Compares a Southern California-to-Catalina Island crossing with the Paradox's routine, longer and rougher Gulf Stream passage to the Bahamas, and tracks the running census of how many Paradoxes had been launched, from four in May 2002 to eight or more named boats by October 2004.
* [Pointing and tacking-angle expectations](pointing-and-tacking-angles.md) - Concrete tacking-angle figures for the Paradox — a 90/110-degree estimate and a 19-tack GPS-measured 98-150 degree range — plus a practical afloat-trim check for ballast and a forward-weight-shift technique for completing a stubborn tack.
* [Running lights](running-lights.md) - Covers why Paradox and Little Cruiser carry a single masthead all-around white light instead of red/green running lights, and how builders wire and construct compact LED masthead units.
* [Hull speed — the displacement-speed formula and real-world GPS figures](hull-speed.md) - The Paradox's theoretical hull speed under the classic 1.3-times-square-root-of-waterline formula, set against logged GPS speed readings and real-world cruising figures.
* [GPS and navigation instruments](gps-navigation.md) - Choosing, buying, and setting up a handheld marine GPS for a Paradox, whether a depth sounder is worth fitting to a boat that draws about a foot, and compass choice and mounting.
* [Heavy-weather tactics — rudder position under sea anchor, lying a-hull, and maximum heel](heavy-weather-tactics.md) - Storm and heavy-weather tactics for the Paradox — rudder position under a sea anchor, why lying a-hull is discouraged in favor of a sea anchor, a starboard-tack sea-anchor technique for heaving-to, the Bolducs' practice-tested reduce-sail-and-shelter doctrine, why a keel-less hull resists tripping in steep waves, why a closed cabin is a comfort choice rather than a trap, and whether the crew needs to be physically restrained during a knockdown.
* [Gulf Stream crossing (Florida–Bahamas)](gulf-stream-crossing.md) - The hard-won method for crossing the ~65 nm of open ocean between Florida and the Bahamas in a Matt Layden micro-cruiser — weather-window timing, routing, and, above all, ship-traffic collision avoidance.
* [Radio and electronics](radio-and-electronics.md) - Options for onboard HF radio and e-mail on a Paradox, from the mast's lightning-protection wiring as a ready-made antenna groundplane to portable antennas, SailMail/Winlink tradeoffs, non-ham alternatives, the boat's modest baseline electrical load, and practical 12-volt wiring-gauge choices for a low-draw interior.
* [Onboard entertainment and diversions](onboard-entertainment-and-diversions.md) - What to bring aboard for music, reading, and leisure — battery-powered consumer electronics and reused legacy 12-volt gear weighed against a minimalist safety-gear-only-plus-book-and-harmonica philosophy, and why a compact acoustic instrument beats a full-sized one in so small a cabin.
