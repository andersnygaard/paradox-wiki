# Operations

Sailing, handling, cruising, trailering, and maintenance of the
[Paradox](/paradox.md) in practice — how the boat actually behaves and is used.
Concepts are added here as they are sourced from the archive.

# Concepts

* [Texas 200](texas-200.md) - A long-distance small-boat coastal raid along the Texas coast that owners cite as proof of the Paradox's real-world cruising capability.
* [Ground tackle and anchoring](ground-tackle.md) - Anchor choices, stowage, and cockpit-worked deployment/recovery techniques for the Paradox, whose small size and enclosed layout push ground tackle below deck rather than onto the bow.
* [Secondhand Paradox market](secondhand-market.md) - Resale-market data points for Paradox and a sibling Matt Layden design, spanning a $500 complete Paradox sold in 2001, a June 2002 listing for the older Layden design "GJAC," and a 2003 buyer looking for an already-built Paradox.
* [Self-steering](self-steering.md) - How well a Paradox holds a course unattended — tiller-rope friction gives steady light-air self-steering, stronger winds call for hands-free capability to reef, and a purpose-built wind vane remains an untried but plausible option.
* [Sailing characteristics and rig balance](sailing-characteristics.md) - How the Paradox's lug rig balances under sail — weather and lee helm on different points of sail, real-world tacking and jibing behavior, the weight-distribution and center-of-effort/center-of-lateral-resistance theory behind it, and how her directional stability compares with Little Cruiser and Swamp Thing.
* [Cabin heating for cold-weather sailing](cabin-heating.md) - Cabin heating options for the Paradox in cold weather — body heat and solar gain, the cabin stove run on low with cracked ventilation, an inverted-flower-pot heat retainer, window-condensation mitigation, and why unvented combustion heaters are a carbon-monoxide hazard.
* [Newcomer FAQ — cabin comfort, capacity, and perceived safety](newcomer-faq-comfort-and-safety.md) - Stub; folded into [first-boat-suitability.md](first-boat-suitability.md) as an "open newcomer questions" subsection.
* [Crew size and cabin fit ("wearing the boat")](crew-size-and-fit.md) - How a sailor's height and weight fit the Paradox's enclosed cabin, and how the boat performs sailed and cruised with two or three aboard.
* [Trailering — tow weight, vehicle capacity, and trailer adequacy](trailering.md) - Trailering a Paradox — fully-loaded tow weight versus vehicle capacity, why a stock or undersized trailer fails and how to build/rebuild an adequate one, ground-clearance and low-tide launch considerations, and the Bolducs' real-world trailering practice.
* [Is Paradox a suitable first boat?](first-boat-suitability.md) - Weighs whether a complete novice should attempt a Paradox as a first build/boat, ranging from caution about its finishing detail to successful first-time builders, and lays out the practical build-time and difficulty figures involved.
* [Auxiliary outboard power](auxiliary-outboard-power.md) - Auxiliary outboard-motor options for the Paradox, from converting a small outboard to run on an external fuel tank to the Bolducs' multi-year field experience and eventual move to engine-free sail-and-oar cruising.
* [Sculling and yuloh technique](sculling-and-yuloh-technique.md) - How to scull a Matt Layden micro-cruiser with a yuloh as engine-free auxiliary propulsion, and the evidence — from David Bolduc's technique write-up and Watertribe results to Matt Layden's own race-tested yuloh speed — that it works as primary propulsion, not just a calm-weather trick.
* [Head and sanitation arrangements](head-and-sanitation.md) - Options and practice for handling sanitation aboard a Paradox, which has no dedicated stowage for a portable head — ranging from placement ideas for a marine sanitation device to the informal "bucket and chuck it" practice, its legality and uneven enforcement, and disposable-bagging alternatives.
* [Provisioning — food and water for multi-month cruises](provisioning-and-water.md) - How the Bolducs provision food and water for multi-month winter Bahamas cruises aboard a Matt Layden micro-cruiser with no refrigeration, how the ship's stores double as working ballast, and two regional trolling techniques for supplementing stores with caught fish.
* [Group cruise planning and firsthand trip reports](group-cruise-reports.md) - Group-cruising practice among Paradox owners — the Apalachicola Bay and Cedar Key gatherings that became the community's recurring meeting points, plus firsthand two-up and solo winter cruise reports that ground out crew-size, cold-weather, and heavy-weather handling knowledge.
* [Southern California sailing and the Catalina Island crossing question](southern-california-and-catalina-crossing.md) - Compares a Southern California-to-Catalina Island crossing with the Paradox's routine, longer and rougher Gulf Stream passage to the Bahamas, and tracks the archive's running census of how many Paradoxes had been launched, from four in May 2002 to eight or more named boats by October 2004.
* [Pointing and tacking-angle expectations](pointing-and-tacking-angles.md) - The archive's only concrete tacking-angle figures for the Paradox — 90 degrees at best, 110 degrees for better speed and comfort — plus a practical afloat-trim check for ballast.
* [Running lights](running-lights.md) - Explains why Paradox and Little Cruiser carry a single masthead all-around white (anchor) light rather than conventional red/green running lights, the COLREGs reasoning behind it, options for building a compact LED tricolour/all-round masthead unit and routing its wiring without cutting the mast open, and the ongoing kerosene-vs-LED debate over cost, legality, and reliability.
* [Hull speed — the displacement-speed formula and real-world GPS figures](hull-speed.md) - The Paradox's theoretical hull speed under the classic 1.3-times-square-root-of-waterline formula, set against Glen Maxwell's own GPS speed readings and the Bolducs' logged cruising figures.
* [GPS and navigation instruments](gps-navigation.md) - Choosing, buying, and setting up a handheld marine GPS for a Paradox, from entry-level Garmin units and the eTrex Mariner to waypoint software, firmware updates, and a theft-deterrent personalization trick.
* [Heavy-weather tactics — rudder position under sea anchor, lying a-hull, and maximum heel](heavy-weather-tactics.md) - Storm and heavy-weather tactics for the Paradox — rudder position under a sea anchor, why lying a-hull is discouraged in favor of a sea anchor, and the Bolducs' practice-tested reduce-sail-and-shelter doctrine.
* [Gulf Stream crossing (Florida–Bahamas)](gulf-stream-crossing.md) - The Bolducs' hard-won method for crossing the ~65 nm of open ocean between Florida and the Bahamas in a Matt Layden micro-cruiser — weather-window timing, routing, and, above all, ship-traffic collision avoidance.
* [Radio and electronics](radio-and-electronics.md) - Options for onboard HF radio on a Paradox, including using the mast's built-in lightning-protection wiring as a ready-made antenna groundplane, versus portable antennas and non-ham weather-data alternatives.
