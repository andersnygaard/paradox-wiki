# Registering a home-built Paradox — state hull numbering

Once a home-built [Paradox](/paradox.md) is finished, a US builder faces a purely administrative step before she can legally go afloat: registering the boat and obtaining a state-issued hull identification number. The requirement traces to federal Coast Guard law and works essentially the same in every state, regardless of which state agency actually administers it.[1][2][3]

## The process

Keep every materials receipt through the build — this paperwork is what proves the boat was actually built rather than stolen when it comes time to register her. At the state's boat- or vehicle-licensing office (a state Department of Motor Vehicles, in most cases), declare the boat built for personal use rather than for resale, and give the office an estimated value. The state in turn issues a permanent hull identification number, affixed to the boat's right rear transom area for good — the same number a police department uses to identify and return a stolen boat to its owner.[3]

## Two independent state accounts agree

A Washington State builder's own registration is the fullest first-hand account on record; a California builder, going through the process independently a couple of years later, confirmed his own state's DMV gave him the identical answer.[1][2][3][4]
