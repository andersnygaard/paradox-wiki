# Registration and the US "fourteen foot rule"

Paradox's stock **13'10"** length has been attributed in part to a US "14 ft rule": a small craft **under 14 ft** that is **not powered by an engine** (oars, paddles, or a Chinese yuloh/"yuhlo" are fine) does **not need to carry registration numbers and letters** on its hull.[1][2] Registration requirements otherwise vary by US state regardless of the 14 ft threshold.[3] No specific US statute or state is cited for the rule in the source discussion, so it should be read as a builder's understanding rather than a verified legal citation — and, lacking a UK equivalent, it is what let [Paramour](/design/variants/paramour-design.md)'s designer size her up to 16 ft.[4]

## A bowsprit, and how state registration rules treat it

A gaff-sloop rig with a bowsprit was floated purely speculatively, illustrated with a photo of an unrelated Brewer-designed sloop's bowsprit, without further development or a build.[5] The idea it prompted with more substance was a hinged bow anchor roller, to keep the anchor from scratching the bow when brought back aboard — but a roller of that kind pushes the boat's effective length-overall out beyond 14 ft, which matters directly for the 14 ft registration threshold above: at her stock **13'10"** and with no engine, Paradox needs no registration in Florida, Alabama, or Texas, a margin an anchor roller or bowsprit could easily erase.[6] Texas specifically excludes bowsprits and bumkins from how a hull's registered length is measured, so a Texas-registered Paradox could gain a bowsprit or anchor roller without crossing into the 14 ft threshold — a state-specific answer to the general rule stated above, rather than a universal one.[7]
