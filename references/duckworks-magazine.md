# Duckworks Magazine

**Duckworks Magazine** is an online magazine for small-boat and amateur boatbuilders.

It intersects the Paradox community as an outlet for builders' tips: permission was sought and granted to reprint a [bottom-painting-over-epoxy technique](/construction/bottom-painting.md) in Duckworks in 2001.

Its larger contribution to the archive is as a **republisher**. In 2004 it reprinted **"Paradox Sea Trials,"** the first-person account of the 3–9 December 2001 shakedown cruise of "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)" from Cedar Key to Honeymoon Island, Florida — the archive's best-documented single cruise of a builder-completed Paradox, and the source of its densest set of real-world speed figures for the design. The article had appeared the previous August in *[Messing About In Boats](/references/messing-about-in-boats.md)*; the two texts are identical, so Duckworks adds no new content, but its reprint is a **cleaner, more legible witness** to the same wording than the scanned MAIB original. It also carries **ten photographs** with the article. Where a fact from this cruise is cited anywhere in the tree, both publications are named in the citation for that reason.[3]

Only this single article is covered in this bundle; the rest of Duckworks Magazine's wider small-boat coverage is not examined here and is a plausible source of further Layden-adjacent material.

Duckworks also carries two original pieces — not reprints — documenting the build and launch of a second Paradox: "[Enuf](/boats/enuf.md)," the second UK-built example, by [Derek Clark](/people/derek-clark.md). "Building a Paradox by a Beginner" (`06/projects/paradox/index.htm`) covers the build; "Paradox 'Enuf' Launched" (`07/outings/paradox/index.htm`), published the following year, covers her July 2006 launch at Ridge Wharf near Wareham, Dorset.[4] Neither page is covered in this bundle beyond the summary above; a fuller account of the second article's later-flagged content is cited at [boats/enuf.md](/boats/enuf.md).
