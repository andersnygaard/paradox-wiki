# Fireside Yarns "MAYDAY MAYDAY MAYDAY" — Roger Barnes on crossing Bideford Bar

In an undated **"Fireside Yarns"** video, **Roger Barnes** — president of the **Dinghy Cruising Association (DCA)** — recounted a late-September DCA passage in **Bideford (Barnstaple) Bay**, on the north Devon coast, that he sailed in his own **Ilur** in company with a friend, "Alistair," in his **"little green Paradox"** [1]. Unlike the [AYRS meeting talk](/references/ayrs-meeting-video.md), which is Alastair's own first-person account of his boat, this is a **third-party eyewitness source**: a different, experienced sailor describing "[Little Jim](/boats/little-jim.md)" seen from another boat in extreme conditions [1].

## What it is used for

Its value to this bundle is a single, unusual data point — an **outside observer watching a Paradox cross a breaking bar in a gale**. Running back in over Bideford Bar in a rising sea, Barnes saw "Little Jim," a little ahead of him, **"vanish completely between the waves"** — seas he put at "at least 6 m … maybe 8 m" against the boat's roughly 5 m mast — and get through, while his own Ilur was rolled over, flooded, and prompted a **Mayday call** that launched both Appledore lifeboats [1]. Only the Paradox-relevant portion is drawn on here; the bulk of the video is Barnes's own account of his capsize, the Mayday, and the lifeboat response, ingested only so far as it sets the scene for the Paradox [1].

**A caveat carried onto the boat page:** the capsize and the Mayday in the video were **Barnes's own Ilur, not the Paradox** — "Little Jim" went in ahead of him and crossed the bar unaided. The account therefore stands as outside testimony to the design's heavy-weather ability, **not** as a record of a Paradox knockdown on this passage (Alastair's own knockdowns are documented separately on the [boat's page](/boats/little-jim.md)).

## Identifying the boat

Barnes names neither the boat nor his friend's surname, calling her only "his little green Paradox" and the sailor "Alistair" [1]. The identification as **Alastair Law's "Little Jim"** is nonetheless secure on converging grounds: Alastair Law is the DCA's long-standing Paradox owner (Barnes, in the [AYRS meeting](/references/ayrs-meeting-video.md), calls him "one of our most regular attenders"); "Little Jim" is a **green** boat — her hull is "Great Mills … Buckingham green" (see [boat page](/boats/little-jim.md#a-topping-lift-question-from-abroad-answered-with-photographs-august-2008)), and Alastair himself jokes about her "green rubbing strake"; and the cruising ground — **Appledore, the Torridge/Taw estuary, Clovelly, and Lundy** — is precisely where Alastair sailed her (compare his own [Appledore–Clovelly passage](/boats/little-jim.md#a-load-tested-anchor-rode-measurement-and-a-passage-to-clovelly-july-2008)).

## Provenance and dating

The source is a YouTube **auto-generated transcript**; punctuation, speaker attribution, and proper nouns are approximate, so wording is cited as paraphrase unless clearly verbatim. Several place names in the raw transcript are garbled and are decoded here from context: **"Celli" → Clovelly** (a drying harbour with an ancient stone breakwater in Bideford Bay), **"bidford" → Bideford/Barnstaple**, **"londy" → Lundy**, and the boat name in the opening Mayday hail (**"AAL Dr, AAL Dr, AAL Dr"**) → **"Avel Dro,"** Barnes's Ilur. The passage is dated only to "the end of September"; the "Fireside Yarns" format and production place it well after the 2001–2008 mailing-list era, but no firm year is stated in the video and none is asserted here.
