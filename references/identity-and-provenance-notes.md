# Identity and provenance notes

A few names and spellings in this bundle are not self-evident from a single source. They were settled by weighing a person's own self-identification against how publishers printed their name, or by taking a name as inferred rather than given. That reasoning is recorded here, once, so the articles that touch these names can state the settled result plainly and link back rather than re-run the argument each time. This is an editorial-methods note, not archive fact; it holds no claims a builder needs.

## Alastair Law — the forename spelling

The bundle spells the name **Alastair Law**. The surname appears only in print sources — two *Dinghy Cruising Association Bulletin* articles, a *Messing About in Boats* cruising account, and an Amateur Yacht Research Society talk — and never in the mailing-list archive itself. The forename is spelled **"Alastair"** on the strength of self-identification: his own email account's display name and his message sign-offs use "Alastair" consistently, across hundreds of archive messages. The print sources spell it **"Alistair"**, and a single *Bulletin* issue prints it **both ways** — "Alistair" in one article's byline, "Alastair" in a companion article's running text and photo credit two pages later. One publisher setting the name two ways in one issue is what settles it: "Alistair" is a publication-side variant, and the owner's own spelling is taken as controlling. The collated evidence is held in the citations on [Alastair Law](/people/alastair-law.md).

## "The Edge" — Don Elliott's Paradox

Don Elliott's own boat is referred to as **"The Edge"**, but the name was never confirmed by Don Elliott himself; it was inferred from launch photographs, and a 2014 owners' census independently uses the same name. The boat's page is filed under its builder rather than the inferred name. See [Don Elliott's Paradox](/boats/elliott-paradox.md).

## Names deliberately left unresolved

Some questions the record does not settle, and the bundle records the conflict rather than picking a side:

- A 2014 census spells one owner's name **"Hugh Miller"**; a companion post gives **"Hugh Millar."** Both are preserved.
- A 2014 census places one builder in **Portland, Oregon**; every other source places him in **Los Angeles, California** (see [Mark Waters](/people/mark-waters.md)).
- One builder's death is reported only in a single 2014 census entry and is not otherwise attested; it is recorded as reported, not confirmed.
