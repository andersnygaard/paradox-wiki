# Paradox Sailboat Facebook group

The **"Paradox Sailboat" Facebook group** (`https://www.facebook.com/groups/833651060307712`) is the private online community where [Paradox](/paradox.md) builders and owners gather, and it is the archive's principal source for the design's **modern, post-mailing-list era**. Where the [Paradox builders mailing list](/references/mailing-list.md) carries the community from 2001 to the late 2000s, this group carries it forward: a running record, in the builders' own words and photographs, of who was building and sailing the boat through the years either side of 2020. It was started in **February 2019** by **Tim McKinney** — whose "first and only real member" at the outset was **[Sean Mulligan](/people/sean-mulligan.md)** — as "a group interested in the Paradox Sailboat designed by Matt Layden. A little cruiser full of surprises."[1][2]

It is a **source, not a subject**: the value here is the building and sailing the group records, not the group itself. It is distinct from, and should not be confused with, the older **"Paradox" Facebook group** that [Alberto Callejon](/people/alberto-callejon.md) created in August 2011, or the [groups.io mailing list](/references/mailing-list.md) that succeeded the Yahoo group — several members belong to more than one.[3]

## What the group is worth as evidence

Its worth is **breadth and recency**. Across **833 posts and 975 comments** the group gathers first-hand testimony from roughly **180 posters** — builders on four or more continents, owners of finished boats, and prospective builders working from the plans — over a span the older sources do not reach. It is the fullest record the archive has of the **decade after** the mailing list went quiet: who was building, what materials and methods they used when the old lumber and hardware sources had changed, and how the finished boats actually sailed. Much of it is **owner's-own-voice primary source** — build logs posted plank by plank, launch announcements, sailing reports, and failures described candidly by the person they happened to.

It is also the archive's best register of **who the modern Paradox community is**. Many of the boats and builders the bundle already knows from other sources appear here in their own words — [Roselt Croeser](/people/roselt-croeser.md) (posting as "Waldo Cruiser") reporting how "[Planktos](/boats/planktos.md)" sails; [Chris Curtis](/boats/curtis-paradox.md) listing "[Scout](/boats/scout.md)" for sale and describing his other boats; [Eerik Mee](/people/eerik-mee.md) posting his junk-rigged "[THU](/boats/thu.md)"; [Alberto Callejon](/people/alberto-callejon.md) on "[Cachivache](/boats/cachivache.md)"; [David Beard](/people/david-beard.md) on "[Whisper](/boats/whisper.md)"; [Alastair Law](/people/alastair-law.md) answering questions about "[Little Jim](/boats/little-jim.md)." Alongside them are builders and boats the archive had no other record of at all.

Its limits should be read with that. It is a **social-media stream**, not an edited record: much of its bulk is membership administration, plans-sourcing questions asked and answered dozens of times over, and short exchanges that carry no durable fact. Photographs attached to posts are frequently the substance, and text-only records of them lose that. Attribution rests on **Facebook display names**, which are mutable, sometimes pseudonymous ("Waldo Cruiser," "Timbo Wally Smiff," "Burgman Scooter"), and occasionally shared — so identifying the person behind a handle requires corroboration, exactly as with the mailing list's screen names. And like any forum it contains opinion, second-hand report, and error next to first-hand fact; the [source-authority order](/paradox.md) that governs the rest of the bundle applies here too, with a builder's account of their own boat outranking bystanders' recollections.

## Provenance and dating

The group is **private**, so its posts are not publicly reachable and its Facebook URLs resolve only for members. The record drawn on here spans the group's first post, **5 February 2019**, to **1 July 2026**, and totals **833 posts, 975 comments, 219 photographs, and 184 distinct post authors** (372 distinct participants counting commenters).

## What it adds to the bundle

The group's durable content is drawn out on the boat, person, and topic pages it bears on. In outline, it supplies:

- **Modern builders and boats new to the archive** — among them **André-François Bourbeau**, the French-Canadian builder of two Paradoxes and designer of the [Gorfnik](/similar-boat-designs/gorfnik-paraduck.md), who documented a full Enigma-derived ketch build; **Andreas Westerberg**, who 3D-modelled the plans and cut a Swedish Paradox on a CNC router; **Simas Butavičius**, building in Baltic birch in Lithuania; **Christian Blohm**, building in Ohio; **Dave Fahy**, whose Enigma 3.6 "Goliath" was built and launched in Australia; and **Johan Bloom**, sailing a foam-cored Paradox on the west coast of Sweden year-round.
- **First-hand sailing and modification reports** on boats the archive already knew at second hand — how "[Planktos](/boats/planktos.md)," "[Scout](/boats/scout.md)," "[THU](/boats/thu.md)," and "[Cachivache](/boats/cachivache.md)" actually sail, and what their owners changed.
- **Recurring practical knowledge** on materials and methods in the modern market — plywood choices when the specified stock is unavailable, foam-core and Divinycell alternatives, junk-rig conversions, electric outboards, self-steering, and CNC and 3D-printed build aids.
- **Events** the community turned out for, including the **[Texas 200](/merits/texas-200.md)** and the entry of a Paradox in the **2026 Race to Alaska** (Team Forget Me Knot).

## Citing this source

Because the group is private, its posts are reachable only to members, but each post's own id is a stable, resolvable address: `https://www.facebook.com/groups/833651060307712/posts/<id>/`. Cite in the form:

> [Paradox Sailboat Facebook group](/references/paradox-facebook-group.md) — [Author], post <id>, YYYY-MM-DD — [detail].

The post id alone carries the address, so the raw URL is not repeated in each citation; it is reconstructed from the pattern above.

Comments are cited to the post they hang under, naming the commenter. As a dated record of a historical community, its content is cited in the past tense and dated to the post it comes from, consistent with the bundle's historical-archive convention. Display names are reproduced as the group shows them; where a name has been resolved to a person documented elsewhere, the citation says so.
