# AYRS meeting talk — Alastair on "Little Jim" (May 2020)

In **May 2020**, [Alastair](/people/alastair.md) (Yeovil, England) gave a talk
on his Paradox "[Little Jim](/boats/little-jim.md)" at an online meeting of the
**Amateur Yacht Research Society (AYRS)**, held over Zoom during the COVID-19
lockdown. A recording was posted to YouTube; the talk was transcribed and the
transcript imported to this bundle under `source-youtube/`.

Unlike the [mailing-list archive](/references/mailing-list.md) (contemporaneous
2001–2004 email), this is a **retrospective, first-person primary source** — the
same builder describing the same boat some **seventeen years after her launch**,
with the perspective of long ownership. It is firsthand authority: Alastair
built "Little Jim" single-handed and has cruised her extensively, so his account
of her handling, stability, and rig is treated as the builder's own evidence for
his boat. Where it overlaps the archive it largely **corroborates** the earlier
record; where it adds detail (a spring-balance masthead righting-moment test,
the seat's position near the centre of pitch rotation, long-run yuloh cruising
speed, and the boat's later ballast state) it is recorded as a new data point,
dated 2020.

The talk also independently confirms basic provenance from outside the mailing
list: Alastair names the designer as **"Matt Leyden"** (Matt Layden), "an
amateur designer" who "lives in Florida" and built the original Paradox to
"sail to the Bahamas and stay there for a couple of months."

## Provenance and dating

The source is a YouTube auto-generated **transcript** of the meeting recording,
titled *"Alastair Law – Amateur Yacht Research Society (AYRS) meeting May
2020."* The uploader's title gives Alastair's surname as **Law** — the only
place the surname appears; the mailing-list archive records him by first name
only. It was imported to this bundle and saved at
`source-youtube/alastair-law-ayrs-meeting-may-2020.md`. Because the transcript
is machine-generated, its punctuation, speaker attribution, and some proper
nouns are approximate; wording is cited as paraphrase unless clearly verbatim,
and the meeting is cited in the past tense per the bundle's historical-archive
convention.

The recording opens with general AYRS meeting business and member
introductions, including an American designer's wing-sail craft destroyed in an
Oklahoma tornado and other members' self-steering, wing-sail, and
electric-drive projects — material outside the Paradox scope and **not ingested
here**. Only Alastair's Paradox presentation and the Q&A that followed it are
drawn on.
