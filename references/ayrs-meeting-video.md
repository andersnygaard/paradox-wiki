# AYRS meeting talk — Alastair on "Little Jim" (May 2020)

In **May 2020**, a talk on the Paradox "[Little Jim](/boats/little-jim.md)" was given at an online meeting of the **Amateur Yacht Research Society (AYRS)**, held over Zoom during the COVID-19 lockdown [1]. A recording was posted to YouTube and transcribed.

Unlike the [mailing-list archive](/references/mailing-list.md) (contemporaneous 2001–2004 email), this is a **retrospective, first-person primary source** — the same builder describing the same boat some **seventeen years after her launch**, with the perspective of long ownership [1]. It is firsthand authority: "Little Jim" was built single-handed and cruised extensively, so the account of her handling, stability, and rig is treated as the builder's own evidence for the boat [1]. Where it overlaps the archive it largely **corroborates** the earlier record; where it adds detail (a spring-balance masthead righting-moment test, the seat's position near the centre of pitch rotation, long-run yuloh cruising speed, and the boat's later ballast state) it is recorded as a new data point, dated 2020 [1].

The talk also independently confirms basic provenance from outside the mailing list: the designer is named as **"Matt Leyden"** (Matt Layden), "an amateur designer" who "lives in Florida" and built the original Paradox to "sail to the Bahamas and stay there for a couple of months" [1].

## Provenance and dating

The source is a YouTube auto-generated **transcript** of the meeting recording, titled *"Alastair Law – Amateur Yacht Research Society (AYRS) meeting May 2020."* The uploader's title gives a surname, **Law**, which the mailing-list archive never records — it knows the builder by first name only. The surname is corroborated in print by two independent magazine appearances; see ["Sailing The Paradox" — DCA Bulletin 193](/references/dca-sailing-the-paradox.md) and [Alastair Law](/people/alastair-law.md). Those printed sources spell the forename "Alistair," against the "Alastair" of the builder's own mail account and sign-offs; the transcript's spelling here carries no weight either way, being machine-generated. Because the transcript is machine-generated, its punctuation, speaker attribution, and some proper nouns are approximate; wording is cited as paraphrase unless clearly verbatim, and the meeting is cited in the past tense per the bundle's historical-archive convention.

The recording opens with general AYRS meeting business and member introductions, including an American designer's wing-sail craft destroyed in an Oklahoma tornado and other members' self-steering, wing-sail, and electric-drive projects — material outside the Paradox scope and **not covered here**. Only the Paradox presentation and the Q&A that followed it are drawn on.
