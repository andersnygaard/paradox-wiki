# "Sailing The Paradox" — DCA Bulletin 193

A signed article, **"Sailing The Paradox,"** subtitled *"The owner's experiences of sailing Little Jim,"* appeared across pages 48–49 of ***Bulletin* 193** of the **Dinghy Cruising Association**, written by the builder of the UK Paradox "[Little Jim](/boats/little-jim.md)."[1] It is a deliberate, structured appraisal rather than a cruising narrative: after a short preamble the piece divides into two lists — **fifteen advantages and eleven disadvantages** — offered "in no particular order," with the stated aim of being "completely impartial" and of helping "anyone considering building one of these remarkable boats."[1]

This makes it a distinct kind of source from the rest of the record. The [mailing-list archive](/references/mailing-list.md) captures the same builder's views as they formed, scattered across hundreds of messages answering other people's questions; the [AYRS talk](/references/ayrs-meeting-video.md) is a retrospective delivered to a research society seventeen years on. This article sits between them — a **considered, edited, published summary** compiled while the boat was still comparatively new, and the only place the design's drawbacks are set out as a deliberate itemized list by someone who had built and cruised one.[1]

## Provenance and dating

The source is a two-page PDF held locally at `source-articles/dinghy-cruising-association/sailing_the_paradox_alastair_law.pdf`, with a transcription at `source-articles/dinghy-cruising-association/sailing-the-paradox/index.md`. Both pages carry the running foot **"DCA Bulletin 193 : 48"** and **": 49"**.

The *Bulletin* issue is **not dated on the extracted pages**, and no cover or masthead was captured. The article's own opening — "three seasons" of sailing — dates the writing to roughly **three seasons after the April 2003 launch**, so most likely **2005 or 2006**; this is an inference from internal evidence, not a stated date, and is recorded as such.[1] An editorial footnote refers the reader to "Mike Knott's letter p.12" in the same issue, which was not captured.[1]

The byline prints the forename as **"Alistair."** The archive treats this as a publication-side variant: the same sender's own mail account carries the display name "Alastair" across more than three hundred messages, and every archive message signs off "Al, Yeovil, England." The surname **Law** is confirmed here in print, independently of the AYRS talk and of *[Messing About in Boats](/references/messing-about-in-boats.md)*; see [Alastair Law](/people/alastair-law.md) for the full naming evidence.

## The reproduced sail plan

Page 49 carries, below the article text, an editorially added reproduction of **Matt Layden's Paradox sail plan** — hand-lettered "SAIL PLAN / PARADOX / 4.8m COASTAL CRUISER / MATT LAYDEN · 7/94 · DWG #1," scale 1:20.[2] The editor's note sources it to `http://home.triad.rr.com/lcruise/plans1.htm` and marks it as included "for reference," so it is **not** part of the owner's own submission.[2] Its specification block is reproduced in the transcription; the scan is low-resolution and hand-lettered, so several imperial figures are best-effort reads and the metric values are the more reliable of the two columns.[2] The one figure that matters most — **640 kg / 1400 lb displacement** — is legible and agrees with the article text.[1][2]

Note the drawing's own "4.8m" label against an LOA the archive elsewhere records as 4.2 m; the sail-plan sheet is a **1994 drawing**, and this discrepancy is inherited from the source drawing rather than introduced by the *Bulletin*. It is flagged here rather than silently reconciled.
