# Builders and Owners of Paradox Sailboats (2014 census)

A **29-boat table of Paradox builders and owners**, titled "Builders and Owners of Paradox Sailboats," is the most complete single census of the design in the archive.[1] It was first compiled by Bill Serjeant in 2006 for his Small Sailboats website, when 29 Paradoxes were known to him, and republished on [Bill's Log](/references/bills-log.md) on 1 October 2014 with two updates noted in the accompanying text: two of the builders had since died (Don Elliott and Steve Yahn), and a few boats had changed hands, "'Minnow' being one of them."[1]

## Provenance, and the earlier UK/worldwide registry

This table is a later snapshot of the same running database documented at [Small Sailboats / Paradox-UK websites](/references/paradox-uk-website.md) — the `www.smallsailboats.co.uk/paradox/names.htm` registry that page traces from its 2004 count (at least three further completed boats beyond those already listed) through the 2006 "database renewal" described there, always dependent on builders reporting their own boats in to keep it current.[1] The 2014 post is the fullest surviving publication of that registry, seven-plus years after the last renewal documented on the mailing list, and stands as its direct successor rather than a fresh count.

## Not a fully refreshed count: read it as a 2006 base layer with a few named corrections

The table is not a uniformly current-to-2014 snapshot. Its own introductory text names only two categories of update — two deaths and "a few boats," of which only "Minnow" is specified — which implies the remaining entries carry forward largely unchanged from the 2006 original. This shows in the table itself: it still lists "Bill Serjeant | Faith," although [Faith](/boats/faith.md) had already been sold to a new owner, Jim, by 2013, a fact recorded three weeks later in the companion post below and not reflected here.[1][2] Any single entry's "Country/City" or "Comments" column should therefore be read as accurate as of roughly 2006–2007 unless independently corroborated by a more recent source, rather than assumed current to the October 2014 publication date.

## Geographic spread

The table's 29 boats span at least **ten countries across five continents**: the UK (Yeovil, Southend, London, Cheltenham), the US (Wisconsin, Providence RI, Denver, Kansas City, Portland/Oregon, Venice CA, and several unspecified), Australia (Brisbane and elsewhere), Sweden (Sala and elsewhere), Norway (Trondheim), New Zealand, Taiwan (Taipei), Canada (Montreal), and the Netherlands (Amsterdam).[1] Four more builders and boats named in the post's prose, but not in the table itself, extend that spread further: **Lezlie Henson** built "Tardis," later bought and renamed "Scout" by **Sean Mulligan**; **Mike Beebe** owned an unnamed Paradox believed to have since changed hands; **Geoff Davis**, of Fremantle, Western Australia, built "[Small Fry](/boats/small-fry.md)" and rounded Cape Leeuwin, continuing for several days without making landfall; and **Alberto**, of Rawson, Argentina, built "Cachivache," called on the post "the most southerly Paradox."[1] With Argentina added, the count reaches at least six continents' worth of Paradoxes if North and South America are counted separately — Africa and Antarctica remain the only unrepresented continents on record anywhere in this archive.

## A self-reported floor, not a complete count

Both this table and its predecessor registry depend entirely on builders volunteering their own information, and both name that limitation openly. The 2014 post closes on the same note: "Hundreds of plans have been sold, but nowhere near as many boats would appear to have been built, probably because the costs are high, both in terms of expenditure and of effort. Some potential builders lack facilities and others believe their skill level is not up to the mark; others cannot find the time."[1] Twenty-nine boats in the table, plus four more named outside it, is therefore a documented **floor** on the worldwide Paradox count, not a ceiling — consistent with the earlier registry's own recorded doubt that anyone but the plans' seller could ever know the true number built.[2]

## What the census corroborates and extends elsewhere in this bundle

Several entries independently confirm or add to facts already established from other sources:

- **The "Enuf"/"Minnow" chain.** The table lists "Robert Biegler | Minnow | Modified | Norway Trondheim | Finished" — an independent confirmation that the boat was already named "Minnow" under [Robert Biegler](/people/robert-biegler.md)'s Norwegian ownership, before her 2013 sale to Bill Serjeant.[1] See [boats/enuf.md — second owner in Norway, and delivery to Bill Serjeant](/boats/enuf.md#second-owner-in-norway-and-delivery-to-bill-serjeant-2013), where this same table entry is already cited to date the rename to Biegler's ownership rather than Serjeant's.
- **A full name for "Steve (Kansas City)."** "Steve Henderson | Telestai | Per plan | US Kansas City | Building slowly" supplies both the surname and the boat name for the bundle's [Steve (Kansas City)](/people/steve-kc.md) page, which otherwise carries no boat name and identifies him only by first name and city from his 2004 mailing-list posts.[1]
- **"The Edge" as an accepted name, not just an inferred one.** [Don Elliott's Paradox](/boats/elliott-paradox.md) page notes its name was "never confirmed by Don Elliott himself but inferred from launch photographs" in 2003; the census independently uses "The Edge" as the boat's settled name in 2014, still without Don Elliott's own confirmation but a second, later, independent use of the same name.[1]
- **Two builders' deaths.** The table's headline update — Don Elliott and Steve Yahn "have since died" — corroborates [people/don-elliott.md](/people/don-elliott.md#after-his-death), which already records his death independently via a 2014 microcruising.com post.[1] Steve Yahn's reported death is not otherwise attested anywhere in this bundle; it is recorded here only as this single secondhand mention, not as independently confirmed fact.
- **Two unnamed boats stay unnamed.** Jimmy Lu's and Kjell Karlsson's table entries both carry a bare "?" for boat name, matching the bundle's own findings that [Jimmy Lu's Taiwan-built Paradox](/boats/taiwan-paradox.md) has no recorded name and that [Kjell Karlsson's BRIS-built Paradox](/boats/bris-paradox.md) carries only the informal nickname "Paraplastic," not a formal name.[1]
- **A name and an unresolved location discrepancy for Mark Waters.** The table gives his boat a name for the first time — "Raffles" — not recorded anywhere else in the bundle, where [Mark Waters](/people/mark-waters.md)'s Paradox was still unnamed as of the 2006 mailing-list record cited on his page.[1] The same table entry places him in "Oregon, Portland," where every other bundle source places him in Los Angeles, California; this is left unresolved here as a discrepancy rather than corrected, since nothing in the archive confirms whether it reflects a later move or simply an error carried over in the table.

## A companion census: "UK Matt Layden Paradoxes"

A companion post published three weeks earlier, "UK Matt Layden Paradoxes," gives ownership chains for the four Paradoxes then known in the UK, in age order: [Little Jim](/boats/little-jim.md), "Minnow" ([Enuf](/boats/enuf.md)), [Faith](/boats/faith.md), and [Johanna](/boats/johanna.md).[2] A trial sail aboard Little Jim at Plymouth is credited with the decision to build Faith at all — "I became fully convinced she was the boat for me. 'Faith' was conceived, and the rest is history" — and by this post's date Faith had already passed to a new owner, "Jim (not 'Little Jim')."[2] The full ownership chains — including Little Jim's and Johanna's roles in Minnow's first sail and her replacement trailer — are already cited in full on the boats' own pages ([boats/little-jim.md](/boats/little-jim.md), [boats/enuf.md](/boats/enuf.md), [boats/faith.md](/boats/faith.md), [boats/johanna.md](/boats/johanna.md)); this companion post is noted here only to connect the two 2014 censuses as a pair, one worldwide and one UK-specific, published three weeks apart on the same blog.

## Name variants preserved as given

The table spells the "Petite Lady" owner's name **"Hugh Miller."** A separate 2014 post on the same blog about the same boat spells it **"Hugh Millar."** Neither the boat nor her owner has a page yet in this bundle; both spellings are preserved here rather than silently resolved to one, pending further evidence. Steve Yahn's own boat page and this table diverge on a single letter — "Ironsides" (the bundle's page title, per his own mailing-list introduction) against the table's "Ironside" — reproduced above exactly as the census gives it.
