# Paradox builders mailing list

The primary community for Paradox builders and sailors, and the source archive
for this bundle.

# History

| Era | Group | Notes |
|-----|-------|-------|
| 2001– | `paradoxbuilders` (Yahoo Groups) | Founded 7 July 2001 by [Glen C. Maxwell](/people/glen-maxwell.md) to exchange building and sailing information. |
| later | `paradoxbuilders2` (Yahoo Groups) | Second Yahoo group, first proposed in March 2004 as a dedicated file-upload group once the original group's photo storage ran short — see [Glen Maxwell — a second group considered, and the first mention of a dedicated website](/people/glen-maxwell/list-founder-and-moderator.md#a-second-group-considered-and-the-first-mention-of-a-dedicated-website) for the proposal and Glen's initial response (archiving old files, building a separate website); actually founded on 10 May 2004 by [DC](/people/dc.md), acting moderator during Glen Maxwell's Atlantic crossing, once the storage limit was reached in earnest — see [the photo-storage limit, and the founding of paradoxbuilders2](#the-photo-storage-limit-and-the-founding-of-paradoxbuilders2) below. |
| 2019– | `paradoxsailboat` (groups.io) | Created 5 Nov 2019 by the list owner **"Mattsplatt"** (signing "Matt") to replace both Yahoo groups; content migrated over. |

The 2019 groups.io owner posted under the screen name **"Mattsplatt"** and
signed "Matt". This is **plausibly, but not confirmed, to be designer
[Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md)** — the identity is pending
corroborating evidence from the archive and is not stated here as established
fact.

The group's **Files area** is organised into folders — e.g. **construction**,
**stove**, and **Study Plans** — hosting construction photos and a
guide/description of the study plans (uploaded early on). The dedicated "stove"
folder points to a cabin stove being a fitted item on these boats. See
[Study Plans](/references/study-plans.md).

# Sibling and successor groups

Beyond the `paradoxbuilders`/`paradoxsailboat` lineage itself, two related but
separate Yahoo groups came up as cross-references for Paradox builders:

- **[Micro Cruising](/references/micro-cruising-group.md)** — a broader
  micro-cruising discussion group started by
  [Dave Bolduc](/people/bolduc/dave.md) around September 2001, which
  Paradox builders were repeatedly encouraged to also join; still an active
  cross-reference as late as April 2002.
- **Smallboats** (`http://groups.yahoo.com/group/Smallboats`) — a separate
  Yahoo group [Don Elliott](/people/don-elliott.md) redirected Paradox
  questions to after resigning from `paradoxbuilders` in October 2001,
  stating he would "not belong to any group that promotes unsafe
  practices." See
  [plywood grade and foam flotation debate](/construction/plywood-grades-and-foam-flotation.md)
  for the exchange that preceded this. Smallboats' scope, membership, and
  activity are not otherwise documented in this archive.

# The photo-storage limit, and the founding of paradoxbuilders2

`paradoxbuilders`' Yahoo Groups **photo storage limit** — first flagged as a
display-resolution problem in March 2004 (see [Glen Maxwell — a second group
considered](/people/glen-maxwell/list-founder-and-moderator.md#a-second-group-considered-and-the-first-mention-of-a-dedicated-website))
— was reached in earnest the following month, while founder
[Glen C. Maxwell](/people/glen-maxwell.md) was away on his Atlantic crossing
aboard "Hogfish Maximus" (see
[Matt Layden eyewitness — an Atlantic crossing](/people/glen-maxwell/matt-layden-eyewitness.md#an-atlantic-crossing-aboard-hogfish-maximus)).
New "Whisper" photos uploaded during this window triggered a recurring group
error that locked the uploader out of the photo section entirely, a problem
independently confirmed by another member the same day.

**[DC](/people/dc.md)**, filling the administrative gap in Glen's absence,
diagnosed the underlying cause: the group had hit its Yahoo-imposed storage
ceiling outright, rather than just a display-resolution limit as in March. As
an immediate step DC deleted his own large-format photos from the "Swash"
album, then founded a genuinely new group, **`paradoxbuilders2`**, on 10 May
2004, dedicated to photo and file storage overflow — asking members to keep
posting to `paradoxbuilders` itself and treat the new group purely as backup
capacity, so the discussion itself would not split across two groups, and
restricting the new group's photos and files to material specific to Paradox
and other Matt Layden designs. This settles when and by whom
`paradoxbuilders2` was founded: DC, acting in Glen's absence, once the
storage limit had gone from a nuisance to a hard block.

Even after the new group existed, the photo section remained inaccessible for
removing the offending pictures. Further investigation found that **deleting**,
rather than merely resizing, three photos was needed to bring the section back
under its limit — chosen for being the least Paradox-specific of the group's
content rather than for any fault of their own: a steel dinghy, a Bris rudder,
and a "water in my backyard" photo. With those removed, general access to the
photo section was restored. A division of labor followed: use
`paradoxbuilders2`'s **files** section (rather than its photos section) for
posting photos in folders, since the original group's files area had been kept
free of photos, while reserving the original group's own files section for
data.

With access restored, new "Whisper" photos continued over the following days —
first a further batch to `paradox2`, then a complete set of sailing photos to
`paradoxbuilders2` itself, taken with a chase boat to capture the boat "on all
points of sail."

A year later, the original group's files section again neared capacity,
raising the question of a `paradoxbuilders2`-style overflow group — answered
by the fact that `paradoxbuilders2` already existed, with its files section
still empty, confirming that DC's May 2004 overflow group had seen little
actual use for its intended purpose. The exchange also prompted encouragement
for [Kjell Karlsson](/people/kjell-karlsson.md) to keep his "ParaPlastic"
build photos coming (see [the "BRIS Paradox" — window test-fitting in
polycarbonate](/boats/bris-paradox.md#window-test-fitting-in-polycarbonate)).

# List culture

Not every post is technical. The list's lighter, community-building side
includes a running joke on the design's own name: a suggestion that any
future Paradox owner name their boat with a visual pun — "two ducks,"
rendered artistically as "Pair a Ducks." The joke drew further wordplay from
the wider small-boat community, including a plan (from the PDRacer plywood
dinghy racing scene) to build two PDRacers joined by a "brick joiner"/"grout
unit," making a literal "pair of ducks," alongside an existing
PDRacer-as-Paradox mashup nicknamed "PuddleDox" (and a punning "West Wight
Puddle," after the West Wight Potter).

The list also fielded occasional comparisons to other small-craft designs.
Against Jim Michalak's "AF3" — a similarly beach-landable design offering
more interior space, including room for a dog — the judgment on record is
that the AF3 would not be as stable as a Paradox, though it might suit lying
out in the sun better; a capsize-recovery account for the AF3 was separately
linked for context. Weighed against that comparison, a Paradox has been
judged the right boat on the strength of others' experience, but still too
small for some, prompting the suggestion of a small catamaran as an
alternative.

# Spam and moderation

By late September 2002 the list had absorbed six separate bare-link "spam"
postings from a rotating cast of one-off handles (see
[Glen C. Maxwell — the "spammed" pictures thread](/people/glen-maxwell/list-founder-and-moderator.md)).
The structural fix adopted — the group's first, rather than an
after-the-fact "that's spam, ignore it" reaction — was to require
**moderator approval** before a new member could post, since automated
scripts let spammers sign up as Yahoo Groups members purely to post spam.

[Glen C. Maxwell](/people/glen-maxwell.md), the list's founder and sole
moderator at the time, judged the approval requirement workable but harder
to run alone, given an upcoming couple of months away sailing once the
weather cooled, and asked for a **second or third volunteer moderator**.
Reading posts directly on the Yahoo Groups website instead of by individual
e-mail was floated and dropped — the advertising already appended to list
e-mails was "bad enough" without "every 3rd page fetch being an advert on
yahoo itself" — leaving the **daily digest** format as the softer interim
step. Sentiment otherwise favored keeping the group open to new members
over closing it, on the reasoning that this lets each member choose their
own coping strategy (digest, or simply ignoring spam and using the sender's
address as a clue); no alternative to the moderator-approval fix was
proposed. The one concrete **anti-spam mechanism** to emerge distinct from
moderator approval: forward spam e-mails, with all headers included
(Outlook users must check a message's "properties" to get them), to
**`abuse@...`** at the sending network — abuse desks are "responsive" even
if they "can't always do anything," and can only act "if they are
informed."

The moderator-approval proposal was adopted within 24 hours, with
**[Dave Bolduc](/people/bolduc/dave.md)** and **[Paul V.](/people/paul-v.md)**
volunteering as co-moderators alongside Glen Maxwell. With the group now
covered most of the time, closing it to require new-member applications
became possible, expected to eliminate the unwanted spam. A further
volunteer, David LeBlanc, was added as a moderator once Glen left on a
planned winter sailing trip with Dave Bolduc.
