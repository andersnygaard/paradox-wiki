# Lezlie's World (Lezlie Henson's blog)

**Lezlie's World** (`https://lezliesworld.blogspot.com/`) is the personal Blogger diary of **Lezlie Henson**, who signs her posts only "Lezlie." Its Paradox content is a running series documenting the build of one boat: **"Tardis,"** a stock [Paradox](/paradox.md) she built in the Houston, Texas area and named after the time machine in *Doctor Who*. It is the **authored, first-person primary source** behind the boat the archive otherwise knows only through second-hand reports — the same hull Sean Mulligan later bought, renamed, and made well-known as **"[Scout](/boats/scout.md)."**

That is what makes it worth holding: everything the bundle previously carried about the boat's construction came from a UK observer, **Bill Serjeant**, reporting at second hand that she was "built to a high standard" but left unfinished when offered for sale.[1] Henson's own diary is the source underneath that report — it records how she built her, plank by plank, and why she let her go before ever sailing her.

## What the blog is worth as evidence

Its value is that it is **specific and contemporaneous about a build the archive had only heard summarised**. It documents, in the builder's own account and photographs, a first-time amateur working through a full stock Paradox: the insulation method, the lead-pig and water-tank ballast, a home-made gimbaled stove, the copper ground plate, the paint and non-skid, and the trailer.[3][4][5][6][7][8] It also settles the human facts the second-hand record could only gesture at — that she **never sailed the boat**, and that she sold her **almost finished** because a marital separation drained the project of its purpose.[9] And it independently corroborates Bill Serjeant's dating: her January 2013 post noting sudden UK interest in buying "Tardis" is the same event, from the seller's side, as the UK enthusiast's simultaneous shipping-cost estimate.[10][1]

Its limits should be read with that. It is one first-time builder's diary, light on numbers — dimensions, weights, and ballast figures are quoted from the plans rather than measured, and the boat is a **stock** Paradox, so the blog adds construction narrative and provenance rather than design data. And because Henson sold the boat unlaunched, her blog carries **no sailing record at all**; everything the archive knows of how this hull sails comes from her successor's ownership as "Scout," documented separately.[1]

## Provenance, dating, and mirror

The blog was live and public at retrieval. It is a conventional dated-post Blogger site; the TARDIS build runs across roughly nineteen posts from **9 January 2011** (first dry-fit) to **14 September 2013** (the boat's launch, under new ownership, as "Scout"). Her later, unrelated sailing-trip posts are not part of this record.

Retrieved and mirrored for this bundle on **2026-07-23**, held under `source-web/lezlie-henson/` as one Markdown transcript per post (verbatim text, images repointed to local files), the **raw HTML** of each post, and the posts' **photographs** downloaded locally. Three photographs in the 10 April 2011 guest post had already been deleted from Blogger's servers at retrieval and could not be recovered; their absence is noted in that transcript.

## The build, in outline

- **Naming (January 2011).** The boat is christened **"Tardis"** after the *Doctor Who* time machine — "bigger on the inside than it is on the outside."[2]
- **Hull (early 2011).** First dry-fit of the bulkheads in January; the bottom glued on in March, making her "officially a boat"; built to the plans plus **Don Elliott's** build manual, with questions worked out on the **Paradox Yahoo group**.[3][11]
- **Fit-out (2011).** Insulation cut to paper patterns and covered with 5 mm ply screwed to epoxied dowel pegs; lead "pig" ballast (the plans' **400 lb**) in bolt-down "pens" plus water-tank ballast plumbed by her husband **Dale**, an electrical engineer, with 3-way valves; a **home-made gimbaled stove** modelled on **Glen Maxwell's** for "[Zoë](/boats/zoe.md)"; a copper-flashing ground plate bedded over woven roving.[4][5][6][7]
- **Finish (2012).** Painted Glidden Porch & Floor off-white with a navy stripe, spars and trim left bright under varnish; non-skid decks; a trailer modified to fit; the lead ballast installed.[8]
- **Sold, unlaunched (February 2013).** Offered for sale almost finished and sold to a buyer in Arizona; Henson **never sailed her**.[9]
- **Launched as "Scout" (September 2013).** The new owner, **Sean Mulligan**, launched and renamed the boat, which Henson followed from his blog.[12]

The full extraction of the boat is at [Scout](/boats/scout.md), and of the builder at [Lezlie Henson](/people/lezlie-henson.md).
