# Amateur Boat Builders' Association (ABBA) Newsletter (Western Australia)

The **Amateur Boat Builders' Association** is a home-boatbuilding club based in **Perth, Western Australia**, meeting at the South of Perth Yacht Club and publishing a bimonthly newsletter that writes up each meeting's presentation.[6] Its **April/May 2021** issue devotes its lead feature to a Paradox — **"Building of the Paradox Micro Cruiser - Planktos,"** a write-up of the April meeting's presentation by builder Roselt Croeser covering both the four-year build and the shakedown cruise to Shark Bay.[1]

The issue matters to the archive out of proportion to its length, for two reasons. It is the only source on record describing a **junk-rig Paradox that was actually built, launched, and cruised** — the mailing-list archive argues the junk rig only in the abstract, and the one other built example, [Eerik Mee](/people/eerik-mee.md)'s purpose-designed [THU](/boats/thu.md), was day-sailed on a lake rather than cruised (see [junk rig](/design/ideas/junk-rig.md)). And it reproduces Matt Layden's **1994 sail-plan drawing** at a legibility the archive's other copy of the same sheet does not reach, settling a dimension the record had been carrying as an open discrepancy.

It is also a **late** source. The mailing-list archive runs from 2001; this build ran **2013–2016** and was presented in **2021**, so it stands well outside the list era and records the design being built from plans by someone who was never a list correspondent.

## The sail-plan reproduction, and the 4.2 m / 4.8 m discrepancy

The feature reproduces the hand-lettered **"SAIL PLAN / PARADOX / 4.2m COASTAL CRUISER / MATT LAYDEN · 7/94 · DWG #1,"** scale 1:20, with its specification block intact.[5] The scan is clean enough to read directly, and gives: **LOA 4.2 m (13'-10"), LWL 4.1 m (13'-5"), beam 1.23 m (4'-0½"), draft 23 cm (9"), sail area 9.3 m² (100 ft²), displacement to DWL in sailing trim 640 kg (1400 lb), prismatic .56, disp./LWL 270.**[5]

This is the **same drawing sheet** — same title, same date, same drawing number — that the *[DCA Bulletin](/references/dca-sailing-the-paradox.md)* reproduced, where the low-resolution scan was read as **"4.8m" / LOA 4.8 m (15'-10")**. Every other figure in the two specification blocks agrees exactly. The ABBA copy's **4.2 m** is the better-supported reading: it is legible rather than best-effort, it matches the **4.2 m** LOA the archive records elsewhere, and it sits consistently against the shared **LWL 4.1 m** — a 10 cm overhang, as a near-plumb-ended flat-bottomed hull would have, where 4.8 m would imply 70 cm. The DCA reading is therefore most likely a scan misread carried through both the metric and imperial columns. This is recorded here rather than silently corrected in the DCA page's own citation.

## What the Paradox feature covers

The write-up is organised as the presentation was, in short bulleted sections rather than continuous prose: a summary of the design's origins and characteristics, the build process and its timeline, costs and conclusions, a pros-and-cons assessment of the boat as built, and a closing list of prospective longer voyages.[1][2][3][4] Thirty of the issue's photographs belong to the feature, running from hull panels laid out flat, through bulkhead assembly, epoxy coating, deck and cabin-trunk fit-out, to the launched boat under junk sail and at anchor and dried out on the sand at Shark Bay.[1]

The full extraction of the build, costs, systems, and sailing assessment is carried on the boat's own page at [Planktos](/boats/planktos.md), and the builder's record at [Roselt Croeser](/people/roselt-croeser.md).

## Earlier issues not held

The feature refers readers to the association's **June–July 2014** and **April–May 2015** newsletters, in the "Library" tab of its website, for the builder's earlier projects.[1] Neither issue is held in the archive, and neither has been checked for further Paradox content; both are noted as outstanding.

## Provenance and what is held

The issue is held locally as the original PDF at `source-articles/newsletters/abba-2021-04/source.pdf`, downloaded from `https://abba.org.au/pdf/202104-abba-newsletter-apr-may-2021.pdf`, with a full markdown transcription at `source-articles/newsletters/abba-2021-04/index.md` and all **39 photographs and drawings** extracted to `source-articles/newsletters/abba-2021-04/images/` as `fig-01` … `fig-39` in reading order.

The issue runs to thirteen pages and is **not** wholly about Paradox: a second feature covers electric propulsion in two unrelated boats, and the remainder is association administration — committee list, forthcoming meetings, and a clothing-logo notice.[6] Only the lead feature and the sail-plan reproduction bear on the archive.
