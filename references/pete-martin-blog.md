# Pete Martin's blog (paradoxbuild.webs.com)

**Pete Martin's blog** (`https://paradoxbuild.webs.com/`, titled "My Paradox *Johanna*") is a build log of one boat: **"[JoHanna](/boats/johanna.md),"** a [Paradox](/paradox.md) built to plans in **Cheltenham, England** from **1 December 2006** and launched on **11 June 2011**. It is an **authored, first-person primary source** by the builder, [Pete Martin](/people/pete-martin.md).

Its particular value is that it **finishes the story the mailing list leaves open.** Pete's build is documented in exhaustive detail on the [paradoxbuilders](/references/mailing-list.md) list, but his own posting there thins out through 2008 and the list record leaves JoHanna "still in build" that September; the next fixed point is a 2014 note, via [Bill's Log](/references/bills-log.md), that she was by then finished and sailing. The blog spans that gap and closes it precisely — carrying the build through sheathing, fit-out, and painting from 2008 to 2011, and recording the **launch date and the first sail** that no other source in the archive supplies.

## What the blog is worth as evidence

It is **contemporaneous and builder-authored**: entries were posted as the work happened, dated, and illustrated, so the last three years of the build — the part the list does not cover — survive as first-hand record rather than recollection. Against the mailing-list account it is **complementary, not duplicative**: the list holds Pete's questions and the answers they drew (ballast, epoxy choice, vent-trunk drain sizing, hull-gluing sequence — all already extracted to [boats/johanna.md](/boats/johanna.md) and [people/pete-martin.md](/people/pete-martin.md)), while the blog holds the later solo work he no longer posted to the list, and the launch.

Its limits should be read with that. It is one builder's account of one boat, and it is a **build blog, not a cruising log** — it ends within weeks of launch, pointing sailing readers to a separate "Paradoxically Speaking" blog (not captured here), so it documents that JoHanna reached the water and sailed, but almost nothing of how she sailed afterward. And the **capture is incomplete**: two blog pages spanning roughly **August 2007 → October 2008** were not among the pages saved, so the build record has a gap across that stretch (the mailing-list account partly covers it).

## Provenance and dating

The blog was live and public at retrieval. Its build record runs **1 December 2006 → 25 July 2011**, reverse-chronological across paginated "Building Blog" pages, plus a static **Home** page.

Retrieved and mirrored for this bundle on **2026-07-23**, held under `source-web/pete-martin/` as **six full-page screenshots** saved with FireShot (captures 110–117), each a single tall PDF with no text layer. Because FireShot flattens the page, the pages were extracted two ways: the native-resolution image strips to `source-web/pete-martin/pages/`, and a faithful visual **transcription** of each page to `source-web/pete-martin/md/`, with every photograph marked inline as a placeholder against the strip it sits on. The two absent pages are FireShot captures 114–115; the `README.md` in that folder records the gap and the page-by-page date map.[1]

## The build, in outline

Ordered by content date, the pages carry:

- **Build start (Dec 2006 → Jun 2007).** Deck-beam jig, stem (with the miscut half-width stem confessed here as on the list), side-panel scarfs, and the sheer and chine strakes — the boat first "looking a bit like a boat."[3]
- **Hull assembly (Jun → Aug 2007).** Chine and sheer strakes, the vent trunk, dry-assembly and the wet glue-up of the hull — a stretch also flooded out that summer.[4]
- *(Gap, ~Aug 2007 → Oct 2008 — pages not captured.)*
- **Sheathing (Oct 2008 → Jul 2009).** Bottom sheathed in two layers of 450 g/m² biaxial cloth, topsides in lighter cloth, with the hull turned on a rolling frame between coats — a "Paradox Rollover" video posted showing the home-built turning frame.[5]
- **Fit-out and paint (Sep 2009 → Sep 2010).** Rudder leaded and sheathed, decks, and a long, weather-beaten painting campaign that overran a hoped-for September 2010 launch.[6]
- **Completion and launch (Jan → Jul 2011).** Final fit-out; **JoHanna launched on the evening of 11 June 2011**, slipping off her trailer and sailing in a near-calm on her first outing, with a launch-day photo set from Adrian Gingell and Alastair Law; the building blog then closed, sailing moving to a separate "Paradoxically Speaking" blog.[7]

## The home page as a reference hub

The static Home page is the richest single page and doubles as a small Paradox reference: the boat's design specification drawing (the standard 13'10" Paradox figures), a plain-language chine-runner explainer, plan and building-manual contacts ([Dave Bolduc](/people/bolduc.md); [Don Elliott](/people/don-elliott.md)'s manual), a link list to other Paradox builders ([Little Jim](/boats/little-jim.md), [Zoë](/boats/zoe.md), [Jimmy Lu](/people/jimmy-lu.md)'s Taiwan build, and others), and podcast notes. It also **reproduces [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md)'s May/June 2009 *Small Craft Advisor* "Enigma" interview in full** — the same interview the archive draws on for the [chine-runners](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md) pages from the magazine itself, so it is not a new source but a convenient second copy of one already held.[2]

## Relationship to the mailing-list record

The blog and the list overlap on the 2006–2008 build but are cited for different things. Where the list already records a build detail through Pete's own posts, that detail stays cited to the list on [boats/johanna.md](/boats/johanna.md) and [people/pete-martin.md](/people/pete-martin.md); the blog is cited where it adds what the list does not — chiefly the 2009–2011 completion and the launch. One spelling note carried over from the blog: its Home page styles fellow UK builder [Bill Serjeant](/people/bill-serjeant.md)'s name "Sergeant" and his boat "[Faith](/boats/faith.md)" — the bundle's canonical spelling is **Serjeant**, and the variant should not spawn a duplicate person.
