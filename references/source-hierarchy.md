# How this bundle weighs its sources

This archive draws on three kinds of source, and they do not carry equal weight. When a claim from one tier conflicts with a claim from another, the higher tier is the specification and the lower tier is a builder's departure from it — not an equal alternative.

**Tier 1 — the plans, and Matt Layden himself.** [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md) designed the boat, and his [building plans](/references/building-plans.md) are the specification a builder is following. Anything traceable directly to Matt — a plan sheet, a quoted plans note, a mailing-list post in his own name, a direct designer interview — carries this weight even when it reaches the archive secondhand (relayed by another builder), provided the relay is clearly marked as his. A builder's guess about what Matt "probably" meant, or a secondhand account of something Matt supposedly said with no clear chain back to him, is not Tier 1 — see [larger Paradox](/design/larger-paradox.md#how-far-the-designers-sanction-actually-reaches-and-what-the-record-does-not-settle) for a worked example of telling these apart.

**Tier 2 — Don Elliott's "Building Paradox" guide.** [Don Elliott](/people/don-elliott.md)'s sixteen-part construction series (see [the series content](/references/building-paradox-series.md) and [the articles as a product](/references/building-the-paradox-articles.md)) is a builder's guide, not the designer's own word — Don built one of the first Paradoxes and documented the plans faithfully, and held that any deviation should be cleared with Matt first. Where the guide states a technique the plans leave open, it is the next-best word after the plans themselves. Where an outside expert publicly corrected part of it (see [reception and the epoxy-accuracy controversy](/references/building-paradox-series.md#reception-and-the-epoxy-accuracy-controversy)), that correction is noted and the guide's claim is not treated as settled on that specific point.

**Tier 3 — everything else.** The mailing-list archive, microcruising.com, Bill's Log, magazine reader letters and reception, and every other builder's own choices, memories, and opinions. This is the bulk of the bundle's content and its greatest strength — hundreds of builders' real trade-offs, failures, and fixes — but it is builder discretion and secondhand memory, not specification. Strong, repeated, independent agreement across many Tier-3 builders is worth recording as consensus practice, but it does not outrank a clear Tier-1 or Tier-2 statement on the same point.

## How a contradiction should read

When an article records a builder doing something other than what the plans call for, state the plans' specification first, plainly, as the baseline — then present the builder's practice as a **documented deviation**, not a coordinate option. Say why the deviation was made (cost, taste, a local material shortage, a considered engineering judgment) so the reader can weigh it, but do not let the prose imply the two are simply two equal ways to build a Paradox. Where builders have deviated for good, converging, independently-reached reasons — see the woven-roving-versus-biaxial-cloth substitution at [hull planking](/construction/hull-planking.md#woven-roving-versus-biaxial-cloth-what-the-laminating-schedule-is-for) — say so explicitly, because that is a case where Tier-3 consensus has a real engineering argument behind it, not mere preference. Where a Tier-3 claim simply contradicts Tier 1 with no engineering case (a rumor, a guess, an unverified memory), say so plainly and do not stage it as an open question.

This bundle already does this well in places — see [how chine runners work](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md#a-uk-owners-sine-wave-hypothesis-and-a-measured-heelwinddisplacement-optimum) and [larger Paradox](/design/larger-paradox.md#how-far-the-designers-sanction-actually-reaches-and-what-the-record-does-not-settle) for two fully-worked examples. The aim of this page is to make that existing good practice a named, citable convention rather than an occasional individual judgment call.

A separate question — whether it is legitimate to *depart* from the stock design at all — is a genuine, unsettled debate the bundle deliberately leaves open (see [Paradox](/paradox.md)); it is about builder philosophy, not evidence, and this page's tier ranking is not meant to settle it.
