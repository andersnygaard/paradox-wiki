# Hogfish Design (Chris Morejohn's blog)

**Hogfish Design** (`https://hogfishdesign.wordpress.com/`) is the design blog of [Chris Morejohn](/people/chris-morejohn.md), the Florida Keys boatbuilder and liveaboard cruiser in the wider small-boat circle around [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md) and the [Bolducs](/people/bolduc/dave.md). It is an **authored, first-person primary source** — a designer publishing his own boats and reasoning — and is of interest to this archive because Morejohn sailed [Paradox](/paradox.md) first-hand in the early 1990s and returns to it repeatedly as a reference point in his own designs.

## Why it matters to Paradox

The blog is where Morejohn set down two small **Paradox-inspired minimal-cruiser concepts**, each treated here as a design in its own right:

- ["16′ minimal cruiser"](/similar-boat-designs/morejohn-16-minimal-cruiser.md) (2016) — his idea of the smallest boat that works well at sea, adding a ballasted daggerboard, an exterior cockpit, a lowerable mast, and a mizzen for heaving-to.
- ["MULLUSK 16"](/similar-boat-designs/morejohn-mullusk-16.md) (2017) — a slightly longer take on Paradox keeping Layden's roller-furling lug rig, with a ballasted keel and deck seating.

These sit alongside the blog's coverage of his larger flat-bottomed liveaboard sharpies — the "Hogfish" family whose ocean-crossing member is documented at ["Hogfish Maximus"](/similar-boat-designs/hogfish-maximus.md). Because the site is the designer's own words, it is treated as high-quality primary evidence for what Morejohn intended and how he relates his designs to Layden's, while any clearly-labeled opinions are recorded as opinion rather than fact.

## Citing this source

Individual posts are cited in the form:

> [Hogfish Design blog](/references/hogfish-design-website.md) — "Post title," Chris Morejohn, YYYY-MM-DD — [detail].

The address is reconstructed from the identifier: `https://hogfishdesign.wordpress.com/YYYY/MM/DD/slug/`.

The two Paradox-relevant posts resolve at:

- "16′ minimal cruiser" (2016-01-23) — `https://hogfishdesign.wordpress.com/2016/01/23/16-minimal-cruiser/`
- "MULLUSK 16" (2017-05-15) — `https://hogfishdesign.wordpress.com/2017/05/15/mullusk-16/`

They are drawn on for a thorough factual extraction of each design (dimensions, rig, ballast, and the stated relationship to Paradox) rather than a reproduction of the posts' full text; the live blog URLs remain the primary citation and should be consulted for the original wording, drawings, and photographs.
