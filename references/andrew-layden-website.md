# Andrew Layden's BGSU website (physics.bgsu.edu/~layden)

**`physics.bgsu.edu/~layden`** is the personal academic homepage of
**Andrew ("Andy") C. Layden**, an astronomer in the Department of Physics &
Astronomy at **Bowling Green State University** (Bowling Green, Ohio) — and, in
his own words on the site, the **brother of [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md)**,
designer of the [Paradox](/paradox.md). Most of the site is Andy's own
astronomy teaching and research material, but its **"Family and Fun Stuff"**
branch hosts a small set of first-person pages about Matt's boats and the
Layden family that are cited elsewhere in this bundle.

This is the site the archive repeatedly calls "**Matt's brother's website**":
it is the source that documents the construction of Matt's boats "in full"
(see [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md)), and the external
construction-photo host that [microcruising.com](/references/microcruising-website.md)
links from its own [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md) page. Identifying `~layden`
as Andy Layden's site resolves an open question flagged on the Enigma page,
where the bare `physics.bgsu.edu/~layden/...` link was noted as
**not** evidence of any physics-department affiliation for Matt himself — the
page belongs to his brother, the astronomer, not to Matt.

## What kind of source this is

Like [microcruising.com](/references/microcruising-website.md) and
[yrvind.com](/references/yrvind-website.md), this is an **authored, first-person
primary source**, not a mailing-list relay. Two distinct voices appear on it:

- **Andy Layden's own narration** about his brother's boats — the family
  relationship, the build logs, the boat index — written by someone with direct
  family knowledge of Matt.
- **Matt Layden's own first-person voice**, on the "Rob Roy-oid" canoe page,
  which is signed "Matt" and written in the first person. This is a **second,
  independent instance of Matt's own writing** distinct from the single
  first-person post in the mailing-list archive (his May 2003 "New Paradox Sail"
  message — see [biography](/matt-layden/biography.md#distinguishing-matts-own-words-from-what-others-say-about-him)),
  and it comes from a different source entirely.

## Provenance and dating

The site is a **static historical snapshot**. Its pages were authored with
Claris Home Page 3.0 and carry their own "last updated" dates: the homepage and
family/genealogy pages date to **2001**, and the Matt-boat pages to **2005–2007**
(the "Matt's Boats" index and Sand Flea page are stamped **March 2007 ~ ACL** —
Andrew C. Layden's initials). It was **retrieved for this bundle on 2026-07-05**
from the Wayback Machine (CDX enumeration plus raw `{timestamp}id_/` fetches, no
Wayback toolbar) and mirrored locally under `source-web/andrew-layden/` —
**166 files, ~19 MB**. Only the homepage and the **`FunStuff/`** branch were
mirrored; roughly a thousand astronomy teaching files under `~layden/` (course
directories, variable-star project pages, animations, videos, tarballs) were
deliberately skipped as unrelated to Paradox. Consistent with the bundle's
historical-archive convention, the site is cited in the past tense and dated
where a page gives a date.

## Site structure (what lives where)

- **`index.html`** — Andy Layden's homepage: "Assistant Professor," Department
  of Physics & Astronomy, BGSU, 112 Overman, `layden@baade.bgsu.edu`, with a
  link out to **"Family and Fun Stuff."**
- **`fun_links.htm`** — the "Fun Links" gateway: **Family**, **Boats**,
  travel photos, and "Chucky the Astro-Gnomer" (a humor subsite), plus
  assorted external links.
- **`FunStuff/family.htm`** — "The Layden Family": Andy's household (spouse Kim
  (Napper) Layden, son Shawn Layden, the family beagle) and a hedged
  **"Possible geneological connections"** link (see below).
- **`FunStuff/Boats/Matt_Boat/`** — the Paradox-relevant branch: Andy's index
  of Matt's boats plus per-boat build logs and photo galleries (72 files).

## The brother relationship — established in Andy's own words

The most important fact this site carries is the **family relationship**,
stated by Andy directly and independently of the Paradox Facebook group or
mailing list. Both his boat pages open with the same sentence:

> "My brother Matt (Layden) has built a number of small boats in his day."[4][5]

This is material-sourced first-person testimony from a family member, and it is
the bundle's primary basis for treating Andy Layden and Matt Layden as brothers.

## Matt's boats, as documented here

Andy's **"Matt's Boats"** index (`matts_boats.htm`, last updated 22 Mar 2007)
logs three of Matt's craft and cross-links to the wider Paradox world:[4]

- **2005 — "Enigma"** — logged on `matts_boat2005.htm` as "Matt's New Boat --
  2005," described when first captured (23 Feb 2005) as his "newest creation
  (as yet unnamed)," with a build log running **Feb 12 → Feb 22 → Mar 17 → Apr
  26, 2005** (design and layup, butt joints and twisting the hull to shape,
  wiring the hull together, decking and fiberglassing). The 2007 index names the
  boat **Enigma**.[4][5] This is the same page
  [microcruising.com](/references/microcruising-website.md) links as its
  external construction-photo source for [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md).
- **2007 — "Sand Flea"** — a photo gallery of [Sand Flea](/matt-layden/sand-flea.md)
  "at the 2007 WaterTribe Everglades Challenge" (`SandFlea2007.htm`, last
  updated 19 Mar 2007).[6]
- **2005 — "Rob Roy-oid" sailing canoe** — Matt's 2005 Everglades Challenge
  entry (`rob-royoid2005.htm`), carrying a note **in Matt's own first person**,
  signed "Matt": the canoe "is all back together after adding new rudder and
  recutting sail... that little 10 sq. ft. sail makes her fly!", headed to the
  Peace River and then the EC start at St Petersburg on March 5, with an aside
  that Karen was headed "home & back to work, boo."[7] See
  [biography — racing beyond Paradox](/matt-layden/biography.md#racing-beyond-paradox)
  for the 14 ft Rob Roy-type canoe in the wider race record.

The index also cross-links out to the **Bolducs' "Anthology of Matt's boats,"**
*Paradoxes around the World* / *Building Paradox* (smallsailboats.co.uk), a
Jordan Boats **Paradox kit**, and "more Paradox from our friend **Nick
Schade**" — placing Andy inside the same small circle of Paradox documenters as
the rest of this bundle's sources.[4]

## Layden family genealogy — Irish descent not established

Andy's family page links out, under the deliberately hedged label **"Possible
geneological connections,"** to `leyden.com/genealogy`, and the family-crest
image on the page is named `leyden.gif` — the **e-y** spelling, though the
brothers spell the surname **Layden** (a-y).[3] That target site is **not**
mirrored here, but was read directly on 2026-07-05: it is a **different family's**
research (the Leyden/Wynne family of Co. Clare, Ireland, compiled by one Andrew
_Leyden_), it lists the Irish-origin variants as *Leyden / Lydon / Liddane /
Ludden* — **"Layden" is not among them** — and the Northwest-Ohio Laydens
appear nowhere on it. Irish descent for the Layden brothers is therefore
**a family conjecture Andy himself flagged as merely "possible," with no
documented link** in the source he pointed to — recorded here as an unresolved
open thread, not as fact.

## What the construction photographs show

The `Matt_Boat/images/` folders hold roughly 40 build photographs of the 2005
boat (later "Enigma"), plus CAD screen grabs and finished-boat and cruising
shots. Beyond documenting a build, they corroborate several claims made
elsewhere in this bundle from text alone:

- **The design software is confirmed on screen.** Two screen grabs show Matt's
  boat modelled in a CAD program whose on-screen "Osnap" toolbar and command
  line identify it unmistakably as **Rhino 3D** — the same identification the
  archive reached by eye (see [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md#the-mini-paradox-working-name-and-the-2005-construction-era-discussion)).
  One is a perspective wireframe of the hull under sail; the other is a
  **developed-panel layout nested on plywood sheets**, confirming the boat was
  drawn for **stitch-and-glue developable-plywood** construction.[8]
- **The perspective CAD render depicts a two-masted lug rig and two seated
  crew** — whereas every photograph of the finished boat, on land and sailing,
  shows a **single** balanced-lug sail. The two-masted rendering therefore reads
  as an early concept study that was not carried through to the built boat, not
  the rig Enigma actually carried.[8]
- **Chine-runner construction is documented photographically** — a rare thing.
  Close-ups show a **tapered external wooden [chine runner](/chine-runners/how-they-work.md)
  rough-shaped and fitted along the hull's chine line**, dry-fit before gluing:
  a physical record of how the runners are built as separate shaped strips added
  to the outside of the hull.[9]
- **The stitch-and-glue sequence is shown step by step** — developed plywood
  panels, butt-jointed and wire-stitched into a hard-chine sharpie hull, then
  **framed and decked with epoxy and many clamps**, and divided into
  compartments by bulkheads carrying **round screw-in access ports**. A separate
  photo shows the **rudder** as a large kick-up blade and cheeks shaped from
  solid wood.[10]
- **The finished boat corroborates Enigma's description.** A 1 March 2006
  "ready to roll" photo shows the completed white sharpie on a launching dolly
  with a **fold-down soft cabin** and a sculling oar stowed along the side deck —
  matching the bundle's textual description of [Enigma](/matt-layden/enigma.md)
  as a light, cartoppable boat with a fold-down soft cabin. A May 2005 photo
  shows the same boat **sailing two-up at Cedar Key** under a single large lug.[11]
- **The "Rob Roy-oid" canoe is shown, and it is grey.** A 2005 photo of the
  decked sailing canoe with its small (~10 sq ft) lug matches Doug Cameron's
  2009 description of Matt's "**battleship-grey** 13½-foot 'Rob Royoid' canoe...
  decked like a sea kayak" (see [biography](/matt-layden/biography.md#the-man-himself)) —
  a visual confirmation from four years earlier.[12]

All of the boats are built and photographed in a **suburban Florida backyard**
(palms, bougainvillea, chain-link fence, white clapboard house) — the settled,
at-home building context of Matt's later boats, in contrast to the remote
woods-and-bicycle-trailer build of the first [Paradox](/boats/original-paradox.md).

## Citing this source

Website pages are cited in the form:

> Andrew Layden's BGSU website — "Page title"
> (`http://physics.bgsu.edu/~layden/PATH`), Andrew C. Layden — [detail].

A local mirror of the homepage and the full `FunStuff/` branch is kept under
`source-web/andrew-layden/` (166 files) so citations remain verifiable even
though the live BGSU page may change or disappear. Matt-boat pages preserve the
site's own layout under `FunStuff/Boats/Matt_Boat/`, including the per-boat
`images/` and `SandFlea2007/` photo folders.
