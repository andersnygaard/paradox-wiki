# Dave Skaife's blog (daveskaife.wordpress.com)

**Dave Skaife's blog** (`https://daveskaife.wordpress.com/`) is the WordPress cruising log of [Dave Skaife](/people/dave-skaife.md), who built a [Paradox](/paradox.md) in Hawaii and then sailed it, motorless, across four seasons — Hawaii in 2014, the Gulf of Mexico's [Intracoastal Waterway](/merits/inland-waterway-and-canal-touring.md) in 2015, the Bahamas in 2016 and again in 2017, and Cuba in 2017 — before selling the boat in Florida in 2018. It is an **authored, first-person primary source**, and after [Bill's Log](/references/bills-log.md) it is the single longest continuous first-person record of one Paradox in the archive: a day-by-day account of what building and cruising the design was actually like, over more posts and a wider cruising ground than any other owner has left.

It is distinct from [Dave's Wiki (Dave Raftery)](/references/daves-wiki.md), a technical reference site by a different Dave; this is a personal build-and-cruise blog.

## What the blog is worth as evidence

Its value is threefold. It is **contemporaneous** — build and passage entries were written as the work and the sailing happened, so its mistakes, repairs, and reversals survive as first impressions rather than tidied recollection. It is **continuous and wide-ranging** — one boat is followed from a mid-build condensation-management detail through open-water passages in Hawaii, the Gulf, two Bahamas seasons, and Cuba, a cruising range no other Paradox account in the archive covers. And it is **motorless throughout**: Dave sculled and sailed the boat, so the log is a sustained real-world test of engine-free Paradox handling — through locks, calms, and Gulf-crossing passages — of the kind the design was conceived for.

Its limits should be read with that. It is one owner's account, written for a general cruising readership rather than for builders, and its design judgments are stated as personal preference. Where the blog runs to boat-comfort theory it is opinion clearly labelled as such — most explicitly in the **"comfortable microcruising"** essays (see below), which are Dave's own outfitting philosophy, not documentation of Matt Layden's plans.

## Provenance and dating

The blog was live and public at retrieval. Its span is **2013-12-23 → 2018-03-25**; the build starts the record and it ends with the boat up for sale in Pensacola while Dave had moved on to overland travel in Southeast Asia.

Retrieved and mirrored for this bundle on **2026-07-20** under `source-web/daveskaife/`. Capture was via the WordPress.com public REST API rather than page scraping, so the whole post corpus came down in a handful of paged requests; images were pulled at full size. All **189** posts on the blog were indexed and screened, and **160** were kept as one continuous Paradox story, with **383 images**. The **29 skipped** posts are a 2016 motorbike trip through Vietnam and Laos taken while the boat sat idle, plus four posts of unrelated local small craft — boat content, but nothing to do with Paradox; every post, kept and skipped, is listed with its reason in `MANIFEST.tsv`, so nothing was dropped silently.[1]

The cut was made by subject, not by keyword score: 52 of the kept posts are untagged day-by-day Gulf-trip entries that a keyword-only filter would have discarded. `md/` frontmatter carries a `relevance_score` and `signals` list in the same style as the [Bill's Log](/references/bills-log.md) mirror, and slugs are prefixed `YYYY-MM-` so the folder sorts chronologically.

## The cruising arc

The blog splits into a build and five cruising seasons, tagged by WordPress category:

- **The build (2013–14).** A from-scratch Paradox in Hawaii, blogged from a mid-build window-condensation detail through launch — including his electrical system, fender and anchor storage, dual-purpose running lights, a hatch lock, and multipurpose water-storage buckets.
- **Hawaii (2014).** The first season sailing among the islands — a roughly 415 nm inter-island cruise from Hilo up the chain to Kauai — including reef-damage repair and the tiller-pilot he rated his most valuable piece of gear; recorded as a voyage at [merits/skaife-hawaii-cruise.md](/merits/skaife-hawaii-cruise.md).
- **The Gulf ICW (2015).** The boat shipped from Hawaii to Texas and sailed on across the Gulf of Mexico's Intracoastal Waterway from Texas to Florida — a long run of daily entries through the Louisiana and Mississippi marshes, the Chandeleur and Ship Islands, and Florida's Big Bend, with a Gulf-trip damage assessment at the end recording what a long passage actually broke.
- **The Bahamas, twice (2016, 2017).** A Pensacola shakedown and Gulf Stream crossing to Bimini and the Exumas in 2016, then George Town, the Jumentos, and Ragged Island in 2017 — again with a damage-assessment post after the first crossing.
- **Cuba, and the end (2017–18).** A Cuban season (check-in, Puerto de Vita, Cayo Guillermo, money and internet on the island) reached by a 17-hour passage and closed by an engine-free Gulf Stream return — recorded as a voyage at [merits/skaife-cuba-voyage.md](/merits/skaife-cuba-voyage.md) — then a return through hurricane season and the Everglades, and the boat sold in Pensacola in March 2018 as Dave moved on to overland travel.

## The "comfortable microcruising" essays

A twelve-post **`comfortable-microcruising`** series is the densest original design thinking on the blog: a deliberate treatment of how to make a fourteen-foot Paradox genuinely liveable — ventilation, the padded "cell" interior, dressing for the conditions, window covers, an "ultimate personal environment management system" (UPEMS), and the balance of sentiment against minimalism. Alongside a `gear-i-like` review run (Bebi anchor light, JetBoil, Streamlight spotlight), these are the posts most useful to topic pages, and they are cited as Dave's own outfitting opinion rather than as design fact.[2]

## Why he built it

Dave's own account of choosing the design (2014) is a compact record of the plans' reach: moving down from a 36 ft monohull he almost always sailed solo, he wanted something small, simple, and trailerable, found a [Don Elliott](/people/don-elliott.md) sketch, followed it to [microcruising.com](/references/microcruising-website.md), and ordered the plans. He judged them "jam packed with details," warned that the design takes far longer to build than a normal fourteen-footer because of everything built into it, and characterised [Matt Layden](/matt-layden/biography.md) as thinking so far outside the box "that you forget there was a box."[3]

## The boat

Across all 160 posts the boat is referred to only as "my Paradox" / "my microcruiser"; **no name surfaces in this corpus, and none should be invented for it.** It was fourteen feet and **motorless**, registered in **Hawaii** with a hull identification number (Dave registered it there because proving ownership for the paperwork trail was simplest, and most states want residency), and it ended up in **Pensacola, Florida**. The single fullest description of the boat as finally equipped — a full inventory after two Bahamas seasons — is the 2017 for-sale post.[4] No boat page has been created for it (it is unnamed); the fuller cruising account lives at [people/dave-skaife.md](/people/dave-skaife.md).
