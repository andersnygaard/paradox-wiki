# References

External sources and resources referenced by the archive (designer materials,
websites, plans, articles) mirrored as first-class concepts for citation.
Concepts are added here as they are sourced from the archive.

# Concepts

* [Paradox builders mailing list](mailing-list.md) - The Paradox community's mailing list, running continuously from Yahoo Groups (founded 2001) to groups.io's paradoxsailboat (from 2019), and the source archive for this bundle.
* [microcruising.com (David & Mindy Bolduc's website)](microcruising-website.md) - An authored website tied to the authorized Paradox plans distributor and owners of the Layden-designed Little Cruiser, documenting Matt Layden's whole family of boats, Bahamas/Outer Banks cruising, and detailed build/refit, sailmaking, and outfitting how-tos.
* [Messing About In Boats (MAIB) magazine](messing-about-in-boats.md) - The American small-boat magazine, edited by Bob Hicks, that is Paradox's original print venue — where Matt Layden's boats were first documented (from 1991), where the design and its plans-ordering information debuted (1995), and where Don Elliott's sixteen-installment "Building Paradox" construction series ran (1996–1997), followed by decades of sea-trial reports, sightings, and reader builds.
* [Dave's Wiki (aiki.pbworks.com — Dave Raftery)](daves-wiki.md) - A PBworks wiki compiling technical detail on Matt Layden's Paradox and Enigma micro-cruisers, including a Paradox/Enigma-360/Enigma-460 comparison, construction notes, and quotes from Matt Layden and others.
* [yrvind.com (Sven Yrvind's website)](yrvind-website.md) - The personal website and blog of Swedish designer-voyager Sven Yrvind, an authored first-person primary source on the chine runner's mechanism, dating, and history, on Matt Layden's boats and their dimensions, and on extreme-small-boat voyaging and design philosophy.
* [Andrew Layden's BGSU website (physics.bgsu.edu/~layden)](andrew-layden-website.md) - The personal academic homepage of an astronomer at Bowling Green State University — Matt Layden's brother — whose "Fun Stuff" branch hosts first-person build logs and photographs of Matt's boats (the 2005 Enigma, the 2007 Sand Flea, and a 2005 "Rob Roy-oid" sailing canoe) plus a Layden family and genealogy page.
* ["Building the Paradox" articles (Don Elliott)](building-the-paradox-articles.md) - Don Elliott's paid "Building Paradox" construction articles — a sixteen-installment series originally published in Messing About In Boats magazine (1996–97) and later sold by Don directly as a ~50-page compilation; this page covers the articles as an obtainable product (their construction content is captured at building-paradox-series.md).
* [The "Building Paradox" MAIB series — construction content](building-paradox-series.md) - The actual part-by-part construction content of Don Elliott's sixteen-installment "Building Paradox" series in Messing About In Boats (September 1996 – May 1997) — the published design particulars, the whole build sequence from bulkheads to rig, the WEST SYSTEM engineer's public critique of its epoxy claims, and the reader Q&A in which Dave Bolduc validates the chine runners.
* ["Beach Cruising and Coastal Camping" (Ida Little & Michael Walsh)](beachcruising-book.md) - A small-boat cruising book by Ida Little and Michael Walsh that discusses Matt Layden's micro-cruisers and includes a photograph of Swamp Thing (Matt aboard in the Abacos, p. 197).
* [Paradox Study Plans](study-plans.md) - Introductory study plans for the Paradox, distributed via the mailing list's files area.
* [Paradox building plans (source and cost)](building-plans.md) - Where to obtain the full Paradox building plans and what they cost, why the design resists simple scaling up, and answers to the interpretation questions that recur once a builder has the plans in hand.
* [Duckworks Magazine](duckworks-magazine.md) - An online small-boat / home-boatbuilding magazine that reprinted Paradox builders' tips.
* [Micro Cruising (Yahoo group)](micro-cruising-group.md) - A sibling Yahoo group for broader micro-cruising discussion beyond the Paradox design, founded around September 2001.
* ["Wind Vane Self-Steering" (book)](/operations/self-steering.md#a-purpose-built-option-wind-vane-self-steering) - A book containing simple, cheap plywood wind-vane plans suited to the Paradox — out of print as of December 2001, but purchasable through armchairsailor.com for $36.95; folded into [self-steering.md](/operations/self-steering.md#a-purpose-built-option-wind-vane-self-steering).
* [Dave Carnell's boat-building articles](dave-carnell-articles.md) - An external boat-building website (home.att.net/~DaveCarnell) with articles on paint choice and treating wood rot/fungus for small plywood boats.
* [Materials sourcing links (epoxy, fiberglass, general boatbuilding, West System)](materials-sourcing-links.md) - Consolidated external materials-sourcing and reference links for Paradox builders — an epoxy/fiberglass supplier and the Kevlar-vs-fiberglass cost debate, general (non-Paradox-specific) boatbuilding community resources ([general boatbuilding resource links](materials-sourcing-links.md#general-boatbuilding-resource-links-cedar-strip-site-newsgroup-forum)), and West System Epoxy's moisture-barrier and wood/epoxy-sharpie reference material ([West System Epoxy resources](materials-sourcing-links.md#west-system-epoxy-resources-moisture-barriers-and-a-sharpie-building-article)).
* [Bill's Log (bills-log.blogspot.com — Bill Serjeant)](bills-log.md) - A daily-kept personal blog running 2005–2015, carrying a complete first-person Paradox build log for "Faith," her 2007–08 cruises, a full second-hand purchase-refit-resale chapter for "Minnow" ex-"Enuf," and a decade of appraisals, cost breakdowns, and surveys of other builders' boats.
* [Builders and Owners of Paradox Sailboats (2014 census)](builders-and-owners-census-2014.md) - A 29-boat worldwide table of Paradox builders and owners, first compiled in 2006 and republished with updates in October 2014 — the archive's most complete self-reported Paradox census, and the direct successor to the earlier UK/worldwide registry.
* [Small Sailboats / Paradox-UK websites (Bill Serjeant)](paradox-uk-website.md) - A UK-focused website pairing — smallsailboats.co.uk and its dedicated Paradox-UK offshoot — documenting Paradox building and sailing from a British builder's perspective.
* [AYRS meeting talk — Alastair on "Little Jim" (May 2020)](ayrs-meeting-video.md) - A recorded May 2020 online talk to the Amateur Yacht Research Society giving a full firsthand walk-through of the UK Paradox "Little Jim" — its design, construction and stability, and seventeen years of cruising it — a late, mature primary source corroborating and extending the 2001–2004 archive accounts.
* ["Sailing The Paradox" — DCA Bulletin 193 (Alastair Law)](dca-sailing-the-paradox.md) - A signed two-page owner's appraisal of the Paradox published in the Dinghy Cruising Association Bulletin, setting out fifteen advantages and eleven disadvantages after three seasons sailing "Little Jim" — the archive's most compact published statement of the design's practical trade-offs, and one of three independent print attestations of the builder's surname.
* ["Paradox" — DCA Bulletin 193 (Duncan Gilchrist)](dca-paradox-gilchrist.md) - A three-page evaluation in the Dinghy Cruising Association Bulletin by a declared sceptic who sailed the UK Paradox "Little Jim" at Chew Valley Lake in October 2006 — the archive's only independent, non-owner sail trial of the design in print, and the source that dates Bulletin 193 to after October 2006.
* [Sven Yrvind video — Matt Layden's chine runners (July 2025)](yrvind-chine-runner-video.md) - A July 2025 talk-to-camera video recounting a first encounter with Paradox in 1997, a visit to Matt Layden in Florida, how the chine runner works, and a 2011 chine-runner Atlantic crossing — a late retrospective first-person primary source corroborating and enriching the website account of the Yrvind–Layden friendship.
* [The small-boat library behind the Paradox](small-boat-library.md) - A working bibliography of the books, magazines, and manufacturer literature drawn on across Don Elliott's sixteen-part "Building Paradox" series, gathered by what each was used for.
* [Amateur Boat Builders' Association (ABBA) Newsletter (Western Australia)](abba-newsletter.md) - The newsletter of a Perth home-boatbuilding club, whose April/May 2021 issue carries an illustrated build-and-cruise report on the junk-rigged Paradox "Planktos" — the archive's only account of a completed, sailed junk-rig conversion, and the source of its most legible reproduction of Matt Layden's 1994 sail-plan drawing.
