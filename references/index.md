# References

External sources and resources referenced by the archive (designer materials,
websites, plans, articles) mirrored as first-class concepts for citation.
Concepts are added here as they are sourced from the archive.

# Concepts

* [Paradox builders mailing list](mailing-list.md) - The Paradox community's mailing list, running continuously from Yahoo Groups (founded 2001) to groups.io's paradoxsailboat (from 2019), and the source archive for this bundle.
* [microcruising.com (David & Mindy Bolduc's website)](microcruising-website.md) - The authored website of David ("Dave") and Mindy Bolduc, the authorized Paradox plans seller and owners of the Layden-designed Little Cruiser, documenting Matt Layden's whole family of boats, the Bolducs' Bahamas/Outer Banks cruising, and detailed build/refit, sailmaking, and outfitting how-tos.
* [Messing About In Boats (MAIB) magazine](messing-about-in-boats.md) - The small-boat magazine, edited by Bob Hicks, that is Paradox's original print venue — where Matt Layden's boats were first documented (from 1991), where the design and its plans debuted (1995), where Don Elliott's sixteen-part "Building Paradox" series ran (1996–97), and which carried decades of sea-trial reports, Cedar Key sightings, and reader builds.
* [Dave's Wiki (aiki.pbworks.com — Dave Raftery)](daves-wiki.md) - A PBworks wiki by prospective Enigma builder Dave Raftery compiling technical detail on Matt Layden's Paradox and Enigma micro-cruisers, including a Paradox/Enigma-360/Enigma-460 comparison, construction notes, and quotes from Matt Layden, Sven Yrvind, Don Elliott, George Van Sickle, and the Bolducs.
* [yrvind.com (Sven Yrvind's website)](yrvind-website.md) - The personal website and blog of Swedish designer-voyager Sven Yrvind, an authored first-person primary source on the chine runner's mechanism, dating, and history, on Matt Layden's boats and their dimensions, and on Yrvind's own extreme-small-boat voyaging and design philosophy.
* [Andrew Layden's BGSU website (physics.bgsu.edu/~layden)](andrew-layden-website.md) - The personal academic homepage of astronomer Andrew "Andy" C. Layden — Matt Layden's brother — whose "Fun Stuff" branch hosts Andy's own build logs and photographs of Matt's boats (the 2005 Enigma, the 2007 Sand Flea, and a 2005 "Rob Roy-oid" canoe) plus a Layden family and genealogy page; the site the archive calls "Matt's brother's website."
* ["Building the Paradox" articles (Don Elliott)](building-the-paradox-articles.md) - Don Elliott's paid "Building Paradox" construction articles — a sixteen-installment series originally published in Messing About In Boats magazine (1996–97) and later sold by Don directly as a ~50-page compilation; this page covers the articles as an obtainable product.
* [The "Building Paradox" MAIB series — construction content](building-paradox-series.md) - The actual part-by-part construction content of Don Elliott's sixteen-installment series: the published design particulars, the whole build sequence from bulkheads to rig and hatch, the WEST SYSTEM engineer's public critique of its epoxy claims, and the reader Q&A in which Dave Bolduc validates the chine runners.
* ["Beach Cruising and Coastal Camping" (Ida Little & Michael Walsh)](beachcruising-book.md) - A small-boat cruising book by Ida Little and Michael Walsh that discusses Matt Layden's micro-cruisers and includes a photograph of Swamp Thing (Matt aboard in the Abacos, p. 197).
* [Paradox Study Plans](study-plans.md) - Introductory study plans for the Paradox, distributed via the mailing list's files area.
* [Paradox building plans (source and cost)](building-plans.md) - Where to obtain the full Paradox building plans and what they cost, and why the design resists simple scaling up.
* [Duckworks Magazine](duckworks-magazine.md) - An online small-boat / home-boatbuilding magazine that reprinted Paradox builders' tips.
* [Micro Cruising (Yahoo group)](micro-cruising-group.md) - A sibling Yahoo group for broader micro-cruising discussion beyond the Paradox design, founded and moderated by Dave Bolduc.
* ["Wind Vane Self-Steering" (book)](/operations/self-steering.md#a-purpose-built-option-wind-vane-self-steering) - A book containing simple, cheap plywood wind-vane plans suited to the Paradox — out of print as of December 2001, but purchasable through armchairsailor.com for $36.95; folded into [self-steering.md](/operations/self-steering.md#a-purpose-built-option-wind-vane-self-steering).
* [Dave Carnell's boat-building articles](dave-carnell-articles.md) - An external boat-building website (home.att.net/~DaveCarnell) with articles on paint choice and treating wood rot/fungus, cited twice on the Paradox list for small-plywood-boat maintenance.
* [Materials sourcing links (epoxy, fiberglass, general boatbuilding, West System)](materials-sourcing-links.md) - Consolidated external materials-sourcing and reference links for Paradox builders — an epoxy/fiberglass supplier and the Kevlar-vs-fiberglass cost debate, general (non-Paradox-specific) boatbuilding community resources ([general boatbuilding resource links](materials-sourcing-links.md#general-boatbuilding-resource-links-cedar-strip-site-newsgroup-forum)), and West System Epoxy's moisture-barrier and wood/epoxy-sharpie reference material ([West System Epoxy resources](materials-sourcing-links.md#west-system-epoxy-resources-moisture-barriers-and-a-sharpie-building-article)).
* [Small Sailboats / Paradox-UK websites (Bill Serjeant)](paradox-uk-website.md) - A UK-focused website pairing — smallsailboats.co.uk and its dedicated Paradox-UK offshoot — documenting Paradox building and sailing from a British builder's perspective.
* [AYRS meeting talk — Alastair on "Little Jim" (May 2020)](ayrs-meeting-video.md) - A recorded May 2020 online talk to the Amateur Yacht Research Society in which Alastair, builder of the UK Paradox "Little Jim," gives a full firsthand walk-through of the design, its stability, and seventeen years of cruising it — a late retrospective primary source corroborating and extending his 2001–2004 archive accounts.
* ["Sailing The Paradox" — DCA Bulletin 193 (Alastair Law)](dca-sailing-the-paradox.md) - A signed two-page owner's appraisal in the Dinghy Cruising Association *Bulletin*, setting out fifteen advantages and eleven disadvantages after three seasons sailing "Little Jim" — the only place the design's drawbacks are itemized as a deliberate list by someone who built and cruised one, and one of three independent print attestations of the builder's surname.
* [Sven Yrvind video — Matt Layden's chine runners (July 2025)](yrvind-chine-runner-video.md) - A July 2025 talk-to-camera on Sven Yrvind's YouTube channel recounting how he first came to Paradox in 1997, his visit to Matt Layden in Florida, how the chine runner works, and his own 2011 chine-runner Atlantic crossing — a late retrospective first-person primary source from the chine runner's most authoritative outside champion.
* [The small-boat library behind the Paradox](small-boat-library.md) - A working bibliography of the books, magazines, and manufacturer literature drawn on across Don Elliott's sixteen-part "Building Paradox" series, gathered by what each was used for.
