# Construction

Materials, techniques, tools, and build sequence used to build a
[Paradox](/paradox.md) — plywood, epoxy, fitting-out, and lessons learned by
builders. Concepts are added here as they are sourced from the archive.

# Concepts

* [Build effort and workspace](build-effort.md) - Build time, cost, difficulty, and workspace requirements for a Paradox and related Layden designs, drawn from builders' own accounts, including recovering from a total loss and staying motivated through a long build.
* [Hull planking ("getting the bottom on")](hull-planking.md) - How builders bend and fasten the plywood bottom and side panels onto the Paradox hull, and the tradeoffs of stitch-and-glue as an alternative to the plans' single-sheet method.
* [Fastening and gluing](fastening-and-gluing.md) - How the Paradox's plywood joints are fastened and glued — the plans' permanent bronze ring nails versus a builder's removable-screw alternative, epoxy squeeze-out control, the dry-fit-first sequence for sheer clamps and chine logs, and why a small hidden void in a secondary lamination poses no real rot or structural risk.
* [Bottom painting over epoxy](bottom-painting.md) - How to bond antifouling paint durably to an epoxy-coated hull, how to prepare an existing bottom for new roving, how to choose an antifouling product, and how builders locate the waterline on the transom and safely strip old bottom paint.
* [Non-skid deck finish](non-skid-deck.md) - Glen Maxwell's walnut-shell-in-epoxy non-skid deck technique for the Paradox — attractive, foot-friendly, and grippier when wet.
* [Panel layout and nesting](panel-layout.md) - How Paradox builders lay out and nest the panels, bulkheads, and transom on the plywood sheets, including the fits that run tight, ways to resolve overlaps, a bulkhead-side curve detail, a metric-vs-imperial measuring note, and a side-deck/cabin-end-panel plans issue.
* [Hull assembly sequence](hull-assembly-sequence.md) - The Paradox hull's stem-first assembly method — stem, then tank-top/floor doors, then heat-and-wet-forming the side panels onto the stem with repeated tack-fit-and-bevel passes — alongside a builder variant of gluing oversize and cutting all bevels afterward.
* [Plywood grade and foam flotation debate](plywood-grades-and-foam-flotation.md) - Guidance on plywood grade selection for a Paradox — which grades and species safely support bonded interior foam flotation and resist checking, and which are reserved for disposable or budget builds.
* [Mast interior sealing and anchor-light fit](mast-sealing-and-fit.md) - Practical mast-finishing lessons for the Paradox — sealing the hollow mast's interior, keeping anchor-light height and stowage clearances in mind, protecting the mast where it passes through the deck partners, and diagnosing mast-heel play that costs windward performance.
* [Removable foam flotation panels (door-skin technique)](removable-foam-panels.md) - A method for installing foam flotation in a Paradox behind removable, paintable door-skin plywood panels rather than gluing it directly to the hull, so the hull can still be inspected for moisture.
* [Topside and hull painting choices](topside-painting.md) - Economical topside/hull finish-paint options for a Paradox — house paint versus marine finishes, the roll-and-tip application technique, primer choice, and the Bolducs' practice-tested enamel method.
* [Epoxy pre-coating technique](epoxy-coating-technique.md) - Glen Maxwell's technique for pre-coating Paradox plywood panels with epoxy before assembly — squeegee application, a screw-mounted paint/glue board for small pieces, foam-roller tipping, and amine-blush sanding prep.
* [Transom deck plate and vent baffle](transom-deck-plate.md) - Watertightness of the Paradox's transom-mounted access plates and vent baffle, and the sealing techniques builders use to keep them leak-free.
* [Deck and cabin beam lamination](deck-and-cabin-beams.md) - How the Paradox's curved deck and cabin beams are laminated from thin timber or plywood on a shaping mold, including the deck curve's true parabolic (non-radius) shape, standard beam dimensions and chord lengths, and the adhesives (epoxy or polyurethane) used to glue them up.
* [Water tank construction and lining options](water-tank-lining.md) - Options for building and lining the Paradox's built-in water tanks — epoxy suitability and its risks, tarred wood, Vulkem urethane coatings, and flexible-bladder or insertable-tank alternatives.
* [Boom-end tack line holes and drilled-hole rot protection](boom-end-tack-line-holes.md) - Explains the two-hole tack-line arrangement in the Paradox boom-end pulley, the "bottom bevel left standing" bulkhead-drawing convention, and Glen Maxwell's and Don Peters's techniques for coating narrow drilled holes with epoxy to prevent rot without closing them up.
* [Checking a used Paradox for rot (ice-pick probe technique)](rot-inspection-ice-pick.md) - How to survey a secondhand or already-built Paradox for hidden rot before investing in a refit, and how to treat rot once found.
* [Lower rudder fitting — bearing material options](rudder-bearing-materials.md) - Bearing-material choice for a Paradox's lower rudder (gudgeon) fitting — why UHMW beats nylon, Delrin, and Tufnol — plus sourced, priced bronze suppliers for the tack, sail, and yuloh fittings.
* [Help! — undersize (6 mm) side panels on a secondhand Paradox](undersize-side-panel-ply.md) - When a secondhand Paradox's side panels measure thinner than the specified 12 mm ply, the main consequence is reduced stiffness offset by carrying extra ballast, not a structural weakness that ends the boat's useful life.
* [Epoxy brands, chemistry, and cure behavior](epoxy-brands-and-chemistry.md) - Compares epoxy brands used on Paradox and other Layden-design boats (West System, RAKA, FASCO, System Three, generic), the underlying epoxy chemistry, and brand-specific handling and cure behavior including RAKA's temperature sensitivity and West System hardener discoloration with age.
* [Scarf joints vs. butt blocks, and scarf orientation](scarf-joints.md) - Joinery guidance for lengthening Paradox plywood panels — why a true scarf beats a butt block for fairness, which way a scarf should face for smooth water flow, and a note on "scarf" vs. "scarph" spelling.
* [Notching the stem for chine logs and sheer clamps](stem-notching.md) - At the stem, the Paradox plans inlet (notch) the chine logs and sheer clamps at an angle rather than butting them as at the transom, an imperfect cut is forgiven by thickened epoxy, and the notch should be only partly cut until the side panels are fitted, with lamination recommended over bending the chine log solid.
* [Chine log vs. taped-seam construction](chine-log-vs-taped-seam.md) - Compares the Paradox plans' chine-log building method against taped-seam plywood construction, and why chine log is the better choice for this hull specifically.
* [Sailmaking (home-built lug sail)](sailmaking.md) - How to loft, cut, and sew a home-built Paradox-family lug sail — lofting the old sail off a concrete slab, broadseaming for draft, sewing on a gravity-fed hand-crank Singer, and Matt Layden's own dimensioned sailmaking recipe for a more weatherly cut.
* [Folding dinghy (the micro-cruiser's tender)](folding-dinghy.md) - A home-built canvas-hinged folding plywood dinghy, based on one of Matt Layden's early folding-dinghy designs, sized for use as a micro-cruiser's stow-flat tender.
* [Hatch hardware and security](hatch-hardware.md) - How to fit a locking hasp on the Paradox's main hatch without risking locking the sailor inside, and a simpler padlock-through-the-rail alternative.
