# Aluminium Paradox (Alex McGavin's build)

**Alex McGavin** is building a [Paradox](/paradox.md) hull in **welded aluminium** rather than the specified plywood — the **first metal Paradox build documented anywhere on record**, turning the long-running, until-then purely theoretical [aluminium-and-steel construction debate](/design/ideas/aluminum-construction-idea.md) into an actual boat. A **companion hull**, built by his friend **Scotty**, was brought to the same stage shortly afterward, so two aluminium hulls exist rather than one.

## Materials and method

The hull is welded from **3 mm 5005-grade aluminium plate**, **MIG-welded** with a **250-amp Unimig welder** fed through a **spool gun** (the usual way to feed soft aluminium wire without it birdnesting in a normal torch liner). 5005 is a **non-heat-treatable 5000-series aluminium–magnesium alloy** — copper-free, and so in the same salt-water-tolerant family the earlier [speculative assessment](/design/ideas/aluminum-construction-idea.md#technical-assessment-alloy-thickness-and-weight) had recommended (which named 5083, 5086, and 5456), bearing out a 5000-series alloy as the practical choice once someone actually welded one up.

## Plate gauge: 3 mm versus 4 mm

The two hulls test two gauges directly. Alex McGavin's is **3 mm** plate; Scotty's companion hull is **4 mm**, and the thicker plate was found **easier to build** with — the extra stiffness leaves the panels less prone to distortion and easier to hold fair while welding. Both are thinner than the **~3/16 in (≈4.8 mm)** the theoretical debate had floated as a denting-resistant minimum, making these builds the first real-world data point on how thin an aluminium Paradox skin can practically go.

## Progress

By **January 2025**, Alex McGavin's hull had reached a bare welded-shell stage — the topsides up and the transverse bulkheads in, standing on trestles in a workshop amid the welding gear. Progress then slowed, with heavy work commitments leaving the build untouched for a stretch, before a free weekend in **March 2025** carried it a little further. Around the start of that month, Alex and Scotty together built Scotty's 4 mm hull up to the same stage, with Scotty expected to post his own build once he returned. The later photographs show the hull farther along: the raked bow, the flat rockered bottom meeting the topsides at the chine, and the internal frames, all in bare, unfinished aluminium.

## Significance

Until this build, the archive recorded **no metal Paradox — partial or complete — ever begun**, only a week-long 2003 mailing-list debate over whether one could or should be built (see [aluminium and steel Paradox](/design/ideas/aluminum-construction-idea.md)). Alex McGavin's hull, with Scotty's alongside it, is the first physical answer to that debate and the first confirmed departure from plywood into metal for the design — a materials-substitution variant in the same spirit as the composite [BRIS Paradox](/boats/bris-paradox.md), arrived at independently and in a different material.
